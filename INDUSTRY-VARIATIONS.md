## Case Study 1 Adaptation: AI Lead Intake Automation

### Original Context
**Client:** Meridian Legal Solutions | **Pain:** 47% after-hours leads unanswered, 4.2hr response time, 62% qualification accuracy | **Outcome:** 340% more qualified leads, $3.8M attributed revenue, 4.1-month payback

---

### SaaS Version

**Client Profile:** B2B SaaS company with trial/freemium funnel, $50K-$300K ACV, struggling with lead quality and time-to-response for inbound demos.

**Pain in SaaS terms:** Your trial activation rate is 12%. Your sales team is demoing users who will never convert. Your best AE spends 40% of their time on unqualified prospects. Meanwhile, your best-fit customers wait 3 hours for a response while you demo the wrong people.

**What to build:** AI-powered trial qualification and activation bot. Intercepts trial signups, runs a 5-question qualification sequence, scores and routes trial users based on fit, triggers automated onboarding sequences for high-fit users, and flags urgent expansion opportunities for account management. Integrates with your CRM, product analytics, and demo scheduling tool.

**Deliverable framing for SaaS:** *"Your trial funnel is a black hole. We're building the qualification layer that makes sure your best AEs spend their time with the users who'll actually convert — and gets the others to value faster or gracefully out."*

**Key metrics to quote:** Trial-to-paid conversion improvement, demo quality score, time-to-qualified-demo reduction.

---

### Local Services Version

**Client Profile:** Multi-location service business (dental, HVAC, home services, medical) with high-volume phone and web inquiries, front desk bottleneck, after-hours gap.

**Pain in local services terms:** Your phones ring all day. Your front desk is overwhelmed. After-hours calls go to voicemail — and those callers call your competitor at 8am. You're hiring receptionists faster than you can train them and still losing business.

**What to build:** 24/7 AI phone and web receptionist that handles booking, FAQs, and routing. For dental: verifies insurance and pre-qualifies for procedures. For HVAC: schedules service appointments and does initial troubleshooting to determine urgency. Integrates with your existing scheduling software (YAPI, Jane App, ServiceTitan, Jobber).

**Deliverable framing for local services:** *"Your competitor answers the phone at 8pm. You don't have to — you just need to answer it automatically. We'll build the AI front desk that books the appointment while your competitor's voicemail sits unplayed."*

**Key metrics to quote:** Calls handled after-hours, appointment booking rate, front desk hours saved, new patient/customer acquisition cost.

### E-Commerce Version

**Client Profile:** D2C brand with $1M-$20M ARR, high email/list volume, abandoned cart problem, post-purchase communication gaps.

**Pain in e-commerce terms:** Your email list has 50K subscribers. Open rate is 18%. Conversion is 1.2%. You're leaving money on the table with every send. And your abandoned cart sequence is losing 60% of would-be buyers to no-response.

**What to build:** AI-powered email intelligence and automation system that personalizes send time, content, and frequency per segment. Abandoned cart AI that responds conversationally (not just a discount offer). Post-purchase upsell automation that feels consultative, not promotional. Klaviyo integration with product recommendation engine.

**Deliverable framing for e-commerce:** *"Your email list is your most owned channel — and your most undermonetized. We'll turn it from a broadcast tool into a 24/7 selling machine that personalizes at scale."*

**Key metrics to quote:** Email-driven revenue per subscriber, abandoned cart recovery rate, email-attributed repeat purchase rate.

### Professional Services Version

**Client Profile:** Consulting firm, accounting firm, marketing agency, or professional services company with partner-level founders doing their own business development, $10K-$50K average engagement.

**Pain in professional services terms:** Partners are brilliant at the work. They're not great at following up on leads, keeping in touch with past clients, or qualifying inbound inquiries consistently. Proposals go out slow. Follow-ups fall through cracks. Referrals don't get activated.

**What to build:** AI business development assistant that manages inbound lead response, activates referral follow-ups, sends LinkedIn connection notes on behalf of partners, and drafts proposal outlines based on client intake calls. Integrates with Outlook/Gmail, LinkedIn, and CRM.

**Deliverable framing for professional services:** *"Partners should be closing, not chasing. We'll build the BD layer that ensures every lead gets a same-day response, every referral gets a personal touch, and every proposal goes out with the partner's voice — not their administrative assistant's typing."*

**Key metrics to quote:** Response time improvement, proposal close rate, referral activation rate, BD hours recovered per partner.

### Agency Version

**Client Profile:** Creative, digital, or marketing agency with client onboarding bottleneck, account management overwhelm, or scope creep from poor intake.

**Pain in agency terms:** Your best account managers are drowning in onboarding logistics, client communication, and scope management. They're not doing the strategic work clients are paying for. New business is great but onboarding is a nightmare that creates early-client churn.

**What to build:** AI client intake and onboarding automation — chatbots on your website that qualify and schedule discovery calls, onboarding workflows that auto-populate briefs, project kickoff sequences, and weekly status report templates. Integrates with your project management (ClickUp, Asana), CRM, and client portal.

**Deliverable framing for agencies:** *"Your agency's growth is blocked by your best people doing admin. We'll build the onboarding and account management layer that gets new revenue off their plates — so they can focus on the work that keeps clients happy and renewing."*

**Key metrics to quote:** Onboarding time reduction, client satisfaction at 30/60/90 days, AR on new accounts, retention improvement.

### B2B Ops-Heavy Version

**Client Profile:** Manufacturing, logistics, distribution, or industrial company with complex sales cycles, multi-stakeholder accounts, and long RFP processes.

**Pain in B2B ops-heavy terms:** Your sales cycle is 6-18 months. You have 12 people involved in any given deal. You're RFP-heavy and losing to competitors who are faster and more responsive. Your best salespeople are spending half their time on internal coordination, not selling.

**What to build:** AI sales coordination system that manages RFP responses, stakeholder communications, and internal handoffs across the extended sales cycle. Automated status tracking, next-best-action recommendations, and pre-meeting briefing automation. Integrates with Salesforce, Microsoft Dynamics, or your existing CRM.

**Deliverable framing for B2B ops-heavy:** *"In a 12-stakeholder sale, coordination is the product. We'll build the system that keeps every deal moving when your reps aren't in the room — so you stop losing to faster competitors in the gaps between your team's availability."*

**Key metrics to quote:** Sales cycle compression, RFP win rate, stakeholder coverage per account, internal handoff time reduction.

### Startup Version

**Client Profile:** Pre-seed to Series A startup with founder-led sales, limited bandwidth, needing to maximize every inbound opportunity.

**Pain in startup terms:** You're getting inbound interest but responding from your phone between investor meetings. You're losing deals because you responded from the wrong email at the wrong time with the wrong message. You can't hire a sales team yet but you can't afford to lose qualified signal.

**What to build:** Founder-first AI sales assistant — intercepts all inbound, qualifies with startup-specific framework (founder/market/ traction fit), responds with appropriate founder-level message, and books directly onto your calendar. Routes urgent signals (press, partnership, top-tier investor) to your phone. Handles outbound cold outreach sequencing when you have bandwidth.

**Deliverable framing for startups:** *"Every inbound at a startup is precious. You'll never have a sales team — but you can have a founder intelligence layer that makes sure the right signals get your attention and the noise doesn't. We'll build it in a week."*

**Key metrics to quote:** Response time (hours → minutes), founder time spent on sales admin, qualified signal capture rate.

### Mid-Market Company Version

**Client Profile:** $20M-$100M ARR company with established sales team, marketing department, and customer success function, but disconnected lead-to-cash process.

**Pain in mid-market terms:** Marketing generates leads. Sales complains they're unqualified. Customer success gets churned customers who were oversold. Finance can't reconcile ARR because CRM and billing don't match. You're leaving money on the table and creating friction at every handoff.

**What to build:** AI-powered revenue operations intelligence that sits across marketing, sales, and customer success. Qualifies marketing leads before distribution, flags misaligned deals before contracts are signed, triggers customer success alerts when usage patterns predict churn, and reconciles CRM data with billing. The AI layer that makes your revenue team a system, not a collection of silos.

**Deliverable framing for mid-market:** *"You've built marketing, sales, and success teams. They don't talk to each other. We'll build the intelligence layer across your revenue operation that makes every handoff invisible and every revenue leak visible."*

**Key metrics to quote:** Lead-to-close time, revenue leakage reduction, churn prediction accuracy, net revenue retention improvement.

## Case Study 2 Adaptation: Executive Dashboard & Reporting

### Original Context
**Client:** Meridian Capital Partners | **Pain:** 14hrs/week manual reporting, 7 disconnected systems, 3-day board package prep | **Outcome:** 96% reporting time reduction, $127K analyst time recovered, 4.2-month payback

---

### SaaS Version

**Client Profile:** Series B/C SaaS company with a CFO drowning in investor reporting, board materials that take days to prepare, and metrics that don't reconcile between tools.

**Pain in SaaS terms:** Your board wants MRR, ARR, NRR, CAC, LTV, burn rate, and runway — and they want them real-time. Your data team is rebuilding the same dashboards every month. Your finance team and product team disagree on which number is "correct." You're 18 months from IPO and your data infrastructure looks like a startup, not a public company candidate.

**What to build:** Unified metrics warehouse with Snowflake + Looker, standardized KPI definitions across product/finance/marketing, automated ARR and NRR calculations, board-ready dashboard with drill-down, and real-time cohort analysis for retention. dbt for transformation, Fivetran for ELT from Salesforce, Stripe, and your product analytics.

**Deliverable framing for SaaS:** *"Your board data shouldn't require a data team to produce. We'll build the metrics infrastructure that makes your CFO look brilliant every board meeting — and positions you for Series C or IPO with data discipline that investors expect."*

### Local Services Version

**Client Profile:** Multi-location service business (20-100+ locations) where each location reports differently, HQ has no visibility into operational performance until monthly calls.

**Pain in local services terms:** You can't tell on Monday which locations had a good week. You find out about operational problems from Yelp reviews, not from your own systems. Each franchise or location manager has their own spreadsheet. Consolidation takes two weeks and the numbers don't agree with each other.

**What to build:** Centralized operations analytics hub that pulls data from all locations (POS, scheduling, customer feedback), standardizes KPI definitions, and gives HQ real-time visibility across the network. Location scorecards, exception alerting (drops in 5-star reviews, spikes in wait times), and automated weekly performance digest.

**Deliverable framing for local services:** *"You can't manage what you can't see. We'll build the visibility layer that gives you real-time eyes on every location — so problems surface as alerts, not as Yelp reviews."*

## Case Study 3 Adaptation: Business Growth Strategy

### Original Context
**Client:** Apex Manufacturing | **Pain:** $12M flat for 4 years, 60% revenue concentration, no sales/marketing function | **Outcome:** $18.5M revenue (+54%), 23 new customers, $24M acquisition offer

---

### SaaS Version

**Client Profile:** SaaS company at $2M-$5M ARR that has product-market fit but has stalled — growth is flat, churn is creeping up, and the founders can't identify the next lever.

**Pain in SaaS terms:** You're past product-market fit but pre-product-led growth. You don't have a formal sales function. Your PLG motion is generating signups but not revenue. Your NPS is fine but your NRR is slipping. You need strategic clarity about where to focus next — land-and-expand, new segments, pricing change, or channel.

**What to build:** 12-week strategic diagnostic — product usage analysis to identify expansion opportunities, customer cohort analysis to understand which segments are driving NRR, pricing elasticity testing, go-to-market assessment for new segments, and a prioritized 18-month growth roadmap with clear owners and metrics. Not a presentation deck — a working plan your team can execute.

**Deliverable framing for SaaS:** *"Product-market fit isn't a destination — it's a recurring question. We'll help you find the next lever in your growth engine before your competitors find it for you."*

---

### Local Services Version

**Client Profile:** Local service business (plumbing, HVAC, dental, home services) that's been running on referrals for years and is hitting a ceiling — no online presence, no way to scale beyond what word-of-mouth generates.

**Pain in local services terms:** You're the best-kept secret in your zip code. Your competitors show up on Google Maps and you're invisible. You charge premium rates but can't prove it with reviews because customers don't know how to leave them. You can't hire fast enough because candidates can't find you.

**What to build:** Local market domination strategy — Google Business Profile optimization, review acceleration system, service-area expansion mapping, pricing tier restructuring based on competitive analysis, and customer acquisition cost optimization by zip code. Not a marketing campaign — a local market position strategy that compounds.

**Deliverable framing for local services:** *"The best service business in town shouldn't be invisible. We'll build the local presence that makes sure every new homeowner in your service area finds you first."*

---

### E-Commerce Version

**Client Profile:** D2C brand at $2M-$10M ARR that's grown through one channel (usually Amazon or one dominant platform) and is now exposed to platform risk, margin compression, or regulatory change.

**Pain in e-commerce terms:** 70% of your revenue comes from one channel. Amazon just changed their fee structure. Facebook is now charging twice what it did two years ago. You're growing revenue and watching margin disappear. You need to own your customer relationships, diversify channels, and build a brand that survives channel disruption.

**What to build:** Channel diversification and brand building strategy — DTC channel build-out with first-party data strategy, Amazon vs. DTC margin analysis, email/SMS owned channel acceleration, new channel testing framework (TikTok Shop, Walmart, wholesale), and subscription/loyalty program design. Creates a business that isn't held hostage by any single platform's algorithm changes.

**Deliverable framing for e-commerce:** *"If all your revenue came from one platform, it's not really your business — it's their platform. We'll build the channel mix that makes your revenue yours."*

---

### Professional Services Version

**Client Profile:** Established professional services firm (consulting, accounting, law) at $5M-$30M that's hitting a partnership ceiling — the managing partner can't figure out how to get to $50M without burning out the current team.

**Pain in professional services terms:** Your revenue is a direct function of partner headcount. Every dollar of growth requires a new partner. You're scaling people, not systems. You have no idea what your most profitable service lines are versus your loss leaders. Pricing is based on gut feel, not value delivered.

**What to build:** Firm scaling architecture — service line profitability analysis, alternative fee arrangement design (value-based pricing, subscription retainers, outcome-linked fees), rainmaker development program, delegation and leverage model (senior associates → junior partners), and equity vs. revenue share structuring for growth-stage partners.

**Deliverable framing for professional services:** *"The professional services ceiling isn't revenue — it's structure. We'll help you design the firm architecture that grows revenue without requiring you to clone yourself."*

---

### Agency Version

**Client Profile:** Creative, digital, or marketing agency at $2M-$10M that's hitting the founder ceiling — the principals are involved in every project, there's no way to grow without burning out, and margin is shrinking as the team grows.

**Pain in agency terms:** Your revenue is trapped in your people's time. Every new project requires more of you. You can't hire fast enough and the hires you make take 18 months to become profitable. Your margins are 25% and you want them to be 40%. The business needs to work without you in every room.

**What to build:** Agency unlock strategy — scope and service offering rationalization (what should you stop doing?), delivery model redesign (internal vs. freelance vs. partner), pricing restructuring (value-based, not hourly), new business system that generates inbound qualified leads, and team architecture for scale without founder dependency.

**Deliverable framing for agencies:** *"Agencies don't die from lack of work — they die from too much of the wrong work done inefficiently. We'll help you redesign your agency to scale without cloning the founder."*

### B2B Ops-Heavy Version

**Client Profile:** Manufacturing, industrial, or distribution company at $20M-$100M that's been growing through capacity expansion but is now facing margin pressure from input cost inflation, labor shortages, or competitive pricing pressure.

**Pain in B2B ops-heavy terms:** Your volume is up but your margin is down. You're investing in equipment but not in the systems that would make that equipment more productive. Your sales team is discounting to protect volume and destroying margin in the process. You need to grow revenue AND protect margin — which requires operational excellence, not just sales.

**What to build:** Margin recovery and growth acceleration program — operational efficiency assessment (OEE, yield, throughput), pricing strategy overhaul (should-cost vs. is-cost analysis, value-based pricing for key accounts), customer profitability segmentation, sales incentive restructuring, and lean/six-sigma process improvements for highest-impact operations bottlenecks.

**Deliverable framing for B2B ops-heavy:** *"Volume without margin is just busy. We'll help you find the margin you're leaving on the table in your operations, your pricing, and your sales incentives — and give you a plan to recover it."*

### Startup Version

**Client Profile:** Series A/B startup that has discovered its initial thesis was partially wrong — early traction exists but the original market positioning or product direction needs to shift.

**Pain in startup terms:** Your first product is getting some traction but not the breakout growth you promised investors. The market you aimed for has incumbents. The competitor landscape has shifted since you raised. You need to figure out whether to pivot, persevere, or find the wedge that makes your current direction work.

**What to build:** Startup strategic pivot assessment — customer discovery deep dive (who's actually buying and why), competitive repositioning analysis, product-market fit validation across adjacent segments, go/no-go framework for pivot vs. persevere, and a revised 18-month strategy with updated milestones and investor narrative.

**Deliverable framing for startups:** *"The best founders update their strategy, not their vision. We'll help you figure out whether your current direction needs a pivot, a push, or a patient doubling down — and build the strategy for whichever it is."*

### Mid-Market Company Version

**Client Profile:** $50M-$200M ARR company that's been acquired or is preparing for exit — PE firm is pushing for value maximization, management needs to understand what acquirers actually value, and operational gaps could destroy deal value.

**Pain in mid-market terms:** You have 24 months to get the business ready for a process. You don't know which metrics matter most to buyers. Your systems are a mess. Your customer concentration is a due diligence red flag. Your EBITDA is there but the normalization adjustments are a negotiation you'll lose without preparation.

**What to build:** Exit readiness program — buyer perspective analysis (what would a strategic vs. financial buyer value most), operational due diligence gap assessment, EBITDA normalization roadmap, KPI standardization across the business, and 18-month value creation plan that strengthens the story before you go to market.

**Deliverable framing for mid-market:** *"The difference between a good exit and a great exit isn't always the business — it's how prepared the business was when the process started. We'll help you close the gaps a sophisticated buyer will find, before they find them."*
