# LinkedIn Post

Just wrapped a project that transformed how an $85M e-commerce company thinks about marketing.

Streamline Commerce had a conversion rate stuck at 2.1% for 18 months. Their marketing team was flying blind — no unified funnel visibility, no idea which channels actually drove purchases, A/B tests that rarely showed significance.

The biggest discovery?

Their last-touch attribution was lying to them.

Facebook appeared to drive 40% of revenue. In reality, data-driven attribution showed it was 23%. The rest was upper-funnel influence from organic search and email that Facebook got credit for.

We built:
→ BigQuery data warehouse unifying GA4, Shopify, Klaviyo, Facebook Ads
→ 5 attribution models (last-touch, first-touch, linear, time-decay, data-driven)
→ Custom A/B testing platform with revenue-based metrics
→ 47-step funnel analysis

Results:
• Conversion: 2.1% → 3.4% (+62%)
• ROAS: 2.1x → 3.4x (+62%)
• A/B test success: 23% → 67% reaching significance
• $4.67M annual revenue impact
• 2.1 month payback

The lesson? Last-touch attribution isn't reporting — it's fiction.

What channels do you trust? 👇
