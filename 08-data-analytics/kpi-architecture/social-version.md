# LinkedIn Post

Just finished a KPI architecture project for a Series B healthcare tech company that was scaling from 50 to 180 people — and their "everyone knows the numbers" culture was breaking down.

Their situation:
→ Different teams used different metric definitions
→ Board packages took 3 days to compile manually
→ Sales claimed 120% of quota; finance showed 94% — same quarter
→ Product shipped features; nobody knew the business impact
→ Zero leading indicators — leadership saw results 2-4 weeks late
→ Series C in 18 months, needed investor-grade data

We built:
→ 12 company "True North" metrics with documented definitions
→ KPI trees for each team showing how work connects to company metrics
→ Automated board reporting (Looker → 12 slides)
→ 8 leading indicators validated for NRR, churn, and growth
→ Metric governance model with quarterly review process

Results:
• Board package: 3 days → 4 hours (94% reduction)
• Finance time freed: 15 hours/week
• Sales/Finance alignment: 26% variance → < 2%
• 8 leading indicators predicting outcomes 8 weeks ahead
• Series C: $45M raise with clean data room
• 4.2 month payback

The hardest part wasn't technical — it was alignment. Getting sales, finance, product, and CS to agree on what "active user" means took 3 weeks of workshops.

But once you have unified definitions, everything else follows. OKRs connect to metrics. Dashboards show truth. Decisions get made faster.

What's your company's true north? 👇

The problem: everyone "knew the numbers" when they were small. At scale, that breaks down.

Symptoms:
• Sales: "We hit 120% of quota"
• Finance: "You hit 94%"
• Same quarter, same company, 26% apart

We built:

1/ 12 "True North" metrics
   → ARR, NRR, GRR, CAC Payback, LTV:CAC, Magic Number, Burn Multiple, Rule of 40, Time to Value, PQLs, NPS
   → Every metric documented with definition, calculation, owner

2/ KPI Trees (team-level)
   → How each team's metrics roll up to company metrics
   → Every OKR connected to a metric

3/ Leading Indicators
   → 8 validated leading indicators for NRR, churn, growth
   → 8 weeks predictive horizon

4/ Board Package Automation
   → Looker → 12 slides, updated daily
   → 3 days → 4 hours

5/ Governance Model
   → Metric Review Board (quarterly)
   → Change management process

Results:
→ Board package: -94% time
→ Finance: 15 hrs/week freed
