# Objections & Responses: Ad Copy System
## NovaTech Case Study

---

## Objection 1: "We already have an agency running our ads."

**Response:**
"Great — I'd love to understand what's working and what's not. Most agencies are managing spend, not optimizing copy. If they haven't built you a modular system with proven templates, you're dependent on them writing everything from scratch. That's slow and expensive. What I do is build systems that your team can run independently once the foundation is in place. There's room for both in the short term — and my approach means you're not dependent on any single agency long-term."

---

## Objection 2: "Our audience is too niche for templates."

**Response:**
"Niche is your advantage, not your problem. The more specific your targeting, the more specific your copy can be — and the better it converts. 'Project management software' is broad and competitive. 'Stop missing sprint deadlines' is specific and underserved. Templates work because they're structured around psychological principles that apply universally. The fill-in-the-blank elements are what make them specific to your niche."

---

## Objection 3: "We tried Google Ads before and it didn't work."

**Response:**
"What didn't work — the channel, or the execution? Google Ads is highly efficient for B2B SaaS when done correctly. The difference between 1x and 4x ROAS is almost never the product or the market. It's relevance: matching the right ad to the right query to the right landing page. If you were running all queries to your homepage, that's a structural problem, not a channel problem."

---

## Objection 4: "Our sales cycle is long — 6+ months. Ads won't help."

**Response:**
"Long sales cycles mean high-value deals. That makes efficient acquisition MORE important, not less. At $30K+ ACV, a 4x ROAS on ads means you're paying $7,500 to acquire a customer who pays you $30K. Even at 12-month payback, that's a 150% return in year one. Long cycles also mean lots of touchpoints — retargeting and nurture sequences become incredibly powerful when you have months to build relationships with prospects who visited your site."

---

## Objection 5: "We'd rather invest in content/SEO."

**Response:**
"Content and paid ads aren't either/or — they're complementary. Content builds organic presence and captures bottom-of-funnel intent. Paid ads generate immediate traffic while you're building that organic foundation. The question isn't which to use — it's how to allocate. For a SaaS at your stage with your retention metrics, paid ads can fund your own growth immediately while content compounds over years. I'd recommend both, not one or the other."

---

## Objection 6: "We don't have the bandwidth to manage a new ad system."

**Response:**
"That's what the retainer covers. I run the optimization weekly: analyzing performance, identifying test opportunities, managing the testing process. Your team reviews and approves — you don't do the work. The goal is to build a system your team can eventually own, but I handle the heavy lifting during the learning curve. In 90 days, you'll understand the system well enough to manage it with 2-3 hours/week of internal time."

---

## Objection 7: "Our product keeps changing — copy will be outdated."

**Response:**
"The best copy focuses on problems and outcomes, not features and specifications. Features change; the underlying pain of missed sprint deadlines doesn't. When your product evolves, the template system means you update the specifics without rebuilding the entire framework. The psychological triggers — urgency, scarcity, social proof, specificity — remain constant. Only the factual fill-ins change."

---

## Objection 8: "We want to see results before we pay."

**Response:**
"I don't work on pure contingency because I can't guarantee your specific numbers — I can only control the copy and strategy. What I can guarantee is the process: discovery, strategy, testing framework, optimization protocol. If you follow the process and don't hit 3x ROAS within 90 days, I refund 25%. That's my skin in the game. But full contingency pricing usually attracts the wrong clients — people who want upside with no risk commitment, which means they don't prioritize the work."

---

## Objection 9: "Our competitor is spending 5x more on ads."

**Response:**
"Bigger budgets don't win. Better data wins. If they're spending $1M/month with generic copy and a 1.2x ROAS, they're burning $200K/month. You're spending $180K/month with 4.1x ROAS, generating $738K in MRR. Scale efficiency matters more than absolute spend. When you're ready to scale, you can — but only if the unit economics work. You want to scale a 4x ROAS machine, not a 1.2x money pit."

---

## Objection 10: "We'd need to involve our CFO/legal before committing."

**Response:**
"Normal. I build in review time for exactly this scenario. The proposal is structured for executive approval — it's not a binding contract until you sign. If legal needs specific contract terms, I can provide them. If finance needs ROI projections, I can walk through the math. The numbers are real and defensible — they're based on your actual data and industry benchmarks."
