# Talking Points: Product Page Conversion Copy
## Lumina Skincare Case Study

---

## For Sales Calls & Pitches

### The Core Problem We Solved

"They had beautiful products, a credible founder, and strong brand aesthetics — but their product pages read like ingredient labels. '15% L-Ascorbic Acid, Vitamin E, Ferulic Acid' is what a chemist finds interesting. Customers want to know what their skin will look like in 14 days. The conversion rate reflected that gap: 1.8% vs. 3.2% industry benchmark."

### What Made the Difference

1. **Outcome-first positioning** — "We stopped describing what was in the bottle and started describing what would happen to the customer's skin. 'Skin that looks like you slept 8 hours' instead of '15% L-Ascorbic Acid.'"

2. **Specificity built trust** — "'89% of users reported visible brightening' was more credible than 'clinically proven.' Real data, specific timeframes, verifiable claims."

3. **Real scarcity** — "Small-batch production was a genuine constraint we surfaced. 'Next batch ships in 3 weeks' — not fake urgency, but real production truth."

4. **Benefit storytelling** — "We transformed features into felt experiences. 'Brightens and evens skin tone' became 'See it in 14 days.'"

### The Results Story

"Within 60 days of implementation:
- Conversion rate jumped from 1.8% to 5.4% (+200%)
- Add-to-cart rate went from 8.2% to 28.1% (+243%)
- Time on product page increased from 1:24 to 3:12 (+129%)

At 40,000 monthly visitors, that's $135K+ in additional monthly revenue. The copy paid for itself in the first week."

---

## Key Objection Responses

### "Our products are clinical — won't emotional copy undermine the credibility?"

"Clinical and emotional aren't opposites. '15% L-Ascorbic Acid' is clinical. '89% of users saw visible brightening in 14 days' is clinical AND compelling. We're not replacing the science — we're translating it into language customers respond to. The credibility comes from the specificity, not the jargon."

### "We're worried about FTC compliance with outcome claims."

"All claims are substantiated. We have clinical studies to back every specific statistic. The '89% reported visible brightening' is from an independent clinical trial with documented methodology. No 'results may vary' needed because we're not claiming universal outcomes — we're reporting study data."
