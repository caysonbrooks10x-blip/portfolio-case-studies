## Problem Statement

Lumina's products work. The brand is credible (founder is a board-certified dermatologist). The packaging is beautiful. The store looks premium.

But the product pages read like ingredient lists with a price tag.

"15% L-Ascorbic Acid, Vitamin E, Ferulic Acid" — that's what your competitors say. That's what a chemist finds interesting. Your customers are looking for something else: "What will my skin look like in 14 days?"

At 1.8% conversion rate, you're well below the 3.2% industry benchmark for premium DTC skincare. The traffic is there. The product is there. The page copy is the bottleneck.

---

## Proposed Solution

Rewrite product page copy to convert browsers into buyers — without sacrificing the clinical credibility that makes Lumina different from "wellness" brands.

### Key Transformations

1. **Headlines** — Descriptive → Evocative
   "Vitamin C Serum" → "The Radiance Protocol"

2. **Descriptions** — Ingredient-first → Outcome-first
   "15% L-Ascorbic Acid" → "Skin that looks like you slept 8 hours, even when you didn't"

3. **Benefits** — Feature lists → Felt experiences
   "Brightens and evens skin tone" → "See it in 14 days: 89% of users reported visible brightening"

4. **Social proof** — Generic ratings → Specific outcomes
   "★★★★☆ Great product!" → Outcome-specific testimonials with real names

5. **Scarcity** — Generic shipping → Real production context
   "Ships in 2-3 days" → "Limited batch production — next batch ships in 3 weeks"
