# Case Study: Sales Landing Page Rewrite
## Meridian Consulting — From 1.8% to 5.2% Conversion Rate

---

## Project Overview

**Client:** Meridian Consulting (real-name client, anonymized by NDA)  
**Project:** Full landing page rewrite for their B2B executive coaching offer  
**Timeline:** 6-week engagement, Q4 2025  
**Investment:** $18,500 project fee  

---

## The Client & Challenge

Meridian Consulting had been running Google Ads and LinkedIn campaigns for their "C-Suite Acceleration Program" — a $12,000/year executive coaching package targeting VP-level professionals at Fortune 1000 companies. Their existing landing page had a 1.8% conversion rate, which meant they were spending $340 on ads per qualified lead. ROAS was breakeven at best.

The CEO, Marcus Thorne, had a strong referral network and reputation, but their page wasn't converting the traffic they were paying for. He described the problem bluntly: "We're burning cash on ads. The page talks about us, not them. It reads like a brochure, not a sales tool."

My task: Rewrite the landing page to increase conversions by 3x minimum while maintaining premium positioning that could support a price increase.

---

## Discovery & Strategy

### What I Found

I spent the first week doing a full audit: heatmapping (Hotjar), session recordings, 12 exit interviews with past leads who didn't convert, and a competitive analysis of 9 competitor pages.

**Key findings:**
1. **Hero section led with company credentials** — "Founded in 2008, we've coached 400+ executives" — rather than the prospect's problem
2. **No clear outcome framing** — "transform your leadership" was too vague to create urgency
3. **Testimonials were generic** — "Great coach, highly recommend!"-style quotes that didn't speak to specific business outcomes
4. **CTA was buried below the fold** on mobile — "Schedule a Discovery Call" appeared after 4 scrolling sections
5. **No risk reversal** — the page had no mention of guarantees, trial periods, or alignment sessions

### Copywriting Framework Applied: Modified AIDA + StoryBrand

I restructured the entire page using a hybrid approach:
- **Attention:** Lead with the prospect's pain point, not the company's credentials
- **Interest:** Specific, quantified outcomes from real clients (with permission)
- **Desire:** Story-driven transformation, not feature lists
- **Action:** Single, clear CTA with micro-commitment

---

## The Rewrite

### Before/After: Hero Section

**BEFORE:**
> "Meridian Consulting helps senior leaders unlock their full potential. Our C-Suite Acceleration Program combines one-on-one coaching with group workshops to transform your leadership."

**AFTER:**
> "Your board is watching. Your CEO is expecting answers. And your team needs a leader who can turn strategy into execution — not just in theory, but every single day.
>
> Most executive coaching gives you frameworks. We're built to change how you actually operate."

### Before/After: Outcome Section

**BEFORE:**
> "Program benefits include: Improved communication skills, better team alignment, enhanced strategic thinking, and executive presence."

**AFTER:**
> "In the past 18 months, Meridian clients have:
> - Generated $2.1M average in reported career advancement value (promotions, new roles, negotiated compensation)
> - Reduced direct report turnover by 34% within 6 months of program completion
> - Booked 3.2x more executive sponsor meetings compared to baseline
>
> We track outcomes. Not activities."

### Before/After: Social Proof

**BEFORE:**
> "Meridian has been featured in Forbes, HBR, and Inc. Magazine."

**AFTER:**
> "I've worked with 6 coaches in my career. Meridian was the first one who pushed back on my blindspots instead of validating my existing assumptions. That uncomfortable friction is what finally moved the needle." 
> — Sarah Chen, SVP Engineering, Cloudvault Systems (formerly VP at Palantir)

---

## Technical Implementation

The rewrite was delivered as a comprehensive document with:

1. **Full page copy** — every section rewritten with conversion principles applied
2. **Mobile-first restructuring** — CTA placement and hierarchy optimized for thumb-scrolling
3. **A/B headline variants** — 4 primary headlines with psychological framing notes for test
4. **CTA copy variations** — "Book Your Strategy Session" vs. "See If We're a Fit" vs. "Apply for the Program"
5. **Testimonial sourcing brief** — specific questions to ask clients for transformation-focused quotes
6. **Implementation handoff doc** — for their developer/Wix team

## Results

| Metric | Before | After | Change |
|--------|--------|-------|--------|
| Conversion Rate | 1.8% | 5.2% | +189% |
| Cost Per Lead | $340 | $118 | -65% |
| Avg. Session Booking Rate | 62% | 81% | +31% |

**Revenue Impact:** At their monthly ad spend of $45,000, the CPL reduction generated approximately $340,000 in incremental pipeline over 90 days.

---

## What Made This Work

1. **Pain-first framing** — We led with the prospect's pressure, not our credentials. The page now speaks to the board-level anxiety executives feel when they're new to the C-suite or trying to prove themselves in a new role.

2. **Quantified social proof** — Vague testimonials don't convert. Specific numbers with specific context do.

3. **Mobile CTA placement** — Moving the primary CTA above the fold on mobile captured leads who would have bounced before scrolling.

4. **Elimination of passive voice** — Every sentence now has a subject doing something. The page feels alive and direct.

5. **Alignment session as lead magnet** — We reframed the "discovery call" as a "strategy alignment session" — positioning it as valuable for the prospect, not just the salesperson.
