# Objections & Responses: Landing Page Rewrite
## Meridian Consulting Case Study

---

## Objection 1: "Our page already looks professional."

**Response:**
"Professional and converting are different things. Your page probably looks fine — clean design, good credentials, reasonable copy. But looking professional and engineering conversion are different disciplines. The goal isn't to look good in a portfolio award. It's to turn a visitor into a lead. Those require different approaches."

---

## Objection 2: "We don't have time for a rewrite."

**Response:**
"I handle the heavy lifting. My process is designed to minimize your time investment: 90-minute discovery call, two review rounds, one handoff walkthrough. That's roughly 4 hours of your time total. The rest is me doing the work. If you can't find 4 hours, that's a different conversation — but I'd argue that leaving $200K+ in incremental pipeline on the table is the bigger time problem."

---

## Objection 3: "We might be too niche for a general approach."

**Response:**
"That's exactly why generic templates don't work. Your niche is your conversion advantage — it allows for specific language, specific outcomes, specific social proof that resonates harder than vague claims. My approach is to dig into the specifics of your offer, your audience, and your market positioning until the copy sounds like it was written by someone who lives in your world. Not a template."

---

## Objection 4: "Our CEO is protective of the current messaging."

**Response:**
"Being protective of existing messaging is natural — it took work to create. But the data is the data: 1.8% CVR means the current messaging isn't working. My job isn't to replace your CEO's voice; it's to amplify what's already working and eliminate what's not. The rewrite will still sound like Meridian — just Meridian performing at its best."

---

## Objection 5: "We'd need to test this before committing."

**Response:**
"Testing is built into my process. I deliver A/B headline variants with psychological framing notes so your team can run proper experiments. I'm not asking you to bet everything on one headline. I'm giving you options with rationale so you can validate with data. The goal is to give you confidence in what you're running, not just faith."

---

## Objection 6: "What if our traffic quality is the real problem?"

**Response:**
"Possible. But if traffic quality were the issue, you'd be seeing low engagement metrics — high bounce rate, low time on page, no scrolling. What Hotjar showed us was high engagement (2+ min average time, 60%+ scroll depth) but low conversion. That means people are interested, but the page isn't closing them. That's a copy problem, not a traffic problem."

---

## Objection 7: "Our product is more expensive than competitors."

**Response:**
"Premium pricing requires premium positioning. If you're competing on price, you've already lost — there's always someone cheaper. The rewrite positions you on value, outcomes, and transformation. At $12K/year for executive coaching, the real question isn't 'why is this more expensive?' It's 'what's the cost of NOT investing in your leadership at this level?' I help you answer that question compellingly on the page."

---

## Objection 8: "Our testimonials aren't that strong."

**Response:**
"That's a process problem, not a permanent condition. Most clients have strong experiences — they just don't know how to articulate them. I include a testimonial sourcing brief in my deliverables that gives you specific questions to ask clients. 'How has your career changed since working with us?' gets you better quotes than 'Would you recommend us?' The brief pays for itself immediately."

---

## Objection 9: "We'd need to involve our legal/compliance team."

**Response:**
"Normal. I build in review time for exactly this scenario. If legal needs to approve specific claims, that's fine — I can provide supporting documentation for any quantified statistic I use. Everything in the rewrite is either client-reported data (which you own) or defensible industry benchmarks. Nothing that will create compliance exposure."

---

## Objection 10: "We're planning to redesign our site anyway."

**Response:**
"If you're doing a full redesign, that's actually the perfect time for a copy rewrite. Design and copy should be developed together — visual hierarchy and messaging hierarchy need to align. If you're redesigning anyway, don't redesign around weak copy. Get the messaging right first, then build the design around a conversion architecture. Otherwise you're building a beautiful house on a weak foundation."
