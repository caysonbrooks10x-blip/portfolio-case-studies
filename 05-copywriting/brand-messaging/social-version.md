## LinkedIn Articles

### Article: "Why Mid-Market Transformations Fail (And What Actually Works)"

**Opening:**
I've watched dozens of transformations fail. Not because the strategy was wrong, not because the execution was poor — because the model was wrong.

Large consulting firms are built for large clients. When they engage with mid-market companies, they bring the same model: teams of junior consultants, rotating casts, heavy documentation.

For a Fortune 500 company with a PMO and a full-time internal team, that works.

For a mid-market manufacturer running at 80% capacity with a lean leadership team and a workforce that can't stop to absorb change — it doesn't.

**Body:**
The failure pattern is always the same:

1. Firm arrives with methodology
2. Firm produces assessment, roadmap, recommendations
3. Firm rotates to next engagement
4. Client realizes implementation is harder than the assessment suggested
5. Client attempts implementation without support
6. Transformation stalls or fails

**What works:**

The embedded model. Senior consultants who stay through implementation. Realistic roadmaps built for how mid-market companies actually change. Accountability for outcomes, not just deliverables.

That's what Northridge built. Not because we're smarter — because we've been in the room when it goes wrong.
