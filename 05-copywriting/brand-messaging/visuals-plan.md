# Visuals Plan: Brand Messaging + Offer Positioning
## Northridge Partners — Visual Identity Alignment

---

## Visual Strategy Overview

The repositioning shifts Northridge from "generic consulting firm" to "strategic partner in critical moments." The visual identity must support that narrative without contradicting it.

**Core principles:**
- Serious, not stuffy — confidence without arrogance
- Industrial context without being heavy — modern manufacturing aesthetic
- Photography over illustration — authenticity over abstraction
- Human presence without being casual — leadership, not casual

---

## Photography Direction

### Hero Images (Website)

**Style:** Documentary-style photography of real manufacturing environments — not stock photography of suits shaking hands.

**Subjects:**
- Manufacturing floor (real operations, not posed)
- Leadership teams in meeting settings (authentic, not staged)
- Factory/process imagery (clean, well-maintained operations)
- Plant manager or COO in context (not corporate portrait)

**Avoid:**
- Handshake imagery
- Whiteboard presentations
- Generic "business meeting" stock photos
- Suits without context

**Color treatment:** Warm, not cool. Industrial but human. Avoid overly dramatic lighting.

### Team Photos

**Style:** Documentary, on-location, in context

**Options:**
- Northridge team on-site at client facility (with permission)
- Team meeting in real office setting (not conference room)
- Leadership headshots (environmental, not studio white)

---

## Typography

### Primary Fonts

**Headlines:** Inter or similar clean sans-serif
- Bold weight for impact
- Large size for authority
- Dark colors for professionalism

**Body:** Inter or similar clean sans-serif
- Regular weight for readability
- Comfortable line height

**Accent:** Small caps or bold for emphasis
- "The continuity you need" — tagline in small caps
- Key phrases in bold throughout

### Font Don'ts

- Serif fonts (too traditional/corporate)
- Script fonts (too casual)
- Display fonts (too trendy)

## Color Palette

| Color | Hex | Usage |
|-------|-----|-------|
| Navy | #1a2744 | Primary, headlines, authority |
| Charcoal | #2d3748 | Body text |
| Steel Gray | #64748b | Secondary text, captions |
| White | #ffffff | Backgrounds, contrast |
| Warm Gray | #f7f5f2 | Alternate section backgrounds |
| Gold | #b8860b | Accent, selective use (CTAs, highlights) |

**Rationale:** The palette is intentionally conservative — premium consulting without being flashy. The navy establishes authority; the warm gray adds approachability.

---

## Logo Treatment

**Current logo:** Preserved (Northridge wordmark)

**Treatment guidelines:**
- Always leave adequate clear space
- Navy or reversed (white) on dark backgrounds
- Never stretch, rotate, or alter proportions
- Minimum size: 1 inch width

---

## Layout Principles

### Website

**Structure:** Clean, minimal, content-forward

- Generous white space
- Single-column body copy
- Clear section breaks
- No busy backgrounds or gradients
- Photography as texture, not decoration

**Visual rhythm:**
- Hero sections: Full-width, dramatic
- Body sections: Contained width (720px max)
- Alternating content/image sections

### Sales Collateral

**Structure:** Minimal, focused

- 12 slides maximum (reduced from 40)
- One idea per slide
- Large type, minimal bullets
- Photography dominant, text supporting
- Clear call-to-action on final slide

---

## Iconography

**Style:** Simple, line-based icons

**Appropriate icons:**
- Manufacturing/industrial (gears, process flow)
- Strategic planning (target, compass)
- Transformation (arrows, growth)
- Leadership (seat at table, whiteboard)

**Avoid:**
- Clip art
- 3D rendered icons
- Photographic icons

---

## Video Guidelines

### Overview

- Documentary style, not corporate video
- On-site footage preferred (with client permission)
- Executive interviews: Direct address, minimal editing
- No heavy music — ambient industrial sound acceptable

### Tone

- Serious, not promotional
- Documentary authenticity over polished production
- Real conversations, not scripts
- Outcomes over methodology

---

## Presentation Templates

### Capability Deck

**Design:**
- 12 slides, minimal
- One headline per slide
- Supporting visual (photo, icon, or chart)
- No more than 3 bullet points per slide

**Format:**
- 16:9 aspect ratio
- Consistent header placement
- Navy, white, and warm gray palette
- Gold accent for emphasis only

### Proposal Template

**Design:**
- Clean, readable
- Professional but not stuffy
- Logo in header
- Clear section breaks
- Page numbers and headers

## Print Collateral

**Design:**

**Design:**
- Single page (avoid lengthy brochures)
- Key differentiators listed
- QR code to website/case studies
- Professional but memorable

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Over-designed materials** — Premium positioning requires restraint
2. **Generic stock photography** — Authenticity matters more than polish
3. **Too many colors** — Stick to the palette
4. **Dense layouts** — White space signals premium
5. **Outdated imagery** — Manufacturing photography must feel contemporary
