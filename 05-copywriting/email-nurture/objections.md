# Objections & Responses: Email Nurture + Launch Sequence
## ArcLight Courses Case Study

---

## Objection 1: "Our audience doesn't want to be emailed this much."

**Response:**
"If your emails are relevant, targeted, and valuable, more frequent contact deepens the relationship — it doesn't damage it. The ArcLight list went from twice-monthly broadcasts (24 emails/year) to a full nurture system (60+ emails/year for active subscribers). Unsubscribes dropped 74%. Why? Because we stopped treating everyone the same and started sending content relevant to each segment's interests."

---

## Objection 2: "We don't have time to approve 12 emails for a launch."

**Response:**
"That's a process problem, not a permanent condition. Most of the 12 launch emails don't need heavy approval — they're educational content, not claims. I build in two review rounds: first for strategic outline (one email outline per day), second for final copy. Your team reviews, approves, and we launch. If you don't have bandwidth, we can set up automated approval workflows. The sequence is designed to minimize your time investment."

---

## Objection 3: "Our list is mostly international — different time zones."

**Response:**
"This actually favors email over live webinars or calls. Email is asynchronous — everyone gets it when they wake up, wherever they are. For the launch sequence, we send at one primary time (optimized for your highest-engagement segment) and let the open rates guide optimization. For welcome sequences and behavioral triggers, send-time automation means each subscriber gets the email at their local morning time. Time zone is an argument for more email, not less."

---

## Objection 4: "We don't have the data to segment like you're describing."

**Response:**
"Most lists have more data than they realize — it's just not surfaced. Course interest is captured at signup. Engagement data (opens, clicks) is in your ESP. Purchase history is in your CRM. We do a full data audit in week one to surface what's there, then structure segments around existing signals. Where we need new data (preference centers), we build lightweight collection into the welcome sequence. The segmentation plan is built around what exists plus what we can reasonably collect."

---

## Objection 5: "Our last email campaign had high unsubscribes. We're worried about making it worse."

**Response:**
"High unsubscribes usually signal one of two problems: You're emailing too frequently for the value delivered, or you're sending the same content to everyone. We address both. First, we set up preference centers so subscribers can choose their frequency. Second, we segment so people only get content relevant to their interests. For ArcLight, unsubscribes dropped from 340 per launch to 89. The people who unsubscribed weren't your buyers anyway — they were frequency mismatches."

---

## Objection 6: "We want to own this system, not be dependent on you."

**Response:**
"That's exactly the goal. The deliverables include a complete playbook — every sequence documented, every segment logic explained, every decision framework laid out. By week 8, your team can run the entire system without me. The retainer is optional for ongoing optimization, not dependency. If you want to own it fully, you can. I provide the documentation to make that possible."

---

## Objection 7: "We already have a 'welcome email' — is that enough?"

**Response:**
"One welcome email is the start, not the solution. A real welcome sequence:
- Confirms the subscription (trust)
- Sets expectations for what's next (reduce anxiety)
- Gets them to take a first action (reduce inertia)
- Follows up with relevant content over 30 days (build relationship)
- Introduces your product naturally (first sale trigger)

One email can't do all that. We build an 8-email welcome sequence that accomplishes all five goals over 30 days."

---

## Objection 8: "Our course catalog is broad — segmentation sounds complicated."

**Response:**
"Complicated segmentation is a trap. We start simple: two primary dimensions — engagement (active vs. cold) and purchase history (buyer vs. non-buyer). That's a 2x2 matrix that covers most use cases. From there, we add course interest as a third dimension where you have the data. The complexity grows with your data quality, not upfront. Start simple, add layers as you prove the system works."

---

## Objection 9: "What if the launch fails? We don't want to burn the list."

**Response:**
"A failed launch burns a list when it's a panic email ("BUY NOW!") after months of silence. Our launch sequence is 30 days of value delivery before any ask. Subscribers who engage with the content are more receptive to the offer — and if they don't engage, they simply don't see the sales emails (they're filtered by engagement). The sequence is designed to protect the list, not pressure it. If the product doesn't convert, we've still built goodwill and brand trust."

---

## Objection 10: "We'd need to involve our legal/compliance team."

**Response:**
"Normal. ESP compliance is built into every email (CAN-SPAM, GDPR, CASL). I use standard compliant practices — no deceptive subject lines, no misleading preheaders, clear unsubscribe links, physical address in footer. If your legal team has specific requirements (affiliate disclosures, specific disclaimers), that's documentable and addressable. I build compliance into the template structure, not as an afterthought."
