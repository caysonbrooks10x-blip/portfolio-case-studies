# Talking Points: Email Nurture + Launch Sequence
## ArcLight Courses Case Study

---

## For Sales Calls & Pitches

### The Core Problem We Solved

"They had 47,000 subscribers and a proven product, but their last launch generated only $31,000. That's a 0.07% conversion rate. The issue wasn't the list or the product — it was the system. Every email went to everyone at once, no segmentation, no sequence, no behavioral triggers. It was broadcasting, not marketing."

### What Made the Difference

1. **Problem-first launch arc** — "We didn't start with 'buy our course.' We started with 'the cloud job market just shifted.' Four trust-building emails before any ask. The list needed to remember why they subscribed before they could be sold to."

2. **Segmentation** — "We split the list by engagement and purchase history. Warm subscribers got the launch sequence. Cold subscribers got a re-engagement sequence first. Same product, different approach."

3. **Behavioral triggers** — "Browse abandonment, cart abandonment, re-engagement. These are automated flows that capture intent signals. Someone who browsed 5 course pages is showing intent — we catch that with a triggered email."

4. **Real scarcity** — "Cohort-based pricing with real enrollment windows. Not fake urgency — genuine scarcity that the sales team confirmed. That authenticity shows in the conversion rate and the low unsubscribe rate."

### The Results Story

"The launch generated $180,000 in 30 days — 5.8x their previous launch. The unsubscribe rate dropped from 340 to 89 (per launch). But more importantly, they now have a system: a launch playbook, welcome sequences, behavioral triggers, segmentation. Every launch going forward compounds on this foundation."

---

## Key Objection Responses

### "Our list is old and unresponsive."

"Old doesn't mean dead. Unresponsive usually means uninspired. Our first email to the cold segment had a 22% open rate — twice their list average. The problem wasn't the subscribers. It was that no one had given them a reason to engage. Once we sent content that matched their interests, they came back."

### "We don't have the content for a full sequence."

"You don't need to create new content — you need to organize what you have. Testimonials you've collected. Curriculum outlines. Student outcomes. Instructor bios. We build the sequence around existing assets, then fill gaps as needed. The work is structuring, not creating."

### "Won't more emails just annoy people?"

"More irrelevant emails annoy people. More targeted, valuable emails deepen relationships. The ArcLight list went from 12% average open rate to 34% average open rate. That's not annoyance — that's engagement. The key is segmentation: people get emails relevant to their interests, not the same broadcast to everyone."

### "Our product is seasonal — does this still work?"

"Seasonality actually makes sequences more valuable. Instead of 'flash sale' panic emails during your peak season, you build anticipation and nurture over months. By the time the launch window opens, the list is already primed. The launch is the culmination of months of relationship building, not the start."
