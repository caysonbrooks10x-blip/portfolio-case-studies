# Case Study: Email Nurture + Launch Sequence
## ArcLight Courses — $180K in 30 Days from Cold List

---

## Project Overview

**Client:** ArcLight Courses (real-name client, anonymized by NDA)  
**Project:** Email nurture sequence + product launch sequence  
**Timeline:** 10-week engagement, Q4 2025  
**Investment:** $22,000 project fee  

---

## The Client & Challenge

ArcLight Courses is an online education platform focused on professional certifications for tech professionals — cloud architecture, data engineering, and cybersecurity. They had built a strong content library (40+ courses), a decent email list (47,000 subscribers), and a decent product (completion rates above industry average at 68%).

The problem: their email marketing was essentially nonexistent. They sent a newsletter twice a month and expected that to drive sales. It didn't. Their last product launch generated $31,000 over 30 days from a list of 47,000 — a 0.07% conversion rate. Their CRM had a graveyard of unsegmented contacts, no automation, and no systematic way to nurture leads toward purchase.

The CEO, Tom Reyes, had a theory: "Our list is full of people who wanted free stuff and never intended to pay." My analysis showed otherwise — the list was under-monetized, not dead.

My task: Build an email nurture system that would prove the list's value — starting with their next course launch.

---

## Discovery & Strategy

### What I Found

**List Analysis:**
- 47,000 total subscribers
- Segmentable by: course interest (cloud/data/security), engagement level, purchase history
- 12,400 had purchased at least one course (26% conversion rate historically)
- 8,200 were "highly engaged" (opened 3+ emails in last 90 days)
- 34,800 were "cold" (no open in 60+ days)

**Existing Email Problems:**
1. No welcome sequence — new subscribers got the same newsletter as 3-year subscribers
2. No behavioral triggers — no re-engagement, no browse abandonment, no cart recovery
3. No launch playbook — each launch was ad-hoc, no systematic sequence
4. No segmentation — same content sent to everyone
5. No trust-building — every email was a sales email

**The Core Insight:**
The list wasn't dead — it was underexplored. 26% had already proven they would pay for courses. The cold segment (34,800) wasn't necessarily uninterested — they were uninspired. Different problem, different solution.

### Strategy

**Two-Track Approach:**

**Track 1: Launch Sequence (Immediate)**
For the upcoming "Cloud Architecture Professional" launch — a 12-week certification program at $1,497. Build a full launch sequence that would go out over 30 days to the full list.

**Track 2: Nurture Infrastructure (Long-term)**
Segment the list, build a welcome sequence, create behavioral triggers, and establish an ongoing nurture system that would compound over time.

## The Launch Sequence (30-Day ArcLight Cloud Architecture Launch)

### Framework: Modified Product Launch Formula (Jeff Walker) + Direct Response

The launch followed a strategic arc:

**Week 1: Seed the Problem**
Goal: Remind the list why this matters, without selling anything.

- **Email 1:** "The cloud job market just shifted" — Market trend data, salary growth, job posting analysis. Position the problem (skills gap) without mentioning the course yet.
- **Email 2:** "What cloud architects actually do all day" — Behind-the-scenes look at the role. Build desire and curiosity.
- **Email 3:** "The certification that changed my career" — Story-driven (real student testimonial, with permission). Not "buy this" — "here's what changed for someone like you."

**Week 2: Establish Credibility**
Goal: Show that ArcLight is the trusted source for this outcome.

- **Email 4:** "Why most cloud courses teach you to pass the test, not do the job" — Differentiation content. Position ArcLight's hands-on approach.
- **Email 5:** "Meet your instructor" — Instructor backstory, credentials, teaching philosophy. Humanize the product.
- **Email 6:** "The curriculum, deconstructed" — What you'll actually learn week by week. Specific, not vague.

**Week 3: Build the Offer**
Goal: Present the full offer with clear value.

- **Email 7:** "What the Cloud Architecture Professional program includes" — Full feature list with value stacking. Everything included (video modules, labs, practice exams, community access, 1:1 coaching calls).
- **Email 8:** "The price" — Anchor it properly. Show the $1,497 price against the value (salary increase potential, job security, role advancement). Frame as investment, not expense.
- **Email 9:** "The enrollment window" — Open cart with clear deadline. Scarcity (cohort-based, limited seats).

**Week 4: Convert**
Goal: Final push, handle objections, close the sale.

- **Email 10:** "One more reason this works" — Last social proof piece (newest testimonial, urgent outcome).
- **Email 11:** "Enrollment closes tonight" — Urgency email. Last chance.
- **Email 12:** "A question for you" — Soft re-engagement for non-opens. Personal tone, low pressure.

## The Welcome Sequence (For New Subscribers)

**Days 1-7: The Foundation**

- **Day 1:** Welcome + "Start here" guide — What ArcLight is, what they should explore first, how to get the most from the platform.
- **Day 3:** "Your first course is on us" — Free course offer (lead magnet to first paid course). Get them to take action immediately.
- **Day 5:** "What students like you are achieving" — Outcome-focused social proof.
- **Day 7:** "The approach that actually works" — ArcLight's teaching philosophy. Differentiate from passive video courses.

**Days 8-30: Build the Relationship**

- **Day 10:** Content email (relevant to their signup interest).
- **Day 15:** "Common questions answered" — FAQ about online learning, ArcLight's support, etc.
- **Day 21:** Product recommendation based on their interest (segment).
- **Day 30:** First sale trigger — Relevant course offer or launch invite.

## Behavioral Triggers

### Browse Abandonment
When: Subscriber browses 3+ course pages but doesn't enroll  
Email: "You left something behind" — Re-engage with specific courses viewed, highlight reviews and outcomes.

### Cart Abandonment
When: Subscriber adds course to cart but doesn't purchase  
Email: "Your cart is waiting" — Remind of what's in cart, include testimonial for that specific course, offer small incentive (free add-on).

### Re-Engagement (Cold List)
When: No open in 60+ days  
Email: "We miss you" — "Here's what's new at ArcLight" — If no open in 90 days, trigger "Are you still there?" with preference center.

### Post-Purchase
When: Customer completes purchase  
Email 1: "Your course is ready" — Immediate access, next steps.  
Email 2 (Day 3): "Getting started tips" — Help them start strong.  
Email 3 (Day 7): "You're not alone" — Community invitation, cohort connection.  
Email 4 (Day 14): "Progress check" — Encourage continuation, address barriers.

## Why This Worked

### 1. The Problem-First Launch Arc
Instead of "Buy our course!" we led with "The cloud job market just shifted." This respected the audience's intelligence and created genuine interest before the ask.

### 2. Segmentation and Timing
We didn't blast the entire list on day one. We warmed up the warm segments first, let social proof propagate, then expanded to cold segments. The 34,800 cold subscribers got a modified sequence with more re-engagement content first.

### 3. Value Stacking
The $1,497 price point was positioned against outcomes: salary data ($120K-$180K range for cloud architects), job security, career advancement. Every email reinforced the value math.

### 4. Trust Before Transaction
Four emails before any offer mention. The list needed to remember why they subscribed in the first place — ArcLight as a trusted learning partner, not just a course vendor.

### 5. Scarcity That Was Real
Cohort-based pricing with real enrollment windows. Not fake urgency — genuine scarcity that the sales team confirmed.
