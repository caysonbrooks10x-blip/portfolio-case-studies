# Talking Points: VSL Script
## Velocity Coaching Case Study

---

## For Sales Calls & Pitches

### The Core Problem We Solved

"They had a webinar funnel that was working — 8% attendee-to-buyer — but it couldn't scale because it required live attendance. The VSL funnel replaced the webinar bottleneck with a pre-recorded video that could convert cold traffic 24/7. The script did the heavy lifting: problem identification, objection handling, offer presentation. The sales call just confirmed fit."

### What Made the Difference

1. **Acknowledged skepticism immediately** — "You don't need another business course." We didn't pretend the audience trusted coaches. We led with understanding, which disarmed the resistance.

2. **Problem-agitation-solution structure** — "The problem isn't motivation. It's certainty." We built the case that the audience needed to feel understood before they could be sold to.

3. **Jason's story as the primary proof** — Not "here's what we teach" but "here's what I figured out." Authentic, specific, believable.

4. **Objection handling built into the script** — "You might not be ready for this." We addressed the main objections (time, prior mastermind failure, price) before they became blockers.

5. **Real scarcity** — 15-person cohort, 8 spots remaining, three-week window. Not manufactured urgency — genuine constraints that the sales team could confirm.

### The Results Story

"First campaign: $50K ad spend → $205K in enrollments. 13 new members at $15,775 average. 4.1x ROAS. The VSL completion rate was 68% — well above the 40-60% industry benchmark. People watched the whole thing because it spoke to their specific situation, not generic inspiration."

---

## Key Objection Responses

### "Can't we just use a webinar recording?"

"Webinars have a framing problem: they're inherently 'event' content, even when recorded. Audiences know they're watching a sales pitch that was filmed live. VSLs are designed for on-demand consumption. The pacing, the hook, the objection handling — it's all structured for the viewer who can stop at any moment. That's a different medium requiring different craft."

### "The price is too high for a video."

"The video isn't selling a $12,500 program. The video is pre-qualifying leads and handling objections. The actual sale happens on the application call. The VSL's job isn't to close — it's to get qualified prospects to raise their hand. If the video is doing its job, the sales call is a confirmation, not a conversion."

### "Our audience is skeptical of video sales pitches."

"Which is why we led with skepticism acknowledgment. 'You don't need another business course.' We named the elephant in the room immediately. That signals to the audience: we're not naive about how they feel. That honesty is more persuasive than pretending to be trustworthy."
