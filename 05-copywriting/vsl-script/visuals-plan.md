# Visuals Plan: VSL Script
## Velocity Coaching — Video Production Recommendations

---

## Production Philosophy

The VSL should feel like a conversation, not a presentation. Jason is talking directly to one person — not reading a script to a camera crew.

**Core principles:**
- Authentic, not polished
- Direct address, not performance
- Simple setup, professional output
- Client testimonials: unscripted, specific, real

---

## Filming Setup

### Location
- **Recommended:** Home office or professional studio
- **Background:** Bookshelf or simple wall (not blank white)
- **Lighting:** Natural light from window (45-degree angle) + one softbox fill
- **Avoid:** Conference rooms, corporate offices, generic stock locations

### Camera Setup
- **Primary:** 4K mirrorless or cinema camera (Sony A7S III, Canon R5)
- **Lens:** 35mm or 50mm prime (not zoom)
- **Frame:** Medium close-up (chest to head)
- **Height:** Eye level or slightly below (flattering)
- **Distance:** Close enough to feel intimate, far enough to avoid distortion

### Audio (Critical)
- **Primary:** Lavalier mic (Rode Lavalier II or similar)
- **Backup:** Shotgun mic (Rode VideoMic Pro+)
- **Ambient:** Record room tone for editing consistency
- **Standard:** -12dB headroom, 24-bit audio

---

## Jason's On-Camera Presentation

### Dress Code
- **Recommended:** Solid color, professional casual
- **Colors:** Navy, burgundy, forest green (avoid black or white)
- **Avoid:** Patterns, logos, distracting accessories
- **Fit:** Well-fitted, not baggy

### Delivery Style
- **Tone:** Conversational, direct, slightly urgent
- **Pacing:** Natural speech with intentional pauses at key points
- **Eye contact:** Look at lens (not screen), as if talking to one person
- **Gestures:** Minimal, intentional (avoid nervous fidgeting)

### Direction Notes in Script
- **[JASON ON CAMERA]:** Direct address, conversational
- **[ON CAMERA - STANDING]:** Movement allowed, energy higher
- **[ON CAMERA - SITTING]:** More intimate, lean forward slightly
- **[DIRECT ADDRESS]:** Emphasize "you" — look at lens

## Client Testimonial Filming

### Setup
- **Location:** Client's office or home (authenticity over polish)
- **Camera:** Same as Jason setup
- **Frame:** Medium shot (waist up)
- **Interview style:** Off-camera interviewer (not on-screen)

### Client Guidance
- **Dress:** Professional casual matching their brand
- **Background:** Their workspace (books, equipment = authenticity)
- **Energy:** Excited, specific, not scripted
- **Length:** 60-90 second answers

### Questions to Ask (Per Interview Guide)

1. "Before Velocity, what was your biggest frustration with your business?"
2. "What specifically did Velocity help you change?"
3. "What was the result? Give me the numbers if you can."
4. "What would you say to someone considering Velocity?"

### Good Testimonial Indicators
- Specific numbers ("$340K" not "a lot of money")
- Real situations ("I was working 50 hours" not "I was busy")
- Named outcomes ("My average project went from $8K to $22K")
- Emotional authenticity (not scripted enthusiasm)

## B-Roll Suggestions

### Throughout Script
- **Problem section:** Clock, overflowing inbox, late-night laptop glow, calendar full of meetings
- **Proof section:** Client testimonial footage, community events, retreat photos
- **Offer section:** Course platform screenshots, community interface, retreat venue

### Style
- **Authentic over polished:** Real moments, not staged
- **Cinematic:** Shallow depth of field, good lighting
- **Brief:** 3-5 second clips, not extended sequences

---

## Graphics & Text Overlays

### Lower Thirds
- **Style:** Simple name + title card
- **Duration:** 3-4 seconds
- **Font:** Sans-serif (Inter, Helvetica)
- **Color:** White text, subtle shadow

### On-Screen Text
- **Key statistics:** Large, bold numbers
- **CTA:** URL displayed throughout final section
- **Scarcity:** "8 spots remaining" — red text, bottom of screen

### Transition Graphics
- **Between sections:** Simple fade or dissolve
- **Duration:** 1-2 seconds
- **Avoid:** flashy animations or distracting effects

---

## Color Grading

### Tone
- **Warm and professional:** Not cool/clinical
- **Consistent across all footage:** Match B-roll to interview footage
- **Slight vignette:** Draws attention to center frame

### Specifications
- **Deliverable:** 4K ProRes or H.264 MP4
- **Color space:** Rec. 709
- **Frame rate:** 24fps or 30fps (not 60fps — looks too "video")

## Editing Pacing

### By Section

| Section | Duration | Pacing | Notes |
|---------|----------|--------|-------|
| Hook | 0:00-1:00 | Fast, urgent | Hook must land in first 10 seconds |
| Problem | 1:00-5:00 | Medium | Build empathy, don't rush |
| Agitation | 5:00-10:00 | Slower | Let tension build |
| Proof | 10:00-20:00 | Variable | Client testimonials break up Jason |
| Offer | 20:00-28:00 | Medium | Clear, not rushed |
| Objections | 28:00-35:00 | Conversational | Relatable, not defensive |
| Urgency | 35:00-38:00 | Urgent | Pick up energy |
| CTA | 38:00-40:00 | Calm, confident | Land the plane |

### Total Runtime
- **Target:** 38-40 minutes
- **Allow:** +5 minutes for natural variation
- **Hard cut:** No single section should exceed 8 minutes without a break

## Thumbnail Recommendations

For cold traffic (YouTube, Facebook):

### Option A: Emotion-Focused
Jason's face, direct address, slight concern/urgency expression  
Text: "Why 'raise your rates' advice fails"

### Option B: Stat-Focused
Jason with microphone or presentation  
Text: "$180K → $400K in 18 months"

### Option C: Problem-Focused
Overworked consultant imagery  
Text: "Stop working 55 hours"

**Test:** All three variants with 33/33/33 traffic split for first 7 days.

---

## Mobile Considerations

- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9 for YouTube/web; 9:16 vertical cut for Instagram/TikTok
- **Text legibility:** Large fonts for on-screen text (24px minimum)
- **CTA visibility:** Clear and readable on small screens
- **First 10 seconds:** Most critical on mobile (most drops happen early)

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Over-produced look** — Feels like corporate video, loses authenticity
2. **Scripted delivery** — Reads as sales pitch, not conversation
3. **Weak hook** — First 10 seconds determine whether someone watches
4. **Slow pacing** — Modern attention spans require intentional energy
5. **Generic testimonials** — Must have specific numbers and outcomes
6. **Poor audio** — Viewer leaves immediately if audio is bad
