## Velocity Coaching — 4.1x ROAS on $50K Ad Spend

## The Client & Challenge

Velocity Coaching is a high-ticket business coaching program for consultants, agency owners, and freelancers who want to scale from $100K to $500K+ in annual revenue. Their flagship "Velocity Mastermind" costs $12,500/year and includes group coaching, mastermind community, and strategic planning sessions.

They had strong organic content (10,000 YouTube subscribers, 22,000 email list) and decent conversion on their webinar funnel (4.2% registration-to-attendee rate, 8% attendee-to-buyer rate). But the webinar model required live attendance, which limited scale and created a bottleneck.

The founder, Jason Torres, wanted to test a VSL funnel: cold traffic → landing page → pre-recorded video → phone call booking. The pitch: if the video could pre-qualify and pre-sell at 2-3% conversion, they could scale without the webinar bottleneck.

My task: Write the VSL script — the most critical conversion element in the funnel.

---

## Discovery & Strategy

### What I Found

**Target Audience Analysis:**
Business coaches call them "tired generalists." They're consultants who've been profitable for a few years but are maxed out — working 50-60 hours/week, taking on every client because they can't say no, pricing by hourly instead of value. They joined Jason's content community because they resonated with his story of scaling from solo consultant to $400K in 18 months.

**Psychographic Profile:**
- Burned out on "hustle culture" but trapped by their own business model
- Aware they need to change, unsure what to change first
- Resistant to coaches who "don't get it" — they've tried generic business coaching before
- Drawn to specificity and evidence, not inspiration

**The Core Objection:**
The audience doesn't trust coaches. They've seen the "6-figure consultant" courses, the mastermind hype, the lifestyle branding. They want proof before belief.

**Strategic Approach:**
The VSL needed to:
1. Acknowledge the skepticism immediately (don't pretend it doesn't exist)
2. Use Jason's specific story as the primary proof element
3. Frame the problem in their exact language (not coach-speak)
4. Build the case for Velocity's specific methodology
5. Handle the price objection before it surfaces
6. Create urgency that feels authentic, not manufactured

## The VSL Script

### Framework: Problem-Agitation-Solution + Modified PAS + Social Proof Stacking

The script followed a classic direct response structure but with modifications for video:

**Hook (0:00-1:00):** "You don't need another business course."

**Problem (1:00-5:00):** "Here's what's actually keeping you stuck."

**Agitation (5:00-10:00):** "Why the obvious solutions don't work."

**Proof (10:00-20:00):** Jason's story + client transformations.

**Offer (20:00-28:00):** "Here's what Velocity actually is."

**Objection Handling (28:00-35:00):** "Here's why you might not be ready."

**Urgency (35:00-38:00):** "Here's why now makes sense."

**CTA (38:00-40:00):** "If you're ready, here's what to do."

---

### Full Script

---

**[HOOK — 0:00-1:00]**

**[JASON ON CAMERA]**

"You don't need another business course.

You've probably bought three or four of them by now. Maybe you've even done a mastermind or two. You have the notebooks. You have the templates. You have the frameworks.

And you're still working 55 hours a week. Still saying yes to clients you don't want. Still waking up at 2 AM with your to-do list scrolling through your head like a stock ticker.

So before we go any further — I want you to know: I'm not here to give you another playbook. I'm not here to tell you to 'work less, earn more' like it's a simple equation.

I'm here because I've been where you are. And I know exactly why the obvious solutions don't work.

But more importantly — I know what does."

**[TRANSITION]**

---

**[PROBLEM — 1:00-5:00]**

**[JASON ON CAMERA]**

"Let me describe what's actually happening in your business right now.

You're profitable. You're good at what you do. Your clients refer you. You've built something real.

But here's the trap: your income is still tied to your time.

Every hour you don't bill is an hour you're not earning. So you take on more clients. More projects. More commitments. And now you're not just doing the work — you're managing the business of doing the work. The proposals. The onboarding calls. The scope creep. The 'can you just' emails that turn into six hours of unpaid labor.

You know this pattern. You've named it. You've tried to break it.

But every time you try to 'raise your prices' or 'fire bad clients,' something happens. A slow month shows up. A good referral comes along. You get nervous. You take the project anyway.

And the cycle continues.

This isn't a motivation problem. You don't need to be told to 'raise your rates' or 'set boundaries.' You've heard that advice. You know that advice.

The problem is that advice doesn't account for the real reason you keep taking the work: you don't have enough certainty that better clients will show up if you do."

**[TRANSITION]**

---

**[AGITATION — 5:00-10:00]**

**[JASON ON CAMERA]**

"Now here's what nobody tells you about this problem — and why the obvious solutions fail.

You go to a business course. They teach you how to write better proposals, how to present value, how to raise your prices.

You try it. It works — kind of. You raise your rates. You get some pushback. A few prospects disappear. You get nervous.

A few months later, you're back where you started. Maybe slightly better, but fundamentally the same.

Or you join a mastermind. You get accountability partners. You get weekly calls. You get a community of people who 'get it.'

And for a while, that helps. The calls give you clarity. The community gives you support.

But six months later, you're still showing up to the calls. Still describing the same problems. Still not getting the breakthrough you came for.

Why?

Because most business coaching is designed for people who have a marketing problem or a sales problem. If you have a pipeline problem — not enough qualified leads, not enough certainty — generic business advice doesn't touch the core issue.

The 'raise your prices' advice doesn't work when you're terrified that your next month will be slow. The 'fire bad clients' advice doesn't work when you're not sure good clients exist.

You need a different approach. One that addresses the real constraint: not how to run your business better, but how to build a business that runs without you being the bottleneck."

**[TRANSITION]**

---

**[PROOF — 10:00-20:00]**

**[JASON ON CAMERA — DIRECT ADDRESS]**

"Let me tell you about how I figured this out.

Four years ago, I was exactly where you are. I'd been consulting for six years. I was making good money — $180,000 in my best year. But I was working 60 hours a week. I hadn't taken a real vacation in three years. My wife told me she missed me at dinner. Not because I was traveling — because I was in the next room, working.

I knew something had to change. So I did what consultants do: I bought courses. I joined masterminds. I hired a business coach.

Nothing worked.

Here's what I finally realized: I didn't have a business problem. I had a pipeline problem. I didn't need better time management — I needed certainty that good clients would keep showing up.

Once I fixed that — really fixed it — everything else became easy. Raising my rates was obvious. Firing bad clients was easy. Working 40 hours became possible.

Within 18 months, I went from $180K to $400K. And I wasn't working more. I was working differently.

That's what I teach in the Velocity Mastermind."

**[VIDEO: Client testimonials, on camera, unscripted feel]**

I took a vacation for the first time in four years."

Jason helped me see that my 'control issues' were really a pipeline problem. Once I had certainty about where my next clients were coming from, I could let go. Now my team runs the delivery. I work 30 hours a week. Revenue is $340K."

Velocity showed me how to package my value for the clients I actually wanted. I doubled my rates in 90 days. My average project size went from $8K to $22K."

**[TRANSITION — BACK TO JASON]**

---

**[OFFER — 20:00-28:00]**

**[JASON ON CAMERA]**

"The Velocity Mastermind is a 12-month program for consultants, agency owners, and freelancers who are making money but want to break through to the next level — without working more hours.

Here's what you get:

**One: Weekly Group Coaching Calls**
Every week, you get a 90-minute live session with me and a small group of your peers — 12 to 15 people maximum. These aren't recorded trainings. These are working sessions where we dig into your specific situation.

**Two: The Velocity Framework**
This is the system I developed over four years of testing and iteration. It has three phases:

Phase one: Pipeline certainty. Getting to the point where you have three months of qualified leads, always. This is the foundation — without this, nothing else works.

Phase two: Offer architecture. How to package your expertise so clients pay for outcomes, not hours. How to create retainers and project-based work that scales.

Phase three: Team and systems. How to build a business that runs without you being the bottleneck. This is where the real leverage is.

**Three: The Mastermind Community**
Private community of 200+ Velocity alumni and current members. This isn't a Facebook group where people post memes. It's active business owners who are in the trenches, sharing what's working and what's not.

**Four: Annual Strategy Retreat**
Once a year, we bring the full community together for a three-day intensive. Two days of deep work on your business, one day of strategic planning for the year ahead.

**The price is $12,500 for the full year.**

That's less than most business coaches charge for six months of coaching. And you're not just getting coaching — you're getting the framework, the community, and the retreat.

But here's the real question: Is this program right for you?"

---

**[OBJECTION HANDLING — 28:00-35:00]**

**[JASON ON CAMERA]**

"Let me address the main reasons you might not be ready for Velocity.

**One: 'I don't have time for another program.'**

Fair. If you're already working 55 hours a week, the last thing you need is another commitment.

But here's what I'd ask you to consider: The Velocity Mastermind is designed to give you time back, not take more of it. The weekly calls are 90 minutes. The work between calls is designed to save you hours, not create more work.

If you're too busy to invest 90 minutes a week in fixing the root cause of your time problem, you'll be too busy forever.

**Two: 'I've done masterminds before and they didn't help.'**

Me too. Most masterminds are just accountability groups with some coaching on top. You show up, you share your problems, everyone gives advice, you leave feeling heard but not changed.

Velocity is different because it's built around a specific methodology. We're not just processing your problems — we're applying the framework to your situation. Phase one, two, three. Every conversation has a structure.

**Three: '$12,500 is a lot of money.'**

It is. And I don't want you to make this decision lightly.

But let me reframe it: What's the cost of staying where you are?

If you're stuck at $100K/year, the gap between where you are and where you want to be is $100K. Or $200K. Or $300K, depending on your goals.

$12,500 is an investment in closing that gap. Not a cost — an investment.

And if you go through the full program and don't feel like you've gotten at least $12,500 in value, I'll refund your money. No questions. That's how confident I am."

If you're serious about this, I'd encourage you not to wait for the next cohort. Every month you wait is a month you're still working 55 hours a week. It's a month you're still saying yes to clients you don't want. It's a month you're not building the business that gives you freedom.

The next cohort. Three weeks. Fifteen spots. Eight remaining.

If you're in, here's what to do."
