# Objections & Responses: VSL Script
## Velocity Coaching Case Study

---

## Objection 1: "We don't have the budget for video production."

**Response:**
"The script is the conversion engine — production quality is secondary. There are VSLs that convert at 10%+ shot on iPhone with basic lighting. The ROI calculation isn't 'what does video production cost' — it's 'what does a 3x ROAS improvement generate in revenue.' For a $12,500 program with a $50K launch budget, the math is obvious. Invest in the script first. Production quality can scale as revenue grows."

---

## Objection 2: "Our founder isn't comfortable on camera."

**Response:**
"Then don't use the founder. Use a professional spokesperson trained in direct response video. The story (client transformations, methodology) can be told through interviews and B-roll. The key is the script — someone with no video presence can still have a high-converting funnel if the copy is right. Production quality doesn't convert; persuasion converts."

---

## Objection 3: "Video is expensive to update. What if the market changes?"

**Response:**
"The core framework doesn't change — the problem, the agitation, the solution architecture. Those are evergreen. The specific examples, testimonials, and pricing can be updated without re-filming the entire VSL. Modular production (filming founder segments separately from testimonial segments) means targeted updates without full re-production costs."

---

## Objection 4: "Our audience prefers written content."

**Response:**
"Preference and conversion are different things. Your email list prefers written content — but do they buy at higher rates than video funnel traffic? Video consistently outperforms text for high-ticket items because it builds parasocial trust faster. For a $12,500 program, buyers want to feel like they know the person they're buying from. Video does that more efficiently than text."

---

## Objection 5: "We already have a webinar. Why replace it?"

**Response:**
"Don't replace it — add to it. VSL funnels and webinar funnels serve different acquisition channels. VSL handles cold traffic at scale. Webinars handle warm traffic (email list, existing contacts) with higher touch. The webinar becomes a second conversion layer for people who watched the VSL but didn't apply. It's additive, not substitutive."

---

## Objection 6: "How do we measure VSL performance?"

**Response:**
"Standard funnel metrics: Landing page CVR, video completion rate, application rate, close rate. The VSL script itself should have clear drop-off points (we built in natural transitions) so you can identify where viewers are losing interest. Key leading indicator: video completion rate above 60% signals the script is resonating. Below 40% signals a problem in the hook or pacing."

---

## Objection 7: "What if people watch but don't apply?"

**Response:**
"Two causes: wrong traffic or wrong script. If the VSL completion rate is high (60%+) but applications are low, the issue is in the offer or CTA — not the script's persuasion. A/B test the CTA section. If completion rate is low, the hook isn't grabbing the right audience or the pacing is off. Review the heatmap of average watch time to identify where dropoff happens."

---

## Objection 8: "We don't have enough testimonials for a proof section."

**Response:**
"You need three to five, not 50. The proof section needs specificity: exact numbers, specific situations, real names and companies. Jason's story can carry most of the weight if it's specific enough. For client testimonials, reach out to your top 10 past clients with a specific question about a specific outcome. You'll get one or two great ones. That's enough."

---

## Objection 9: "Our program is different from coaching programs. The framework won't apply."

**Response:**
"The VSL framework is a structure, not a script. The specific content — the problem, the agitation, the proof — is built from your specific offer and audience. The framework is the skeleton; the copy is the flesh. If your program has a different structure or outcome, we build different content within the framework. The conversion principles apply universally; the specific words are custom."

---

## Objection 10: "We'd need legal review of the claims."

**Response:**
"Normal. I build in review time. All statistics and testimonials are sourced from real data with documentation. No income claims (beyond what past clients reported), no exaggerated results, no "typical" language. Everything is either verifiable or attributed to specific individuals. Legal can review the script line-by-line and flag anything that needs modification."
