# Talking Points: Lead Generation + Outreach Operations

## For Sales Conversations

**When asked about the problem:**
"Derek was doing outreach the way everyone does it wrong — 40 generic emails a week, no research, no follow-up, and then wondering why nobody replied. Four meetings a year from 18 months of effort isn't a bad response rate. It's a nonexistent system."

**When asked about the approach:**
"We didn't just write better emails. We built Derek a complete lead generation machine. Apollo.io for prospecting data, a 12-touch multi-channel sequence across email, LinkedIn, and phone, HubSpot tracking to see exactly where prospects dropped, and automated follow-up that actually completed. The sequence handles 97% of the work; Derek handles the conversations."

**When asked about results:**
"Reply rates went from under 1% to over 12%. Meeting volume went from 4 a year to 31 in a single quarter. Pipeline from outbound went from $50K to $480K per quarter. The investment returned over $900K in new client revenue in year one."

**When asked about investment:**
"$16,000 over 8 weeks. Derek closed 14 new clients from outbound in the first year at $25K average retainer. That's $350K in new revenue, plus another $550K in pipeline that closed subsequent quarters. The outreach machine keeps generating."

## Objection Handlers

**"We tried cold email before and it didn't work"**
"Did you try it with proper data, personalized research, multi-channel sequences, and automated follow-up? Most 'tried' cold email means sent a few hundred generic templates with no follow-up. We built Derek a machine that touches each prospect 12 times across 3 channels with personalized content."

**"Our industry is too niche for cold outreach"**
"Summit works with mid-market manufacturers — exactly the type of 'niche' everyone says can't be reached by outbound. We found 287 verified decision-makers, built persona-specific messaging, and generated 31 meetings in one quarter. 'Niche' just means fewer competitors doing it right."

**"Our buyers are too sophisticated for cold outreach"**
"Exactly right — that's why generic templates don't work. Our approach is research-intensive, personalized, and multi-channel. We're not selling; we're starting conversations with people who've already identified a problem we solve. Sophisticated buyers respond to sophistication, not spam."

**"We don't have bandwidth to manage outreach"**
"Derek was spending 70% of his BD time on low-leverage outreach. After we built the machine, he spends 25% — because the system runs itself. You don't manage the machine; it books meetings for you."
