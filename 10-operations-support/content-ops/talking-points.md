# Talking Points: Content Operations Pipeline

## For Sales Conversations

**When asked about the problem:**
"Sarah had built Maven into a premium content agency known for quality. But she was the ceiling — 60% of her time on content oversight, every piece going through her approval, 4 pieces a month maximum. The agency had a 6-month waitlist. Demand wasn't the problem. Production capacity was."

**When asked about the approach:**
"We didn't just hire more writers. We built Maven a complete content operations pipeline — Notion hub for planning, strategic brief templates that ensured quality before production started, client playbooks locking in voice and brand, a 12-type content framework covering full-funnel needs, Asana workflow with tiered approvals, and analytics connecting content to revenue."

**When asked about results:**
"Output went from 4 pieces a month to 28. Sarah's oversight dropped from 60% to 15% of content — she went from content bottleneck to strategic advisor. Client retention jumped from 72% to 94%. Agency revenue grew from $1.2M to $2.8M. The waitlist disappeared."

**When asked about investment:**
"$20,000 over 8 weeks. Maven's content-attributed revenue went from $280K to $1.4M. Sarah's billable strategic work increased from 20% to 65% of her hours. That's the difference between being a content producer and running a content agency."

## Objection Handlers

**"Our content is too unique to systematize"**
"Maven's content WAS unique — that was the problem, it was all in Sarah's head. We systematized the PROCESS, not the creativity. Every piece still requires original thought; we just built the infrastructure so that thought doesn't have to be Sarah's on every piece."

**"We've tried content templates before and they killed creativity"**
"Bad templates kill creativity. Our templates are strategic frameworks — they ensure alignment with business goals and brand voice, then let writers create freely within those guardrails. Maven's writers actually report MORE creative freedom now because they're not guessing about client expectations."

**"Our clients have very different needs"**
"Every Maven client got a custom content playbook — voice, brand, audience, goals. The 12-type framework is just a menu of options; the strategy is always custom. Different needs are handled through different playbooks, not different systems."

**"This sounds like it requires hiring more writers"**
"Maven didn't hire a single additional writer. They restructured how existing team members worked. Sarah was spending 60% of her time on oversight — that capacity became available for strategy and new client relationships without adding headcount."
