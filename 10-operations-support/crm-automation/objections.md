# Objections & Responses: CRM Cleanup + Automation

## Objection: "Our agents will never consistently use a CRM"

**Response:** "Northvista's agents had a 60% refusal rate before we started. After training and the accountability systems we built, adoption hit 94%. The difference is that we involved agents in building the workflows — they told us what didn't work, and we built around their actual habits. Compliance isn't forced; it's designed into the system."

---

## Objection: "We tried cleaning our CRM before and it didn't help"

**Response:** "Did you rebuild the workflows at the same time? Cleaning data without fixing the processes that created bad data is like mopping while the faucet runs. We fixed both — the existing 12,400-contact mess AND the system that was creating new problems every day. That's why it stuck."

---

## Objection: "Our leads are just bad quality"

**Response:** "If your lead response time is averaging 6 hours, you're not giving any leads — good or bad — a fair chance. Most leads have a 5-7 minute window of peak interest after first contact. After that, they cool off significantly. Northvista's leads weren't bad quality — they were just being followed up on when they were already cold. 8-minute response times changed everything."

---

## Objection: "This is too expensive for a software problem"

**Response:** "It's not a software problem — it's a $4.7M revenue problem. Northvista was spending $40K/year on marketing leads and closing $2.1M from them. After the CRM rebuild, that same $40K budget produced $6.8M in closed business. That's $4.7M in additional revenue from a $24K investment. What's the ROI on $24K that returns $4.7M?"

---

## Objection: "We don't have time for a 2-week audit"

**Response:** "The audit is two weeks. In that time, we found 47 distinct data quality issues and cleaned 8,400 contacts. Without knowing what's actually in your CRM, any solution is a guess. The audit isn't delay — it's the foundation for everything else. Would you prescribe medicine without a diagnosis?"

---

## Objection: "Our team is too busy to learn new workflows"

**Response:** "Northvista's team was the busiest I'd ever seen — 42 agents juggling listings, showings, and closings. We designed workflows that saved them 45 minutes a day on average. The training investment pays back within week one. And we do the training in focused sessions, not all-day disruptions."

---

## Objection: "We're switching to a different CRM anyway"

**Response:** "Which one? If you're planning to migrate anyway, that's actually MORE reason to clean the data now — dirty data migrates dirty, and you just repeat the same problems in a new system. Let's do a data audit before you migrate so you start clean. It's the right order of operations."

---

## Objection: "My manager/broker isn't on board with spending this"

**Response:** "That's exactly who should hear this conversation. Northvista's broker was the one who demanded answers about the $40K marketing budget. The ROI we delivered — $4.7M in new attributed revenue — made the investment an easy sell. I can help you build the business case if you need it."
