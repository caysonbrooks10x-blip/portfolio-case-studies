# Talking Points: CRM Cleanup + Automation

## For Sales Conversations

**When asked about the problem:**
"Nobody opens HubSpot at Northvista. That's 60% of agents ignoring the main tool. Why? Because the data was garbage, the workflows were broken, and following up meant nothing happened. They had a $40K/year marketing budget pouring into a leaky bucket with no bottom."

**When asked about the approach:**
"We didn't touch the existing data first. We audited everything — 12,400 contacts, 47 distinct data quality issues, 12 agent workflows. Only then did we rebuild. 8,400 contacts cleaned in 2 weeks, then we layered on 14 automated sequences that made following up the path of least resistance."

**When asked about results:**
"Lead response went from 6.3 hours to 8 minutes. Conversion tripled. Marketing ROI went from 31 cents on the dollar to $1.87. The CRM went from a tool nobody used to the backbone of their entire revenue operation."

**When asked about investment:**
"$24,000 over 8 weeks. Northvista attributed $4.7M in new revenue to the CRM rebuild. That's a 19,500% ROI. The question isn't whether you can afford it — it's whether you can afford not to."

## Objection Handlers

**"Our agents will never use a CRM consistently"**
"Northvista's agents were some of the most resistant I've encountered — 60% actively refused. After training and the accountability systems we built, adoption hit 94%. It works because we involved agents in building the workflows, not imposed them."

**"We tried cleaning our CRM before and it didn't help"**
"Did you rebuild the workflows too? Cleaning data without fixing the processes that created bad data is like mopping while the faucet runs. We fix both — the existing mess and the system creating new messes."

**"Our leads are just bad quality"**
"If your lead response time is 6 hours, you're not even giving good leads a chance. Most leads have a 5-minute window of peak interest. After that, cold. 8-minute response times changed everything for Northvista."

**"This seems expensive for a software problem"**
"It's not a software problem. It's a revenue problem. $40K/year in marketing was producing $2.1M in closed business. After fixing the CRM, that same budget produced $6.8M. That's $4.7M in additional revenue from a $24K investment."
