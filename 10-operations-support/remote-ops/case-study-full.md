# Case Study: Remote Operations Management Setup

## Building Operational Infrastructure for a Distributed Team at Stellarwave Marketing

---

### The Client

**Jordan Willis**, Co-founder and COO of Stellarwave Marketing — a digital marketing agency that had grown from 4 people in one city to 23 team members across 6 countries in 18 months. The growth was exciting, but the operations were collapsing under their own weight.

Jordan had built the company with an "everyone figures it out" culture that worked when the team was small and local. Now with people in Austin, Toronto, London, Berlin, Manila, and Sydney, that culture was creating chaos: 18 tools in use with no integration, project hand-offs that fell through the cracks, timezone drama that made simple decisions take days, and a burn rate that was 40% above plan because nobody had visibility into actual utilization.

They'd just signed a $2.4M contract with a major retail brand — their biggest client — and Jordan knew their operations would need to handle it or they'd implode.

### The Challenge

The symptoms were visible to everyone, including the new enterprise client:

- **Tool chaos**: 18 different tools, zero integration, data silos everywhere
- **Timezone paralysis**: Simple decisions taking 3-5 days due to timezone gaps
- **Project hand-off failures**: 34% of projects missing at least one deliverable
- **Invisible utilization**: Nobody knew who was overworked vs underutilized
- **Knowledge fragmentation**: Tribal knowledge locked in individual heads
- **Burn rate bleeding**: 40% above plan with no clear reason

The company was growing fast but felt like it was held together with duct tape and good intentions.

### The Approach

We didn't just add more tools. We built a complete remote operations infrastructure that made distributed work actually function.

**Phase 1: Operational Discovery (Weeks 1-2)**
- Mapped all 18 tools currently in use
- Interviewed all 23 team members about workflows and pain points
- Tracked 30 days of actual project handoffs and identified failure points
- Analyzed timezone coverage and communication patterns
- Reviewed burn rate by project and service line

**Phase 2: Infrastructure Architecture (Weeks 3-4)**
- Designed integrated tool ecosystem (4 tools replacing 18)
- Built async-first communication protocols
- Created project workflow templates for all service lines
- Designed utilization tracking system
- Built timezone-aware meeting frameworks

**Phase 3: Implementation (Weeks 5-8)**
- Migrated to Asana as project hub (replacing 5 other tools)
- Set up Slack workspace with structured channels and automation
- Built Notion knowledge base with 200+ pages
- Implemented Screen Studio for async video updates
- Created Loom-based onboarding system for new hires
- Configured Zapier integrations between core tools

**Phase 4: Team Rollout & Optimization (Weeks 9-10)**
- Led team through new systems (minimal disruption)
- Created async onboarding playbook for future hires
- Established weekly ops rhythm and reporting cadences
- Set up real-time burn rate dashboard
- Documented all processes for ISO-style documentation

### The Results

**Tool & Systems Consolidation:**
- Tools in use: **18** → **4** (78% reduction)
- Tool spend: **$4,200/month** → **$1,840/month** (56% reduction)
- Data silos: **12** → **0** (fully integrated)
- Knowledge base pages: **0** → **247**

**Operational Efficiency:**
- Project handoff success: **66%** → **96%**
- Decision cycle time: **3.5 days** → **4 hours**
- Meeting hours/week: **22** → **8** (64% reduction)
- Avg project delivery time: **18 days** → **12 days** (33% faster)

**Team Performance:**
- Utilization visibility: **0%** → **100%**
- Burn rate variance: **40% over** → **3% over** (92% improvement)
- New hire ramp time: **6 weeks** → **2 weeks** (67% faster)
- Team satisfaction with ops: **3.1/10** → **8.4/10**

**Business Impact:**
- $2.4M enterprise client retained and expanded
- Revenue per employee: **$124K** → **$187K** (51% improvement)
- Gross margin: **34%** → **52%** (18pp improvement)
- Client NPS: **31** → **67**

### Tools Implemented

- **Asana**: Project management hub (replaced 5 tools)
- **Slack**: Team communication with structured channels
- **Notion**: Knowledge base and documentation
- **Loom**: Async video updates and onboarding
- **Zapier**: Tool integrations and automation
- **Grain**: Recorded meeting transcripts and searchable archives
- **Clockwise**: Smart scheduling across timezones
- **Google Workspace**: Email, calendar, drive

### Key Frameworks Developed

1. **Async-First Communication Protocol**: When to async vs sync, response time expectations, documentation standards
2. **Timezone-Aware Workflow**: "3-2-1" rule (3 overlapping hours, 2 async updates, 1 weekly sync)
3. **Project Handoff Checklist**: 12-point verification before any project transfers
4. **Utilization Tracking Dashboard**: Real-time visibility into team capacity and allocation
5. **Burn Rate Early Warning System**: Alerts when project exceeds 10% of budget
6. **Remote Onboarding System**: 2-week structured ramp with Loom videos and check-ins
