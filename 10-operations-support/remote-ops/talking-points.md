# Talking Points: Remote Operations Management

## For Sales Conversations

**When asked about the problem:**
"Stellarwave had 23 people across 6 countries and operations held together with duct tape. 18 tools, zero integration, project hand-offs failing a third of the time, decisions taking 3-5 days because of timezone gaps. They had just signed their biggest client — $2.4M — and everyone knew their operations would collapse under the pressure."

**When asked about the approach:**
"We didn't add more tools. We rebuilt the operating model from scratch. 18 tools became 4 integrated systems. We built async-first protocols, timezone-aware workflows, real-time utilization tracking, and a 247-page knowledge base. Everything designed for distributed work actually function."

**When asked about results:**
"Decision cycles went from 3.5 days to 4 hours. Meetings dropped 64%. Project hand-off success went from 66% to 96%. Tool spend dropped 56%. And their $2.4M client is still with them and expanding. That's the difference between duct tape and infrastructure."

**When asked about investment:**
"$32,000 over 10 weeks. Stellarwave's gross margin improved 18 percentage points — on $4.2M in revenue, that's $756K in additional profit. Not to mention they retained a $2.4M client that was about to walk. The ROI was immediate and massive."

## Objection Handlers

**"We're too distributed to have standard processes"**
"That's exactly what Stellarwave said — 23 people, 6 countries, every timezone imaginable. What we built was actually MORE structure than their collocated competitors had, because the structure replaced in-person oversight. Async-first protocols and documentation let distributed teams outperform local ones."

**"Our team will resist changing tools again"**
"Stellarwave's team had already accumulated 18 tools through shadow IT and adoption fatigue. We consolidated, didn't add. The new tools replaced the chaos, not familiar systems. And we trained everyone before cutting over — minimal friction."

**"We tried Asana/Notion before and it didn't work"**
"Most implementations fail because they're dropped in without understanding how teams actually work. We spent 2 weeks auditing workflows before configuring a single tool. The tools we chose match how Stellarwave's team actually operates, not how a best-practice guide says they should."

**"Our timezones are too complex for async"**
"Timezone complexity is the reason MOST teams need MORE meetings. We built the opposite — a '3-2-1' framework (3 overlapping hours, 2 async updates, 1 weekly sync) that turned timezone gaps from a problem into an advantage. Decisions went from 3.5 days to 4 hours."
