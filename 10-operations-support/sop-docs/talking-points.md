# Talking Points: Internal SOP + Documentation System

## For Sales Conversations

**When asked about the problem:**
"Rebecca had built something remarkable on tribal knowledge. The problem: that knowledge walked out the door every time someone took a vacation, got sick, or left. Her operations director had just resigned with 11 years of undocumented processes. 12% documentation coverage. New hires taking 14 weeks. And PE investors asking about operational maturity in 90 days."

**When asked about the approach:**
"We didn't just write some documents. We built a complete documentation system — Notion workspace with 8 department hubs, 87 critical SOPs, 12 video walkthroughs, 45 role guides, and a governance system that ensures it stays current. Built for actual adoption, not for show. The team was involved in building it, not just receiving it."

**When asked about results:**
"Documentation went from 12% to 94%. New hire ramp went from 14 weeks to 5. Decision escalations dropped 83%. The PE deal closed — $12M. And Rebecca took her first real vacation in 3 years without her phone ringing 47 times. The documentation paid for itself in the first deal."

**When asked about investment:**
"$28,000 over 12 weeks. The PE deal that closed was $12M. Operational readiness was a key factor in that closing. Beyond that, faster hiring saves $180K/year in contractor costs, reduced errors saves $340K/year. The documentation ROI was immediate and massive."

## Objection Handlers

**"Our team won't use a documentation system"**
"Cadence's team didn't use documentation either — because it didn't exist. We built the system with the team, not for them. Documentation champions in each department, weekly review rituals, integration into performance reviews. By week 12, the team was the one asking for more documentation."

**"We don't have time to document while doing the work"**
"That's exactly what Cadence said. But continuing without documentation costs more — 14-week ramp times, 47 escalations a week, errors from inconsistent execution. We built a documentation sprint process that minimizes operational disruption. The team documents as they work, with our support."

**"Every company is different, templates won't work"**
"Every company IS different — that's why we don't use templates. We interviewed all 45 employees, observed 30+ workflows, and built documentation that matches how Cadence actually works. The templates are just formatting standards; the content is entirely custom."

**"We tried documentation before and nobody read it"**
"That's because it was documentation for its own sake — files dumped in a shared drive nobody opened. Our system is integrated into daily work: tasks reference SOPs, onboarding uses the guides, decisions flow through documented trees. People read it because it's actually useful."
