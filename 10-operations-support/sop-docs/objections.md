# Objections & Responses: Internal SOP + Documentation System

## Objection: "Our team won't use a documentation system"

**Response:** "Cadence's team didn't use documentation either — because it didn't exist to use. We didn't drop a system on them; we built it with them. Documentation champions in each department, weekly review rituals, integration into performance reviews. By week 12, Cadence's team was the one asking for more documentation coverage, not resisting what existed."

---

## Objection: "We don't have time to document while doing the work"

**Response:** "That's exactly what Rebecca said. But continuing without documentation costs more — 14-week ramp times for new hires, 47 escalations a week to leadership, errors from inconsistent execution. We built a documentation sprint process that minimizes disruption: the team documents as they work, with our structured support and templates. The investment in time now saves multiples later."

---

## Objection: "Every company is different, generic templates won't work"

**Response:** "Every company IS different — which is why we don't use generic templates. We interviewed all 45 Cadence employees, observed 30+ workflows across 8 departments, and built documentation that matches exactly how Cadence works. The templates are formatting standards; the content is 100% custom to your operations."

---

## Objection: "We tried documentation before and nobody read it"

**Response:** "Most documentation fails because it exists for its own sake — files dumped in a shared drive that nobody opens. Our system integrates documentation into daily work: tasks reference SOPs, onboarding uses role guides, decisions flow through documented trees. People read it because it's useful, not because it's mandatory."

---

## Objection: "Our processes change too frequently to document"

**Response:** "Cadence's processes change too — like every company's. That's why we built a governance system with ownership, review dates, and update protocols. Documentation isn't a one-time project; it's a living system. We established weekly review sessions and quarterly audits to keep everything current. Change happens; the system handles it."

---

## Objection: "This is expensive for just writing documents"

**Response:** "$28,000 over 12 weeks. Cadence closed a $12M PE deal — operational readiness was a key factor in that closing. Beyond that, faster hiring saves $180K/year in contractor costs. Reduced errors saves an estimated $340K/year. Faster project starts save 3+ weeks per development. Documentation isn't writing documents; it's operational infrastructure."

---

## Objection: "I need buy-in from my leadership team"

**Response:** "We include a leadership presentation in Phase 1 that makes the business case for documentation. But frankly, the PE timeline creates its own urgency — investors will ask. Rebecca's leadership team was convinced the moment they saw the PE due diligence checklist and realized how exposed they were."

---

## Objection: "We have sensitive information that can't be documented"

**Response:** "We build permission levels into the system. Not every document is for every person. Sensitive compensation details, client information, and proprietary processes can be restricted to appropriate audiences. Documentation doesn't mean broadcasting everything to everyone."
