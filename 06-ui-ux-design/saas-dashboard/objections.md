# Prism Analytics — Objection Handling

## "Our users are technical—they'll figure it out"

**Acknowledge:** "You're right that Prism attracts technical users. And many of them have."

**Pivot:** "But 'figuring it out' isn't the same as efficient use. We found that even power users had developed elaborate workarounds—signs they were compensating for poor UX, not thriving with it. After the redesign, those same users called it 'life-changing.' Technical users deserve excellent tools too."

---

## "We tried a redesign before and it failed"

**Acknowledge:** "Redesigns are notoriously difficult. The failure rate is high because the approach is often wrong."

**Pivot:** "We'd be happy to share our methodology. The key differences: user research before design decisions, three rounds of testing before any code is written, phased rollout instead of big-bang launch, and a design system that prevents regression."

---

## "Our engineering team is stretched thin"

**Acknowledge:** "This is a real constraint, and we respect it."

**Pivot:** "Our phased approach means engineering doesn't need to build everything at once. We can launch core improvements in Phase 1 and iterate from there. Plus, our Storybook component library means engineering can work in parallel with clear specifications."

---

## "The budget seems high for a 'redesign'"

**Acknowledge:** "It's a significant investment. But we don't do surface-level makeovers."

**Pivot:** "This is a complete UX overhaul with a component library, design system, and ongoing support. We track ROI rigorously—our minimum expectation is 5:1. At Prism, we delivered 13:1."

---

## "How do we know metrics won't regress after launch?"

**Acknowledge:** "This is a valid concern. Many teams see initial gains that fade."

**Pivot:** "We implement monitoring from day one. Hotjar, FullStory, and custom event tracking. Plus, the design system makes it much harder to introduce inconsistencies that degrade UX over time."

---

## "Our stakeholders have conflicting opinions on what the UI should look like"

**Acknowledge:** "This is normal for enterprise products. Everyone has opinions."

**Pivot:** "Our research process creates a shared reality. When stakeholders see 24 users struggling with the same issue, opinions align quickly. We facilitate those conversations and make the final calls based on evidence, not hierarchy."

---

## "Can we do this faster?"

**Acknowledge:** "We could cut corners, but you'd pay for it later."

**Pivot:** "12 weeks is the minimum for quality. We'd rather discuss which features can ship in Phase 1 vs. future iterations. The core experience improvements can launch in 10 weeks if we prioritize ruthlessly."

---

## "What if users hate the new design?"

**Acknowledge:** "Some users always resist change. It's human nature."

**Pivot:** "We test extensively before launch and offer an opt-out period during transition. But the data tells a different story: NPS went from 28 to 61. Users didn't just accept the new design—they preferred it."
