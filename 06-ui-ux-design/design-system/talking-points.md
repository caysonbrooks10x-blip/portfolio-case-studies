## Soundbites

**On the problem:** "Eight product teams had built UI in isolation. There were 47 ways to make a button. Engineers spent 20% of every sprint rebuilding UI that should already exist."

**On the solution:** "We built a token-first system. Tokens propagate to all 85 components automatically. Change one color, it updates everywhere. That's the power of a well-architected system."

**On migration:** "Enterprise products can't stop development for a redesign. We built the system for incremental migration—teams adopt as they ship, not all at once."

**On ROI:** "The engineering time savings alone paid for the system in 3 months. But the real value was NPS improvement and reduced enterprise churn."

## Answers to Common Questions

**"How did you get teams to adopt the system?"**
We made adoption easy: comprehensive docs, training sessions, office hours, and Slack support. But we also made it valuable: components were better than what teams had built themselves.

**"How do you handle custom requirements from enterprise customers?"**
The token system supports white-labeling. We built a theming system that lets enterprise customers customize colors without breaking components.

**"What about legacy code?"**
We built migration guides and provided support. Legacy components were deprecated gradually, not removed abruptly. Teams had time to migrate.

**"How do you maintain the system?"**
We established governance: component champions in each team, monthly office hours, quarterly roadmap reviews. The system evolves with the product.
