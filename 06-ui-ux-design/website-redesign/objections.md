# Meridian Health — Objection Handling

## "Our doctors want full control over their profiles"

**Acknowledge:** "Provider comfort with their public presence is important."

**Pivot:** "We designed the profile system with provider input. Each provider can update their availability and bio directly. The design makes them look professional without requiring them to become marketers."

---

## "Healthcare websites can't be too 'casual'—we need to look professional"

**Acknowledge:** "Trust is paramount in healthcare. Looking unprofessional could hurt rather than help."

**Pivot:** "We tested extensively with patients. The new design scored 34 points higher on 'I trust this service' compared to the old site. Patients described it as 'professional but approachable'—the right balance for telehealth."

---

## "We have a lot of existing content that needs to be migrated"

**Acknowledge:** "Content migration is always a challenge, especially with years of accumulated material."

**Pivot:** "We budgeted 2 weeks specifically for content migration and created an archiving strategy. Old blog content was moved to a new structure, and the most valuable pages were redesigned as landing pages."

---

## "Our legal team will need to review everything"

**Acknowledge:** "Healthcare has compliance requirements that other industries don't."

**Pivot:** "We build WCAG 2.1 AA compliance into every project. We've worked with healthcare clients before and understand HIPAA considerations for web design. We can build in your legal review cycles."

---

## "We already have a dev team—why do we need design-only?"

**Acknowledge:** "Your team is talented. They've built a solid platform."

**Pivot:** "This project is about design strategy and UX, not development. We'd hand off Figma files with complete specifications. Your team would implement. We stay involved for QA and iteration."

---

## "The timeline seems aggressive"

**Acknowledge:** "12 weeks is focused. We built in buffer time for testing and revision."

**Pivot:** "The phased approach means you see results at each milestone. Design is done in week 8, so you'll see the full vision early. Development is weeks 9-12."

---

## "How do we know this will work for our specific patient population?"

**Acknowledge:** "Generic best practices don't always translate to specific audiences."

**Pivot:** "We conducted 16 user interviews specifically with your patient population. Every major design decision was validated with real users before implementation."

---

## "What's the risk that conversion drops after launch?"

**Acknowledge:** "This is a legitimate concern. Change always carries risk."

**Pivot:** "We run a 4-week A/B test so you see data before full commitment. Plus, the old site remains accessible during the transition period. We've never seen conversion drop in a properly tested healthcare redesign."
