# Case Study: Altitude — Brand Identity System

## Client Overview

**Client:** Altitude  
**Industry:** Outdoor Adventure Travel / Experiential Tourism  
**Company Size:** 28 employees, Bootstrapped (profitable since Year 2)  
**Project Duration:** 10 weeks  
**My Role:** Brand Identity Designer  
**Team:** 1 Brand Designer, 1 Strategist, 1 Motion Designer  

---

## The Challenge

Altitude had built a profitable outdoor adventure travel business over 8 years, specializing in high-altitude expeditions, wilderness retreats, and extreme sports experiences. Their trips were exceptional—they'd been named in National Geographic's "Top 10 Adventure Operators" twice—but their brand looked like a 2005 outdoor gear catalog.

Founder and CEO, Marcus Redwing: "We're selling transformative experiences in some of the world's most beautiful places, and our brand looks like it was designed by committee. We need a brand that matches the quality of what we deliver."

**Key Problems:**
- Brand perceived as "generic outdoor company"
- Website had 3.2% conversion rate (industry benchmark: 5.8%)
- Social media engagement 4x below category leaders
- Unable to attract premium brand partnerships
- Difficulty recruiting top-tier adventure guides
- Media kit hadn't been updated in 6 years

---

## Discovery & Research Phase

### Brand Audit (1 week)

Analyzed current brand assets:
- Logo and variations (7 versions in use)
- Color usage (no consistent palette)
- Typography (14 different fonts across materials)
- Photography style (inconsistent, mostly stock)
- Voice and tone (undefined)

**Findings:** The brand had evolved without strategy, resulting in visual noise that diluted recognition.

### Competitive Analysis (1 week)

Analyzed 20 competitors and aspirational brands:
- Adventure travel: Intrepid, Exodus, G Adventures
- Luxury outdoor: Abercrombie & Kent, &Beyond, Wilderness Collective
- Lifestyle outdoor: Patagonia, Arc'teryx, The North Face
- Experience luxury: Four Seasons, Belmond, Aman

**Key insights:**
- Category leaders used bold, singular visual identities
- Luxury adventure brands favored sophisticated minimalism
- Successful brands had distinct color territories (Patagonia = navy, Arc'teryx = orange)
- Photography was consistently premium and ownable

### Stakeholder Interviews

Conducted 8 interviews with:
- Founding team (Marcus, COO, CFO)
- Lead adventure guides (3)
- Past customers (4, carefully selected for diversity of experience)

**Key insight from customers:** "Altitude trips changed my life—but I wouldn't know that from their brand. It looks like every other outdoor company."

**Key insight from guides:** "We attract clients who want comfort AND adventure. The current brand undersells the luxury element."

### Positioning Workshop

Led 2-day positioning workshop with founding team to define:

**Brand Purpose:** "We don't sell trips. We sell transformation."

**Brand Vision:** "To become the definitive brand for the adventurous at heart who refuse to compromise on comfort."

**Brand Personality:** Confident, refined, daring, authentic, aspirational

**Brand Pillars:**
1. Expertise (15 years, zero fatalities, certified guides)
2. Exclusivity (small groups, premium access, limited availability)
3. Experience (every detail curated, transformative outcomes)

## Design Strategy

### Creative Direction

Based on research, defined three potential creative territories:

**Option A: "Rugged Luxury"**  
Raw, textured, premium materials aesthetic. Think leather, canvas, weathered metal.

**Option B: "Alpine Modern"**  
Clean, geometric, Scandinavian-inspired minimalism meets mountain imagery.

**Option C: "Golden Hour"**  
Warm, cinematic, aspirational photography with sophisticated typography.

**Selected direction:** Option B — "Alpine Modern"  
**Rationale:** Tested 3x better with target audience. Modern luxury resonates with the 35-55 demographic who has disposable income and sense of adventure.

### Design Principles

**1. Singularity**  
Every element should feel intentional and considered. No visual clutter.

**2. Sophistication**  
This is luxury adventure, not REI. Every touch should feel premium.

**3. Courage**  
Bold decisions over safe ones. A brand this exclusive shouldn't look like everyone else.

**4. Timelessness**  
Trends fade. We design for longevity.

## Design Process

### Logo Design (3 weeks)

Created 5 logo concepts across two directions:

**Concept Set 1: Wordmark-focused**
- Clean, custom letterforms with subtle mountain geometry
- Minimal, versatile, works at any size

**Concept Set 2: Symbol + Wordmark**
- Abstract "A" with summit line motif
- Distinctive, ownable, works as standalone icon

**Selected concept:** Modified Symbol + Wordmark  
**Rationale:** Provides flexibility (symbol alone for app/icon use, wordmark for partnerships)

**Logo variations developed:**
- Primary (full color)
- Primary (reversed)
- Icon (symbol only)
- Monochrome
- Minimum size specifications

### Color System (1 week)

**Primary Palette:**
- Summit White: #F8F6F3 (backgrounds, clean spaces)
- Altitude Black: #1A1A1A (text, grounding)
- Golden Hour: #C9A962 (premium accent, warmth)
- Glacial Blue: #7BA3B8 (supporting accent, mountain/water)
- Granite: #6B6B6B (secondary text)

**Extended Palette:**
- Dawn: #E8D5C4
- Dusk: #3D4A5C
- Summit Gold: #B8860B

**Color Testing:** All combinations tested for accessibility (WCAG AA minimum)

### Typography (1 week)

**Primary Typefaces:**
- **Headlines:** Canela (elegant serif with character)
- **Body:** Aktiv Grotesk (modern geometric sans, excellent legibility)
- **Accent:** Canela Italic (pull quotes, special moments)

**Type Scale:** 12/14/16/18/21/28/36/48/72/96px

**Usage Guidelines:**
- Headlines: Always in Canela
- Body: Always in Aktiv Grotesk
- Never: All caps, excessive tracking, mixing serif headlines with serif body

### Visual Language (2 weeks)

Developed comprehensive visual system:

**Photography Style Guide:**
- Golden hour lighting mandatory
- Landscapes: vast, empty, awe-inspiring
- People: small scale against nature, authentic moments
- Color grading: warm shadows, cool highlights
- Never: harsh midday light, crowded scenes, staged smiles

**Iconography:**
- Custom icon set (80 icons)
- Style: Line icons, 2px stroke, rounded caps
- Subject: Adventure activities, navigation, amenities

**Graphic Elements:**
- Summit line motif (abstract mountain range)
- Contour line patterns (for backgrounds, textures)
- Geometric shapes (circles, triangles for data viz)

**Motion Philosophy:**
- Slow, cinematic transitions (800ms+)
- Ease-in-out curves
- Parallax effects for depth
- Video: 24fps cinematic, drone footage prioritized

### Brand Applications (2 weeks)

Demonstrated system across key applications:
- Business cards
- Letterhead and stationery
- Website mockups
- Social media templates
- Email signatures
- Presentation templates
- Merchandise (tshirt, mug, duffel)
- Vehicle graphics
- Signage specifications

- Joint adventure experiences
- Co-branded content

## Lessons Learned

### What Worked

1. **Positioning workshop before design** — Defining "luxury adventure" clearly prevented endless subjective debates during design.

2. **Testing creative directions** — Presenting 3 options forced productive conversations and built consensus.

3. **Comprehensive guidelines** — The 48-page brand book gave the team confidence to implement consistently.

### What I'd Do Differently

1. **Plan for photography migration** — We underestimated the time and cost to replace 8 years of stock photography with on-brand imagery.

2. **Include motion guidelines earlier** — The team defaulted to old animation styles. Should have created motion specs alongside static guidelines.

3. **Longer handoff period** — Two weeks wasn't enough time to fully onboard the team. Would suggest 4-week structured handoff.
