# Nexus Freight — Talking Points

## For Sales Conversations

### Opening Hook
"Nexus Freight had 47 carrier integrations, 14 engineers dedicated to maintaining them, and $2.8M in annual costs. After 20 weeks, they have 67 integrations, 4 engineers, and $420K in annual costs. That's not an improvement — it's a transformation."

### Differentiator #1: Pattern-First Approach
"We spent 4 weeks analyzing integrations before writing code. We found 6 archetypes covering 90% of patterns. Most teams build edge cases first and wonder why their framework doesn't scale. We built to the common case and extended for edge cases cleanly."

### Differentiator #2: Contract-First Development
"We defined the unified API contract before building any adapter. This let Nexus's teams work in parallel and ensured the gateway was truly universal — not just 'works for our current carriers.'"

### Differentiator #3: Migration Safety
"We ran old and new systems side-by-side for 4 weeks. Every request validated against both systems. Traffic shifted 10% at a time. Zero downtime migration. Zero data loss."

### ROI Story
"They paid $447K. The annual savings: $2.38M. Payback: 2.4 months. After that, it's pure margin improvement. And their engineers — freed from integration maintenance — shipped 4x more product features in the following quarter."

## Objection Handlers

### "Our carriers have unique requirements. A standard framework won't work."
"We've worked with 47 carriers. Yes, some have unique requirements — but 90% of functionality follows 6 patterns. The framework handles the 90%. Adapters handle the 10% with clean extension points. This is how all successful integration platforms work — Stripe, Twilio, Plaid."

### "Migration will be risky. We can't afford downtime."
"We run parallel systems for 4 weeks. Every request validates against both. Traffic shifts 10% at a time. We've done this for 3 other logistics companies. Zero downtime migrations. Zero data loss."

### "Our team needs to learn a new framework. What about adoption?"
"We built the developer experience first. Adapter SDK with built-in testing and mock responses. Auto-generated documentation. Sandbox environment. 80% team adoption within 30 days of launch."
