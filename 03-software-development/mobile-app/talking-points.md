# MealMoxie — Talking Points

## For Sales Conversations

### Opening Hook
"MealMoxie needed to build iOS and Android apps in 12 weeks with $2.1M seed funding. We built both platforms with 70% shared code, launched a 150-user beta, and hit a 4.7 App Store rating. Six months later, they're processing $890K in monthly GMV."

### Differentiator #1: Code Sharing
"React Native isn't just 'write once, run anywhere' — it's 'write once, maintain everywhere.' When we fixed a checkout bug on Tuesday, it was fixed on both iOS and Android by Wednesday. That's 4 weeks of development time saved."

### Differentiator #2: Offline-First
"Corporate WiFi drops during board meetings, in basement conference rooms, and during power outages. We built offline order queueing — employees never lose an order. This became a key differentiator in sales conversations."

### Differentiator #3: Corporate Beta
"We didn't launch to the public on Week 12. We launched to 3 corporate partners with 150 employees who gave us 47 bugs and 12 UX improvements. By public launch, the app was polished."

### ROI Story
"They paid $193K. Building iOS and Android separately would have cost $280K+. That's $87K saved before they made a dollar. And we delivered 4 weeks faster than native development would have allowed."

## Objection Handlers

### "React Native performance isn't as good as native."
"For this use case — displaying restaurants, taking orders, showing maps — React Native is indistinguishable from native. We hit 60fps on all animations. The one exception: complex map interactions. We used native map components for those."

### "We need our app in the App Store in 8 weeks."
"Expo managed workflow let us submit to TestFlight in 8 weeks. App Store review took the additional 4 weeks. If you need public App Store in 8 weeks, we cut scope: remove the corporate dashboard (ship in Phase 2), simplify SSO."

### "How do you handle restaurant POS integrations?"
"Phase 1: No POS integration. Restaurants receive orders via our tablet app or direct API. Phase 2: Toast and Square integrations. POS integration complexity is underestimated by 3x by most teams."
