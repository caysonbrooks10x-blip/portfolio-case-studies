# FieldSync Case Study — Website Version

## How FieldSync Built a Field Service MVP That Contractors Actually Use

**Industry:** Field Service Management  
**Timeline:** 9 weeks  
**Stack:** Next.js, Node.js, PostgreSQL, Stripe Connect  

---

### The Problem

FieldSync's founders knew the field service industry was underserved. Legacy platforms charged $99-$299/month per user with interfaces that field techs hated. But building an MVP that could compete with entrenched players seemed impossible on a startup budget.

They had 12 months of runway and needed to launch before their seed funding ran out.

---

### Our Approach

We ran a 3-day discovery sprint with 4 contractor design partners. The insight that changed everything: **we rode along with field techs for full service days**.

That's when we saw it — a technician trying to type job notes on an iPad screen in a 100°F attic. Voice-to-text became a flagship feature. No competitor offered it.

**Technical execution:**
- Next.js 14 PWA for iPad compatibility (no App Store required)
- Supabase Realtime for live dispatch board updates
- Full Stripe Connect marketplace payments
- Offline-first mobile with IndexedDB sync
- 87% test coverage before launch

### What Made It Different

**Ruthless scope control.** We cut 12 planned features at Week 4 to protect the timeline. Gas tank tracking shipped in Phase 2.

**Performance obsession.** 94 Lighthouse score. 120ms API p95. 99.94% uptime over 6 months.

**Integration depth.** Full Stripe Connect with split payments, tipping, and refunds — not a shallow plugin.
