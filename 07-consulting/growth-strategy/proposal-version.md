# Thornfield Manufacturing: Business Growth Strategy Proposal

## Prepared for: Katherine Thornfield-Cortez, CEO, Thornfield Manufacturing

## Executive Summary

Thornfield Manufacturing has been trapped at $47M for five years—not because of market limitations, but because of strategic constraints. You're at 92% capacity, 68% concentrated in aerospace, and considering a $20M facility expansion that's neither necessary nor sufficient for growth.

The real growth opportunities are hiding in plain sight: 11% of your capacity is consumed by low-margin complexity work from customers who don't deserve that priority. Your scheduling efficiency leaves 8-10% capacity on the table. Your market concentration limits growth to aerospace cycles. And you've never pursued medical devices, industrial equipment, or e-commerce—all growing faster than your current market.

We propose a 20-week engagement to develop and begin executing a comprehensive growth strategy that will grow revenue from $47M to $70M+ within 24 months without the $20M capital expansion you've been considering.

---

## Proposed Engagement

### Our Philosophy: Strategic Growth Through Asset Optimization

Most manufacturers try to grow by building more capacity—new facilities, new equipment, more headcount. This approach is expensive, risky, and often unnecessary.

Our approach is different: we help manufacturers grow by optimizing what they already have, then strategically expanding into adjacent markets. For Thornfield specifically:

- Free 11% capacity through scheduling optimization and changeover reduction (no capital required)
- Diversify from 68% aerospace to multiple market segments
- Launch e-commerce to capture underserved small-order customers
- Transform sales from relationship-based to systematic growth engine
