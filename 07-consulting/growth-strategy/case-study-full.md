# Case Study: Meridian Capital Partners — Business Growth Strategy

## Executive Summary

Meridian Capital Partners, a mid-market private equity firm managing $1.2B in assets, was sitting on a critical problem: portfolio company Velocite Systems was hemorrhaging market share despite strong underlying fundamentals. The leadership team knew they needed external expertise to diagnose root causes and architect a sustainable growth engine.

Over a 16-week engagement, I led a comprehensive growth strategy initiative that identified $2.1M in untapped revenue streams, rebuilt their go-to-market approach from the ground up, and implemented a data-driven targeting framework that drove a 47% improvement in qualified pipeline within two quarters.

## The Client

**Meridian Capital Partners** is a Boston-based private equity firm with 23 portfolio companies across healthcare technology, manufacturing, and business services sectors. Their investment thesis centers on operational excellence and organic growth acceleration.

**Portfolio Company: Velocite Systems** — A B2B SaaS provider of enterprise resource planning solutions for mid-market manufacturers. $18M ARR, 140 employees, 8-year operating history, 12% YoY decline in net new ARR.

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Marcus Chen, CEO of Velocite Systems (portfolio company)

## The Challenge

When I first stepped into Velocite's headquarters, the sales team was demoralized, the product team was building features no one was buying, and the marketing team was generating leads that never converted. The symptoms were everywhere, but the root causes were buried.

**Surface Issues:**
- Net new ARR had declined 12% YoY while the market grew 8%
- Sales cycle had extended from 67 days to 103 days average
- Win rate against competitors had dropped from 41% to 28%
- Customer churn had crept up from 8% to 14% annually
- Product development backlog was 18 months long with low-confidence priorities

**Deep Dive Findings (Week 1-4):**

Through 34 stakeholder interviews, competitive analysis, and data analysis across their CRM, support ticket system, and product usage analytics, I uncovered the real issues:

1. **Positioning Drift**: Velocite had slowly migrated from their differentiated position as the "manufacturer-first ERP" to generic "enterprise solution" messaging. Competitors like SAP and Oracle had碾压ed them on features; they lost the simplicity story that originally won customers.

2. **Ideal Customer Profile Misalignment**: 62% of their recent wins were from non-target accounts (too small, wrong industry vertical, insufficient technical infrastructure). These customers had higher implementation costs, lower retention, and negative reference value.

3. **Go-to-Market Fragmentation**: Three separate sales teams were operating independently with different playbooks, pricing discretion, and target markets. No shared pipeline visibility, no unified territory strategy.

4. **Product-Market Fit Erosion**: The product team had been building for enterprise requirements that their mid-market customers couldn't use. Usage data showed 73% of features were adopted by fewer than 10% of customers.

5. **Revenue Leakage**: Pricing analysis revealed 34% of deals were discounted beyond approved thresholds without proper business case documentation. The average discount had crept from 18% to 27%.

## My Approach

I structured the engagement in four distinct phases, each building toward a cohesive growth engine rather than isolated tactics.

### Phase 1: Strategic Diagnosis (Weeks 1-4)

**Activities:**
- 34 stakeholder interviews across sales, marketing, product, customer success, and executive leadership
- Full CRM data analysis (3 years of deal history, 2,400+ opportunities)
- Competitive landscape mapping (8 major competitors, 47 differentiating factors)
- Customer profitability analysis (187 current customers)
- Product usage analytics deep-dive (feature adoption rates, engagement patterns)
- 12 customer reference interviews (6 NPS 9-10, 6 NPS 4-6)

**Deliverables:**
- Root Cause Analysis Report (47 pages)
- Competitive Positioning Blueprint
- Ideal Customer Profile Framework with scoring model
- Revenue Leakage Assessment

### Phase 2: Strategy Development (Weeks 5-8)

I facilitated a structured strategy development process with the executive team, ensuring alignment and commitment to the chosen direction.

**Framework: 3 Horizons Growth Model**

Horizon 1 (0-12 months): Core business optimization
- ICP refinement and sales territory redesign
- Pricing governance and discount controls
- Messaging overhaul and sales enablement

Horizon 2 (12-24 months): Adjacent market expansion
- New vertical expansion strategy (distribution/logistics)
- Partnership channel development
- Product bundle strategy

Horizon 3 (24-36 months): Transformational growth
- Platform ecosystem strategy
- M&A add-on acquisition criteria
- International market entry assessment

**Strategic Pillars:**
1. **Reclaim "Manufacturer-First" Positioning** — Dominate the mid-market manufacturing vertical where we have 8 years of domain expertise
2. **Built-Not-Sold Demand Generation** — Shift from reactive lead follow-up to proactive market development
3. **Revenue Intelligence** — Build data infrastructure for real-time pipeline management and forecast accuracy
4. **Customer Success as Growth Engine** — Turn satisfied customers into repeat buyers and references

### Phase 3: Implementation Planning (Weeks 9-12)

I developed detailed implementation blueprints for each strategic pillar, with clear ownership, milestones, and success metrics.

**Key Planning Outputs:**

## Key Lessons

**1. Data Without Context Is Just Numbers**
Velocite had all the data they needed—but no one was synthesizing it into actionable insights. The 47% win rate improvement came not from better data, but from better interpretation of existing data paired with structured interviews that revealed the "why" behind the numbers.

**2. Positioning Is a Discipline, Not a Department**
The drift happened gradually across marketing, sales, product, and executive communications. Fixing it required coordinating all four—not handing off to marketing to "redo the website."

**3. Sales Process Uniformity Enables Scale**
The three fragmented sales teams had developed their own cultures, processes, and "best practices" that were actually holding the company back. Standardization wasn't about control—it was about creating shared visibility and enabling management by exception.

**4. Customer Success Is the Most Underrated Growth Lever**
When I showed the executive team the 4.2x lifetime value difference between NPS 9-10 customers and NPS 7-8 customers, the investment in customer success resources became obvious rather than contentious.
