# Startup Validation: Objection Handling

## "Our product works. We just need better sales."
**Response:** "Vela's product worked—87% burnout prediction accuracy. But 18 months of poor sales proved the market didn't want it the way it was being sold. Product-worked + no-traction = market problem, not sales problem. The pivot to platform distribution found buyers who wanted the technology."

## "Platform distribution takes years to build."
**Response:** "It doesn't have to. Vela signed 4 platform partners in 6 months by targeting HR tech platforms that needed burnout intelligence as a feature. The key: finding platforms with existing distribution and embedding Vela's API into their workflow."

## "Our technology is our moat. We can't give it away via API."
**Response:** "API access isn't giving it away—it's distribution. Vela's moat was the AI, not the UI. By embedding in platforms, they got distribution without building a sales force. API usage fees + platform licensing = sustainable model."

## "We've already pivoted once. We can't pivot again."
**Response:** "The first pivot (from wellness app to burnout prediction) didn't work. The second pivot (from direct-to-employer to platform) did. Waiting in a dying model isn't caution—it's suicide. Four months of runway left."

## Closing: "You have 4 months of runway. The direct model isn't working. Let's validate the platform pivot before you run out of time."
