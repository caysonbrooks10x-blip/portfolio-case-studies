# Operations Optimization: Talking Points

## Opening Hook
"Granitepeak Manufacturing had been in business for 62 years. They had skilled workers, loyal customers, and a reputation for quality. But they were bleeding money from hidden inefficiencies that had accumulated over decades. Twenty weeks later, we had saved them $2.8M annually—and built a company that could compete for the next generation."

---

## Problem Framing

**The Surface Issues:**
"Gross margins declining 1.2% annually for 5 years. Rising competition from overseas suppliers with 40% cost advantages. On-time delivery dropped to 78%. Customer complaints up 34%. The leadership team was wondering if the company could survive the next decade."

**What Was Actually Wrong:**

**Production Scheduling Chaos:**
"Production scheduling was managed through spreadsheets, whiteboards, and tribal knowledge. Each supervisor had their own system. When I mapped the actual production flow, I found that 47% of jobs experienced waiting time between operations. Actual processing time was only 34% of total cycle time—the rest was wait time."

**Hidden Rework Loop:**
"Quality was supposed to be Granitepeak's competitive advantage. But I discovered that 8.4% of production hours were devoted to rework. Each incident cost $340. It wasn't flagged as a problem because it had always been there. The rework was 'normalized'—$890K annually was just part of the cost structure."

**Inventory Crisis:**
"The warehouse looked like a museum of manufacturing. Raw materials dating back 5+ years. Work-in-progress for cancelled jobs. 23% of inventory was excess (no usage in 12+ months). 12% was obsolete. They were carrying $380K annually in inventory carrying costs."

**ERP Limitations:**
"The 20-year-old AS/400 system couldn't provide real-time tracking. Inventory accuracy was 73%—meaning 27% of records were wrong. Integration with newer systems required manual data entry. It was a significant constraint, but the ERP wasn't the problem—the operations around it were."

---

## Approach Talking Points

**Philosophy: Operational Excellence Is a System:**
"Individual tactics—new software, better scheduling, improved quality control—don't create lasting operational excellence. These are components that must be integrated into a coherent operating system. Our approach treats operations as an interconnected system where improving one element creates ripple effects across the whole."

**Phase 1: Diagnosis:**
"Three weeks of direct observation. 42 employee interviews. Data analysis across production records, inventory data, quality metrics. We didn't assume we knew what was wrong—we let the data tell us."

**3 Pillars Framework:**
"We structured the strategy around 3 Pillars: Flow (continuous, optimized production), Quality (built into the process), and Agility (rapid response to changes). These aren't independent initiatives—they're an integrated system."

**Phase 3: Implementation:**
"We didn't just design solutions and hand them off. We implemented them alongside the team. Visual management scheduling. SMED changeover procedures. Poka-yoke fixtures. Daily standup meetings. Every change was practical and sustainable."

---

## Results Talking Points

**Production Performance:**
"Cycle time dropped from 23 days to 17.7 days—a 23% reduction. On-time delivery jumped from 78% to 96%. First-pass yield improved from 89% to 97%. Changeover time dropped from 4.2 hours to 1.8 hours."

**Financial Impact:**
"We delivered $2.8M in annual cost savings. Operating margin improved from 11% to 17%—a 6-point improvement. Inventory carrying costs cut in half. Quality-related costs reduced 76%."

**Inventory Transformation:**
"WIP inventory dropped from $4.2M to $2.8M—a 34% reduction. Excess and obsolete inventory went from $2.1M to $280K. Inventory accuracy improved from 73% to 96%. That's $1.4M in working capital released."

**Customer Impact:**
"On-time delivery improvement directly impacted customer relationships. Quality complaints dropped 74%. Customer NPS improved from 34 to 61. Customer retention improved from 89% to 97%."

**Employee Impact:**
"Supervisor turnover dropped from 18% to 6%. Employee engagement scores improved 24%. We promoted 11 people internally—up from 4 per year. Safety incidents dropped from 7 to 2 annually."

---

## ROI Talking Points

"$145,000 fixed-fee investment. $2.8M in annual savings. That's a 19x return. The engagement paid for itself within 8 months.

But the real value is the $1.4M in working capital released from inventory reduction. That's cash they can reinvest in the business."

## Objection Handlers

**"We're different from big manufacturers. This won't work for us."**
"Granitepeak was a third-generation family business in Grand Rapids, Michigan. 127 employees. Not a massive corporation. The principles of operational excellence—reduce waste, build quality in, optimize flow—apply regardless of size. We adapted every tool to their context."

**"We've tried lean before and it didn't stick."**
"What typically fails is implementing tactics without building the underlying system. You do 5S, but no one maintains it. You create a Kanban board, but it falls out of date. The difference at Granitepeak was that we invested as much in capability building—training, culture, succession planning—as we did in the operational changes themselves."

**"Our workers won't accept this kind of change."**
"That's a valid concern. And it's why we invested so heavily in change management. We interviewed 42 employees before designing anything. We identified champions at every level. We tied improvements to employee benefits—safety, engagement, career development. The result was that supervisor turnover dropped from 18% to 6% because people felt heard and valued."

**"This sounds like a lot of disruption for our operations."**
"I won't pretend it was seamless. There were difficult conversations. Some longtime supervisors had to change how they'd been doing things for 20 years. But we structured the implementation to minimize disruption—small wins first, quick successes to build momentum, training before changes went live. By week 12, the daily standup meetings were being run by the team without any prompting from me."
