# Operations Optimization: Talking Points

## Opening Hook
"Velocity Logistics had a 99.2% on-time delivery rate and loyal clients—but margins were compressing from 24% to 18% over five years. They were firefighting instead of optimizing. Sixteen weeks later, gross margins were 23%, operational costs down 50%, and on-time delivery had actually improved to 99.6%. That's not incremental improvement—it's transformation."

---

## Problem Framing

**The Surface Issues:**
"Margins compressed from 24% to 18% over five years. Operational costs growing faster than revenue. Five different WMS systems across four distribution centers. No standard processes. 38% annual turnover. No real-time visibility into operations."

**What Was Actually Wrong:**

**Fragmentation:**
"Each of the four distribution centers had developed its own way of doing things. DC1 had great receiving but poor put-away. DC2 had excellent pick efficiency but terrible shipping accuracy. DC3 was the opposite. DC4 was mediocre across the board. This wasn't just inefficiency—it was invisible waste."

**Technology Silos:**
"Velocity had grown through acquisition, and each acquisition brought its own WMS. Five different systems meant no real-time visibility, manual data re-entry, inconsistent reporting, and higher IT costs. They had data—it was just trapped in silos."

**Reactive Operations:**
"The operations team was constantly firefighting. They'd receive a client complaint, scramble to fix it, then move on to the next fire. No root cause analysis. No preventive measures. Small problems became big problems: a 0.5% shipping error rate at their volume meant 12,000 errors per month."

---

## Approach Talking Points

**Philosophy: Operational Excellence Is Not About Working Harder:**
"Most operations leaders think optimization means cutting costs or squeezing more out of the same processes. This fails because it treats symptoms, not causes. Our approach: eliminate waste, standardize processes, leverage technology, build continuous improvement. Lower costs AND better performance."

**Four Workstreams:**
"We designed the program around four workstreams: SOPs, technology consolidation, labor optimization, and quality program. Each addresses a specific root cause."

**SOP Standardization:**
"We developed SOPs for all core processes—receiving, put-away, pick, pack, ship, returns. This wasn't about restricting creativity. It was about creating a baseline that could be measured, compared, and improved. Once you can measure, you can improve."

**Technology Consolidation:**
"We consolidated five WMS platforms into one Manhattan Associates platform. But here's the key: we standardized processes BEFORE consolidating technology. If we'd consolidated first, we would have just automated inefficiency."

**Labor Optimization:**
"We combined clear productivity standards with incentive pay, cross-training, and career development. Productivity increased 31% while turnover decreased 17 points. That's not cutting headcount—that's engaging employees."

---

## Results Talking Points

**Financial Performance:**
"Gross margins from 18% to 23%—up 5 points. Operational costs from $8.4M to $4.2M—down 50%. EBITDA from $12.8M to $22.6M—up 77%. Cost per order from $4.82 to $3.28—down 32%."

**Operational Performance:**
"On-time delivery from 99.2% to 99.6%—improved! Shipping accuracy from 99.5% to 99.92%. Order cycle time from 4.2 hours to 2.8 hours—down 33%. Pick productivity from 85 to 118 units per hour—up 39%."

**Labor Performance:**
"Labor cost from 22% to 17% of revenue—down 5 points. Turnover from 38% to 21%—down 17 points. Overtime from 14% to 7% of labor—down 7 points. Cross-trained employees from 12% to 68%—up 56 points."

**Technology Performance:**
"WMS systems from 5 to 1—consolidated. IT maintenance cost from $1.8M to $620K—down 66%. Data visibility from 34% to 96%—up 62 points. System downtime from 18 hours per month to 2 hours—down 89%."

---

## ROI Talking Points

"$185,000 fixed-fee investment. EBITDA improved from $12.8M to $22.6M—$9.8M more annually. That's a 53x return on investment. The engagement paid for itself within the first 8 months."

## Objection Handlers

**"Our operations are too complex for standardization."**
"Velocity had four DCs, 2.4M square feet, 420 employees, and five different WMS systems. If that's not too complex, your operation probably isn't either."

**"Our employees won't accept new processes."**
"At Velocity, we involved employees in designing the new processes. We gave them cross-training, career paths, and incentive pay. Turnover went from 38% to 21%—employees were happier because they had clear standards and growth opportunities."

**"Technology consolidation is too risky."**
"We piloted at DC1 in Atlanta, then rolled out in phases. If there were issues, we caught them early. By the time we got to DC4, we had refined the process three times. The risk was managed—and the results justified it."
