# Granitepeak Manufacturing: Operations Optimization Proposal

## Prepared for: Richard Hammersley, CEO & Leadership Team, Granitepeak Manufacturing

## Executive Summary

Granitepeak Manufacturing is at an inflection point. Sixty-two years of operational excellence built your reputation—but decades of accumulated inefficiencies are now compressing margins and threatening your competitive position. Rising material costs, overseas competition, and an aging ERP system are real challenges. But so is the opportunity to transform your operations from a liability into a competitive advantage.

We propose a 20-week operations optimization engagement designed to:
1. **Diagnose** the root causes of operational inefficiencies
2. **Design** a future-state operating model that drives margin improvement
3. **Implement** targeted improvements with measurable impact
4. **Sustain** gains through capability building and culture change

This engagement will deliver $2.5-3.0M in annual cost savings—primarily through inventory reduction, quality cost elimination, and production efficiency gains.

---

## Proposed Engagement

### Our Philosophy: Operational Excellence Is a System

Individual tactics—new software, better scheduling, improved quality control—don't create lasting operational excellence. These are components that must be integrated into a coherent operating system. Our approach treats operations as an interconnected system where improving one element creates ripple effects across the whole.

---

### Phase 1: Operational Diagnosis (Weeks 1-4) — $28,000

**Objective:** Develop a comprehensive understanding of current-state operations and identify the highest-impact improvement opportunities.

**Activities:**
- 3 weeks of direct observation (production floor, warehouse, office)
- Process mapping of 12 critical workflows
- Data collection: production records, inventory data, quality metrics, financial records
- Employee interviews (40+ across all levels)
- Supplier and customer interviews (20 total)
- Competitive benchmarking against 6 peer companies

**Deliverables:**
- Operational Assessment Report (80+ pages)
- Process Value Stream Maps (current state)
- Root Cause Analysis (5 whys for each major issue)
- Benchmark Comparison Report
- Quick Wins Opportunity Assessment (top 10 opportunities)

**Success Criteria:** Documented agreement on top 5 operational challenges and highest-impact opportunities

---

### Phase 2: Operations Strategy (Weeks 5-8) — $32,000

**Objective:** Design the future-state operating model and implementation roadmap.

**Activities:**
- Strategy workshop with leadership team (2 days)
- Future-state process design
- Technology requirements definition
- Change management planning
- Investment analysis and ROI modeling
- Risk assessment and mitigation planning

**Strategic Framework: The 3 Pillars of Operational Excellence**

1. **Flow** — Create continuous, optimized production flow
   - Reduce wait times and bottlenecks
   - Standardize changeover procedures
   - Optimize production scheduling

2. **Quality** — Build quality into the process
   - Shift from inspection to prevention
   - Implement visual quality standards
   - Reduce rework and waste

3. **Agility** — Enable rapid response to changes
   - Scheduling flexibility
   - Inventory optimization
   - Supplier management

**Deliverables:**
- Operations Strategy Document (3-year view)
- Future-State Process Designs
- Technology Roadmap
- Change Management Plan
- Investment and ROI Model ($2.5-3.0M projected savings)
- Implementation Roadmap (prioritized)

**Success Criteria:** Leadership alignment on strategic direction and investment priorities

---

### Phase 3: Implementation (Weeks 9-16) — $58,000

**Objective:** Execute operational improvements with hands-on support.

**Activities:**

**Flow Optimization:**
- Implement visual management scheduling system
- SMED (single-minute exchange of dies) changeover program
- Production cell organization
- Daily standup meeting cadence

**Quality Transformation:**
- Visual quality standards at each workstation
- Poka-yoke fixtures for critical quality points
- Statistical process control implementation
- First-pass yield improvement program

**Inventory Optimization:**
- Excess/obsolete inventory liquidation
- Cycle counting procedures
- Reorder point optimization
- Supplier JIT program

**Deliverables:**
- New scheduling system (including training)
- Quality management procedures
- Inventory management procedures
- KPI dashboards (real-time visibility)
- Training materials

**Success Criteria:** Measurable improvements in cycle time, on-time delivery, first-pass yield, and inventory levels

---

### Phase 4: Sustainability (Weeks 17-20) — $27,000

**Objective:** Build internal capabilities for continuous improvement.

**Activities:**
- Lean manufacturing training (40 hours per supervisor)
- Kaizen event facilitation (5 events across departments)
- New hire onboarding process redesign
- Performance management system integration
- Succession planning for key roles

**Deliverables:**
- Continuous Improvement Program
- Supervisor training curriculum
- Knowledge transfer documentation
- Succession planning framework
- 90-day transition plan

**Success Criteria:** Internal team capable of driving continuous improvement independently
