# Case Study: AI-Assisted Video Production Pipeline for Meridian Tech

## Client Overview

**Client:** Meridian Tech — a B2B SaaS company offering project management software for construction firms. Their marketing team consisted of 3 people: a content manager, a video editor, and a motion graphics designer. They had an ambitious content calendar that called for 12 videos per month — product demos, tutorial series, customer testimonials, and thought leadership content — but their production capacity was only 4-5 videos monthly. The gap was killing their go-to-market strategy for a new product launch.

**Challenge:** Meridian needed to scale video production by 3x without tripling their team or budget. They were aware of AI video tools but skeptical about quality. Their core concerns: Could AI handle the editing? Could it maintain brand consistency? Would the output feel "robotic"? They needed a production pipeline that leveraged AI for the mechanical work while preserving the creative and strategic elements that required human judgment.

**Objective:** Build an AI-assisted video production pipeline that would increase monthly output from 4-5 videos to 12-15 videos, reduce production cost per video from $3,200 to under $1,200, maintain 95%+ brand consistency, and enable the team to produce at scale without hiring additional headcount.

## The Problem with Their Previous Approach

Before our engagement, Meridian's production workflow was:

- **Script writing:** Content manager, 6-8 hours per video
- **Filming:** External crew, $800-1,200 per shoot day
- **Editing:** In-house editor, 12-16 hours per video
- **Motion graphics:** Motion designer, 4-6 hours per video
- **Revisions:** Multiple rounds, adding 40% to timeline
- **Total cost:** $3,200 per finished video at 4-5/month

The bottleneck wasn't creativity — it was the mechanical repetition: transcription, rough cut assembly, caption generation, multi-format export, and revision tracking. These tasks consumed 60% of production time but required minimal creative judgment.

## Our Approach: The Hybrid Intelligence Pipeline

### Phase 1: AI Audit & Integration (Weeks 1-2)

We evaluated Meridian's workflow to identify AI augmentation opportunities:

**Tasks Suitable for AI:**
1. **Transcription & Captioning** — 2 hours → 10 minutes
2. **Rough Cut Assembly** — 4 hours → 30 minutes
3. **Multi-Format Export** — 1 hour → 10 minutes
4. **Metadata Generation** — 30 minutes → 5 minutes
5. **Content Summarization** — 1 hour → 10 minutes
6. **Revision Comparison** — 1 hour → 5 minutes

**Tasks Requiring Human Judgment:**
1. Creative direction and concept approval
2. Script writing and narrative structure
3. Talent direction and interview skills
4. Brand consistency oversight
5. Final quality approval

**AI Tool Evaluation:**
We tested and implemented:
- **Runway Gen-2** — B-roll generation and background removal
- **Descript** — Transcript-based editing, overdub, caption generation
- **ElevenLabs** — AI voiceovers for tutorials and localizations
- **Pictory** — AI video summarization from long-form
- **Camtasia** — AI-assisted screen recording editing
- **Canva** — AI-powered design suggestions
- **ChatGPT** — Script outlining and content structuring

### Phase 2: Pipeline Construction (Weeks 3-5)

We built the integrated AI-assisted production pipeline:

**Step 1: AI Pre-Production (Week 3)**
- **ChatGPT:** Content brief generation, outline creation
- **Canva AI:** Thumbnail concept generation, design variations
- **Output:** 50% reduction in pre-production time

**Step 2: AI-Assisted Filming (Week 4)**
- **Runway:** Background removal, B-roll generation for stock gaps
- **ElevenLabs:** AI voiceovers for internal/process videos
- **Output:** Reduced external shoot requirements by 40%

**Step 3: AI-Powered Post-Production (Week 5)**
- **Descript:** Auto-transcription, rough cut from transcript, overdub corrections
- **Pictory:** Automated highlight extraction for social clips
- **CapCut:** AI-powered captioning and B-roll matching
- **Output:** 70% reduction in editing time

**Step 4: AI-Enhanced Distribution (Week 5)**
- **Descript:** Auto-chapters and key moments
- **Canva:** Multi-format adaptation (1:1, 9:16, 16:9)
- **ChatGPT:** Platform-specific caption optimization
- **Output:** 80% reduction in distribution prep time

### Phase 3: Optimization & Scaling (Weeks 6-8)

We refined the pipeline and established scaling protocols:

**Revision Workflow:**
- AI generates side-by-side comparison
- Human reviewer marks changes
- AI implements standard revisions
- Human final approval

**Quality Assurance:**
- AI flags potential issues (audio levels, visual consistency)
- Human reviewer validates flags
- Brand consistency checklist automated
- Final approval remains human

**Production Cadence:**
- Week 1: 4 videos completed (new peak)
- Week 4: 8 videos completed (2x previous)
- Week 8: 12 videos completed (3x baseline)

## Key Wins

**3x Production Volume:** By month 2, Meridian was producing 12 videos monthly — meeting their ambitious content calendar for the first time. The new product launch campaign required 18 videos across platforms; the pipeline delivered with 3 weeks to spare.

**67% Cost Reduction:** Production cost per video dropped from $3,200 to $1,050. At 12 videos/month, that's a monthly savings of $25,800 — or $309,600 annually.

**Human Creativity Amplified:** By removing mechanical tasks, the content manager spent more time on strategy, the editor focused on creative decisions rather than transcription, and the motion designer produced more polished graphics. Everyone worked at the top of their license.

**Multilingual Scaling:** ElevenLabs enabled rapid localization. A single tutorial video was produced in English, Spanish, and French for less than the cost of one traditionally localized video. This opened new markets without proportional production investment.

**Social Clip Generation:** Pictory automatically generated 3-5 short-form clips from each long-form video. What previously required a dedicated social media manager now happened automatically, driving 4x more social content volume.

## Lessons Learned

1. **AI augments, doesn't replace.** The best results came from human-AI collaboration — AI handling mechanical tasks, humans providing creative direction. Pure AI output still required human refinement.

2. **Transcription is the foundation.** Descript's transcript-based editing transformed the editing workflow. Once the transcript was correct, rough cut assembly took minutes instead of hours.

3. **Brand guidelines must be codified.** AI tools need explicit instructions. We spent extra time documenting Meridian's brand standards — which actually improved human collaboration too.

4. **Quality monitoring is essential.** AI-generated content required human review for accuracy, brand alignment, and messaging. We built QA checkpoints at every AI handoff.

5. **Localization is AI's killer use case.** ElevenLabs voice cloning enabled rapid multilingual production at a fraction of traditional cost. This opened markets that weren't economically viable before.
