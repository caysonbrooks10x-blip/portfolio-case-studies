## Objection Handlers

**"Our product is too complex for simple ads"**
Response: "Complex products need simple hooks. We strip the complexity in the hook, then deliver the details in the body. The first 3 seconds should create curiosity, not explain everything. We have frameworks for every product category."

**"Our audience doesn't respond to video ads"**
Response: "Your audience responds to relevant messages. If video ads aren't working, it's because the creative isn't relevant — not because the format is wrong. We're seeing 2.8% CTR on video for supplement brands, where static gets 1.2%. The format works when the creative is right."

**"We don't have anyone who can be on camera"**
Response: "We have multiple formats that don't require on-camera talent: product close-ups, screen recordings, text overlays, voiceover-only. And we have a UGC collection system that puts real customers on camera for you — which actually converts better."

**"Our legal team won't approve fast-paced editing"**
Response: "We design templates that meet compliance requirements. The pacing system works within any legal framework — it's about hook structure and cut frequency, not misleading claims. We've worked with legal teams before."

## Questions Prospects Ask

**"How long does each video take?"**
With our templates and batch production system, a single creative sprint (8-12 variants) takes 4 hours total. That's 20-30 minutes per variant — significantly faster than traditional production.

**"What equipment do we need?"**
We provide a complete equipment list. Most brands already have smartphones. The full setup (gimbal, lights, audio) is under $2,000 and pays for itself after 2-3 agency-produced videos.

**"Can we maintain quality at this speed?"**
Yes — because we templatize the structure and focus production effort on the elements that matter (hook, product shots, CTAs). Speed comes from efficiency, not shortcuts.

**"How do we manage legal review?"**
We build a compliance checkpoint into the workflow. All templates pre-approved, final review at the end. The bottleneck is usually approval, not production — we address both.
