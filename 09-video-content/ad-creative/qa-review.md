## Post-Production QA

### Hook Quality (First 3 Seconds)
- [ ] Immediate visual interest
- [ ] Clear product or message
- [ ] Text readable (48pt+ on mobile)
- [ ] Audio hook present (music or voice)
- [ ] No blank/black frames at start
- [ ] Viewer hooked by 3 seconds

### Visual Quality
- [ ] Color grading consistent with brand
- [ ] No pixelation or compression artifacts
- [ ] Text overlays positioned correctly
- [ ] Logo/watermark visible but not intrusive
- [ ] Safe zone respected (no cut-off text)
- [ ] Transitions smooth and intentional

### Audio Quality
- [ ] Voice/music at appropriate levels
- [ ] No distortion or clipping
- [ ] Captions accurate and synced
- [ ] Trending audio properly credited
- [ ] Legal/disclaimer audio audible if required

### CTA Quality
- [ ] CTA visible and clear
- [ ] Action requested is specific
- [ ] Urgency elements present (where appropriate)
- [ ] Link/ destination correct
- [ ] Matches ad destination page

---

## Format-Specific QA

### 1:1 Square (Feed)
- [ ] 1080×1080 resolution
- [ ] Subject in center 80%
- [ ] Text in upper 60%
- [ ] CTA in lower 20%
- [ ] Safe for all device previews

### 4:5 Portrait (In-Feed)
- [ ] 1080×1350 resolution
- [ ] Subject in center 85%
- [ ] Key text in upper 50%
- [ ] Works as both feed and potential Reels
- [ ] Full frame utilized

### 9:16 Stories/Reels
- [ ] 1080×1920 resolution
- [ ] Key content in center 70%
- [ ] No text/logos at extreme edges
- [ ] 10-15 second duration (Stories)
- [ ] Vertical format throughout

## Post-Project Lessons

1. **Hook testing should be the first priority.** Every hour spent testing hooks is worth 10 hours optimizing anything else.

2. **UGC collection needs proactive outreach.** Waiting for customers to submit doesn't work. We built an outreach system that increased UGC submissions by 400%.

3. **Format allocation should be data-driven.** Initially guessed 1:1 would win. Data showed 4:5 outperformed by 34%. Always test, don't assume.

4. **Legal review was bottleneck.** Built pre-approval for templates, saving 2-3 days per batch.

5. **Refresh cadence was too aggressive initially.** Killing creatives at 2 weeks was premature. Extended to 4-6 weeks with hook-only refresh.
