## Post 4: The Hook System
Most YouTubers start videos like this:
"Thanks for clicking! Today I'm going to talk about..."
STOP.
Start with THIS instead:
"Before I show you this workout, I need to warn you — 90% of people do this one thing..."
That's a retention hook. Use it.

---

## Post 5: The Cut Rule
If a shot is on screen for more than 5 seconds, you're losing someone.
Every 3-5 seconds: a new cut, a zoom, a B-roll insert, something.
Your editing software has a "cut" button. Use it.

## Post 7: The Chapter System
YouTube auto-generates chapters for videos with timestamps.
Videos WITH chapters retain 18% more viewers.
Add chapter markers. It's free algorithmic real estate.

---

## Post 8: B-Roll is Not Optional
Every 45-60 seconds, show something different.
Your face talking for 10 minutes straight isn't a video. It's a podcast with a static image.
Show me the workout. Show me the results. Show me something.

---

## Post 9: CTA Timing
Ask for subscriptions at these moments:
→ Right after a value bomb
→ At the natural end of a section
→ When you've surprised them
→ NEVER at the very beginning (they haven't decided to stay yet)
