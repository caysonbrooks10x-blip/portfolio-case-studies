## Visual Identity System

## Content Type Visual Specifications

### 1. Carousel Posts (Primary Format)
**Structure:** 8-10 slides
**Dimensions:** 1080×1080px (1:1) for LinkedIn
**File:** PDF or PNG sequence

**Slide Structure:**
- **Slide 1:** Title slide (bold, high contrast, topic clear)
- **Slides 2-8:** Content slides (one point per slide)
- **Slide 9:** Key takeaway
- **Slide 10:** CTA (follow, subscribe, or question)

**Design Templates:**
- **Template A:** Numbered list format (1, 2, 3...)
- **Template B:** Framework format (visual diagram)
- **Template C:** Before/After comparison
- **Template D:** Case study structure
- **Template E:** Step-by-step guide

**Visual Treatment:**
- Bold headlines (max 6 words)
- Supporting text (max 20 words per slide)
- Iconography for visual interest
- Consistent color blocks
- Professional, clean aesthetic

---

### 2. Short-Form Video (60-90 seconds)
**Platform:** LinkedIn Native Video
**Dimensions:** 1080×1920px (9:16) for vertical
**Format:** MP4, H.264
**File Size:** < 500MB

**Setup:**
- **Camera:** iPhone or professional camera
- **Angle:** Eye level or slightly above
- **Background:** Clean, professional (office or branded backdrop)
- **Lighting:** Natural or softbox, no harsh shadows
- **Audio:** Lavalier or shotgun mic, clean audio

**Content Structure:**
- **Hook (0-5 sec):** Bold statement or question
- **Core Insight (5-50 sec):** One main point with 2-3 supporting thoughts
- **CTA (50-60 sec):** "Follow for more" or question to drive comments

**On-Screen Elements:**
- Key text overlay (short phrases, not full sentences)
- Brand watermark (small, corner)
- Captions required (LinkedIn auto-caption + manual correction)

---

### 3. Quote Graphics
**Dimensions:** 1080×1080px (1:1)
**Format:** PNG or JPG
**Style:** Minimal, high contrast

**Design Options:**
- **Option A:** Photo background + white text overlay
- **Option B:** Solid brand color + white text
- **Option C:** Gradient background + text
- **Option D:** Text-only with subtle design element

**Requirements:**
- Quote: Short (under 20 words ideal)
- Attribution: Name + title below quote
- Readable at mobile size (48pt minimum)
- Subtle branding (logo or handle in corner)

---

### 4. Long-Form Posts
**Platform:** LinkedIn Native
**Length:** 800-1,500 words
**Structure:**
- Hook (first 2 lines, shown before "see more")
- Opening (context or question)
- Body (3-5 paragraphs, use line breaks)
- Closing (question or CTA)

**Formatting:**
- Use line breaks (never wall of text)
- Use emojis sparingly (1-3 per post)
- Add relevant hashtags at end (3-5)
- Tag people or companies where relevant

---

### 5. Commentary Posts
**Platform:** LinkedIn Native
**Length:** 100-300 words
**Structure:**
- React to news, question, or trend
- Provide quick opinion or insight
- End with question to drive engagement

**Style:**
- Conversational, informal
- Personal voice
- No formatting (raw text or minimal)

- First 2 lines: Hook (shown before "see more")

## Visual Templates

### Carousel Template Structure

```
Slide 1: Title
- Bold headline
- Topic identifier
- "Swipe to learn →" indicator

Slides 2-8: Content
- One point per slide
- Icon or visual for each point
- Supporting text (minimal)

Slide 9: Key Takeaway
- "The key insight: [one sentence]"

Slide 10: CTA
- "Follow for more insights on [topic]"
- Handle or logo
```

### Video Hook Templates

**Template 1: Controversial Statement**
"The conventional wisdom about [topic] is wrong. Here's why..."

**Template 2: Problem Agitation**
"Most [target audience] make this mistake when it comes to [topic]..."

**Template 3: Number Hook**
"There are [number] types of [topic]. Most people are type [X]..."

**Template 4: Story Hook**
"I made this mistake for 5 years. It cost me [consequence]..."

**Template 5: Promise Hook**
"In this video, I'll show you how to [outcome] without [pain point]..."

## Engagement Visuals

### Comment Strategy
- Add value with every comment
- Use paragraphs and occasional formatting
- Reference the post's content specifically
- Offer additional insight
- End with a question (drives further engagement)

### DM Strategy (Value-First)
- Never pitch in first message
- Reference something specific they posted
- Offer genuine insight or connection
- Build relationship before business
- "I'd love to learn more about [their thing]" → natural conversation
