# Talking Points: Founder Personal Brand Content System

## Core Value Proposition
We build personal brand content systems that translate your expertise into a thought leadership platform — generating inbound leads, speaking opportunities, and media coverage while you focus on running your business.

---

## Key Messages

**1. Expertise Without Platform Is Invisible**
- You have 10, 15, 20 years of experience
- Without a platform to share it, that expertise is invisible
- Your competitors are building audiences while you're doing outbound
- The question isn't whether to build a brand — it's whether to do it proactively or watch others do it

**2. Personal Branding Isn't About Becoming a Creator**
- You don't need to become a "content creator"
- You need a system that translates expertise into content
- The talent is already there — we build the machine
- 4-6 hours per week is all you need

**3. 80/20 Value Framework Works**
- 80% value: insights, frameworks, lessons, opinions
- 20% business: indirect, never "buy our services"
- This builds trust before you ever ask for anything
- When people need what you offer, they already know and trust you

**4. Consistency Beats Virality**
- 15-18 pieces per week beats one viral post
- Compound growth from consistent presence
- 2-3 week content bank ensures consistency
- The algorithm rewards consistent creators

**5. Video Humanizes Expertise**
- LinkedIn video converts followers to connections at 3x text rate
- Seeing and hearing you builds trust text can't
- You don't need to be polished — just genuine
- 60-90 seconds of insight is enough

## Objection Handlers

**"I'm not a natural content creator"**
Response: "Neither was Daniel Reyes. He spent 15 years doing operational work, not writing LinkedIn posts. What we built was a system — not a talent. The content isn't about being entertaining. It's about sharing expertise in a structured way. Daniel's 'natural' is explaining operational frameworks. That's what he does."

**"I don't have time"**
Response: "4-6 hours per week. That's the time investment. One content production day per week produces 15-18 pieces. The rest of the week is scheduling and engagement — which can be done in 30-minute blocks. If you don't have 4-6 hours, the question is whether inbound leads are worth 1% of your week."

**"I'll look desperate for attention"**
Response: "The 80/20 framework prevents that. 80% of your content is value — insights, frameworks, lessons. You're helping people, not begging for attention. The content that looks desperate is 'buy our services' posts. We're not doing those. We're building a reputation."

**"What if I say something wrong?"**
Response: "You're sharing perspective, not delivering absolute truth. Thought leadership means having opinions that not everyone agrees with. Some people will disagree. That's the point. If everyone agrees with you, you're not leading — you're following."

**"My industry doesn't work on LinkedIn"**
Response: "What industry has successfully built thought leadership on LinkedIn? Legal? Check. Financial? Check. Medical? Check. Construction? Check. B2B services? Double-check. The platform works for expertise. Your industry just needs the right content strategy."

## Questions Prospects Ask

**"What's the minimum time investment?"**
4-6 hours per week. One "content day" for production, 30 minutes daily for engagement. That's the system. More time accelerates results, but 4 hours maintains them.

**"What should I post about?"**
We identify 4-6 content pillars based on your expertise and audience. These become the categories you rotate through. We also build a topic generation system so you never face a blank page.

**"What if I'm not a good writer?"**
Writing isn't required. We have video formats, carousel formats, and quote formats that don't require long-form writing. Daniel wrote 3 long-form posts per week — and that was the format he was least comfortable with. The system adapts to your strengths.

**"How do I get my first 1,000 followers?"**
Consistency and engagement. Post valuable content consistently, engage with others' content, and connect with people in your target audience. The first 1,000 is the hardest — after that, momentum builds.

**"When will I see inbound leads?"**
Typically month 3-4 for initial leads. Month 5-6 for consistent pipeline. The first few months are about building audience. The leads come after the audience forms.
