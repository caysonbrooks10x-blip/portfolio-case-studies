# Case Study: Social Media Performance Marketing System
**Client:** Elevate Skincare | **Project Duration:** 6 Months | **Revenue From Social: 4.1x**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Elevate Skincare, a direct-to-consumer beauty brand selling premium skincare products, was posting content on Instagram and Facebook without a strategic system behind it. They had 28,000 followers, posted sporadically, and generated minimal direct revenue — attribution showed social driving less than 8% of online orders. After implementing a comprehensive social media performance marketing system over 6 months, we grew social-attributed revenue from $94K to $386K annually — a 4.1x increase — while reducing customer acquisition cost from $48 to $22. The system we built turned social from a brand awareness experiment into a predictable revenue channel.

---

## 2. Client Background

**Company:** Elevate Skincare
**Industry:** Direct-to-Consumer Beauty — Premium Skincare
**Founded:** 2019
**Headquarters:** Los Angeles, California
**Team Size:** 23 employees
**Annual Revenue:** $2.8M (at project start)

**Problem Statement:**
Elevate Skincare had built a decent following on Instagram (24K) and Facebook (4K) through product-focused posts and occasional giveaways, but social wasn't translating to meaningful revenue. The marketing team was creating content without a performance framework, posting inconsistently, and had no systematic approach to content testing, audience targeting, or social retargeting. The brand needed a repeatable system that could scale with the business.

---

## 3. The Problem

Elevate's social presence had several structural issues:

1. **No content strategy.** Posts were created ad-hoc without a documented framework for what content types drove purchases. The team didn't know whether educational content, product showcases, user-generated content, or influencer posts converted best.

2. **Zero paid amplification.** Despite organic reach declining on both platforms (as it had for most brands post-2022 algorithm changes), there was no budget for paid promotion of high-performing organic content.

3. **No retargeting from social.** Users who engaged with social content were never re-targeted via paid ads, leaving a significant portion of the customer journey unaddressed.

4. **Inconsistent posting and creative.** Without a content calendar or batch creation system, posting was sporadic. Creatives weren't being iterated based on performance — the same static product shots were recycled endlessly.

5. **No attribution model.** Social's contribution to revenue was essentially invisible. The brand knew social "brought in some customers" but couldn't quantify it or optimize for it.

## 4. Approach & Strategy

### Phase 1: Audit & Framework Design (Month 1)

We began with a comprehensive audit of Elevate's existing social presence:

**Organic Performance Analysis:**
- Reviewed 12 months of Instagram and Facebook insights data
- Analyzed engagement rates by content type (reels, carousels, static images, stories)
- Identified which posts drove website traffic (via UTM-tagged links)
- Assessed follower growth rate and audience demographics

**Competitive Landscape:**
- Analyzed 8 competitor social strategies (content types, posting frequency, engagement)
- Identified content gaps in Elevate's approach
- Documented what was working in the beauty DTC space

**Attribution Baseline:**
- Set up Meta Pixel andConversions API for proper social attribution
- Implemented UTMs on all social links
- Connected social data to GA4 and Klaviyo
- Established baseline attribution window (7-day click, 1-day view)

### Phase 2: Content System Architecture (Month 2)

We designed a content pillars framework that aligned with the customer journey:

**Content Pillars:**
1. **Education (30%)** — Skincare routines, ingredient explainers, sun protection tips. Top-of-funnel awareness.
2. **Product Showcases (25%)** — Individual product features, before/afters, formula highlights. Mid-funnel consideration.
3. **Social Proof (25%)** — Customer testimonials, reviews, UGC reposts. Trust building.
4. **Brand Story (10%)** — Founder story, behind-the-scenes, brand values. Emotional connection.
5. **Promotions & Launches (10%)** — Product drops, seasonal sales, exclusive offers. Conversion.

**Content Calendar:**
- 5 posts per week across Instagram and Facebook (same content, optimized for each platform)
- 3-5 stories per week
- 2-4 reels per month
- Batch content creation: 1 week of content created in a single 4-hour session

### Phase 3: Paid Amplification System (Months 3-4)

With organic content system running, we introduced paid amplification:

**Campaign Architecture:**
- **Campaign 1:** Paid amplification of top-performing organic posts (engagement campaigns)
- **Campaign 2:** Traffic campaigns driving to product pages
- **Campaign 3:** Retargeting campaigns for website visitors (via Meta Pixel)
- **Campaign 4:** Lookalike audiences based on purchasers and high-value engagers
- **Campaign 5:** Dynamic product ads (DPA) for cart abandoners

**Ad Creative Strategy:**
- 12 new ad creatives tested across audiences
- Video creatives (7-15 second product demos)
- Carousel creatives showcasing routines
- Single image ads for retargeting
- UGC-style ads (customer testimonials)

### Phase 4: UGC & Influencer Integration (Months 4-6)

We built a systematic approach to user-generated content and micro-influencer partnerships:

**UGC Program:**
- Created a branded hashtag (#ElevateYourSkin) and encouraged posting
- Monitored and saved tagged posts for permission-based reposting
- Built a UGC content library of 200+ approved posts
- Integrated UGC into ad creatives (testimonial-style ads)

**Micro-Influencer Program:**
- Identified 15 micro-influencers (10K-50K followers) in the skincare space
- Established gifting partnerships with discount codes for tracking
- Created an affiliate program for influencers with trackable links
- Generated 45 pieces of sponsored content over 6 months

## 6. Key Features & Tactics

**The 30-30-30 Rule for Content:**
We developed and implemented a content framework balancing three key objectives:
- 30% of content should educate (builds trust and authority)
- 30% should showcase products (drives consideration)
- 30% should provide social proof (reduces purchase anxiety)
- 10% brand/promotional (converts momentum)

**Batch Content Creation:**
Instead of creating content daily, we implemented a batch creation system:
- 1 four-hour session per week produces all content for the following week
- Templates built in Canva for rapid production
- Creative fatigue reduced by having 4-6 weeks of content queued

**Social-to-Email Pipeline:**
We built a systematic connection between social engagement and email marketing:
- Social followers segmented in Klaviyo by engagement level
- Targeted email sequences for highly engaged followers
- Lookalike audiences built from email purchasers

**Retargeting Architecture:**
- Cold audience → engagement campaigns (build familiarity)
- Engagers → traffic campaigns (drive to site)
- Website visitors → retargeting (convert)
- Cart abandoners → DPA (recover lost sales)
- Purchasers → upsell campaigns (increase LTV)

## 9. Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1: Declining Organic Reach**
Elevate, like many brands, had seen organic reach decline significantly in 2022-2023. Likes and reach were down, making it hard to justify the effort.
*Solution:* We reframed the goal from "maximize organic reach" to "use organic content to feed a paid amplification system." High-performing organic posts got paid boost, turning organic wins into scaled results. Organic became the creative testing ground; paid became the amplification engine.

**Challenge 2: Content Production Bottleneck**
The team was small (23 employees, limited marketing bandwidth) and couldn't sustain daily content creation without burning out.
*Solution:* Batch creation workflow. We redesigned their process so one person could produce a week's worth of content in a single session using templates. Canva templates for each content pillar made production fast and consistent.

**Challenge 3: Attribution Disputes**
Social was credited with only a fraction of actual influence because the attribution window was too narrow.
*Solution:* We implemented multi-touch attribution in Klaviyo that gave social credit across a 7-day click and 1-day view window. We also set up a post-purchase survey asking "How did you hear about us?" to validate attribution. Social's influence jumped from 8% to 24% of revenue once properly measured.

**Challenge 4: Influencer ROI Opacity**
Previous influencer partnerships had been untrackable — Elevate gave product, got posts, but couldn't tie them to revenue.
*Solution:* We established discount code tracking (each influencer got a unique code) and UTM-tagged links. Every piece of influencer content was trackable back to orders. This gave us real data on which influencers drove value, allowing us to renew (or end) partnerships based on actual performance.

## 10. Lessons Learned

1. **Organic and paid work best together.** Treating them as separate channels was Elevate's original mistake. High-performing organic content provides cheap creative testing; paid amplification scales the winners.

2. **Content systems beat heroic efforts.** Instead of relying on daily creative inspiration, we built a templated, batch production system that was sustainable and produced consistent results.

3. **UGC is the highest-converting ad creative.** User-generated content — real customers, real results — consistently outperformed professionally produced brand content in ad tests. Authenticity beat polish.

4. **Attribution determines optimization.** Before proper tracking, Elevate thought social wasn't working. After tracking, they saw it was driving 24% of revenue. You can't optimize what you can't measure.

5. **Micro-influencers outperform mega-influencers for DTC.** The 15 micro-influencers (10K-50K followers) drove more attributable revenue than any single mega-influencer would have at a fraction of the cost.

## 16. Replication Formula

This methodology applies to any DTC brand with an existing social following but no performance system:

1. **Audit** — Full review of existing content performance, attribution, and audience data
2. **Framework** — Define content pillars aligned to customer journey stages
3. **Calendar** — Documented content calendar with batch creation workflow
4. **Tracking** — Pixel, CAPI, UTMs, and attribution model
5. **Paid** — Engagement campaigns → traffic campaigns → retargeting
6. **UGC** — Branded hashtag, content library, ad integration
7. **Influencer** — Micro-influencer partnerships with trackable codes
8. **Optimize** — Weekly creative testing and budget reallocation
