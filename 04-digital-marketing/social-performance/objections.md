# Objection Handling — Social Media Performance Marketing

## Objection: "We're already posting on social. We have someone managing it."
**Response:** "Posting and having a performance system are two different things. Most brands have someone 'doing social' — posting content, responding to comments — but have no framework for what content drives purchases, no paid amplification strategy, and no retargeting. We analyzed Elevate's existing content and found that their engagement was 1.8% but they couldn't tell me which posts drove any revenue. After implementing a content pillars framework and proper tracking, we discovered that UGC-style posts converted at 3x the rate of polished product shots. Without the system, you're just guessing. We turn social from a time sink into a measurable revenue channel."

---

## Objection: "Social media doesn't convert for our brand. It's just for awareness."
**Response:** "That's usually what brands say before they have proper attribution. Elevate thought the same thing — social was credited with driving 8% of revenue. After we installed proper tracking (Meta Pixel, CAPI, UTMs), we discovered social was actually influencing 24% of purchases. The reason it looked like social 'didn't convert' was because the attribution window was broken, not because social wasn't working. Once we fixed tracking and implemented retargeting, social went from $94K to $386K in attributed revenue. The channel was always working — you just couldn't see it."

---

## Objection: "We tried influencer marketing and it didn't work."
**Response:** "Most influencer programs fail for one simple reason: no tracking. Brands send product to influencers, get posts, and have no idea if those posts drove any revenue. We solved this by giving every influencer a unique discount code and UTM-tagged link. Now you can see exactly how many orders came from each influencer, what the revenue was, and whether the partnership paid for itself. We also focus on micro-influencers (10K-50K followers) rather than mega-influencers, because the engagement rates and conversion rates are significantly higher, and the cost per acquisition is dramatically lower."

---

## Objection: "We don't have the budget for paid social on top of everything else."
**Response:** "We recommend $3-5K/month in paid amplification, which is modest compared to what most brands spend on Meta ads without a system. The key insight is that paid social amplifies your organic content — you don't need to create new creative for every ad. We identify your top-performing organic posts and put budget behind the ones that are already resonating. For Elevate, this meant their existing content library was suddenly working harder. The ROI on that $4K/month is 3.8x — so it pays for itself."

---

## Objection: "Our audience isn't on social media enough to make this worthwhile."
**Response:** "Elevate's target demographic — women 25-45 interested in skincare — is extremely active on Instagram. They had 24K followers, which is a decent audience. The issue wasn't audience size; it was that the audience was being undermonetized. We focus on the people who are already following you — they're the warmest audience you have. Retargeting an engaged follower is dramatically cheaper and more effective than trying to reach a cold audience through ads alone. Elevate's retargeting campaigns converted at 11x the rate of cold audience campaigns."

---

## Objection: "Our product is too expensive for people to buy on social."
**Response:** "Elevate is a premium skincare brand with products ranging from $45 to $180. If that price point can convert through social, yours can too. The key is the retargeting sequence — you don't ask for the purchase on the first touchpoint. You build familiarity through content, then retarget with product showcases, then offer a limited-time incentive or free shipping. The average Elevate customer sees 4-6 touchpoints before purchasing. Social is especially effective for premium products because the content builds trust and demonstrates value in ways that a landing page can't."

---

## Objection: "How do we create enough content to feed a social system?"
**Response:** "This is where most brands get stuck, and it's why we built the batch creation workflow. Instead of creating content daily (which is chaotic and unsustainable), you create all your content for the week in a single 4-hour session. We build Canva templates for each content pillar so production is fast. You queue it in Later or Buffer and the system runs itself. For Elevate, this meant going from sporadic posting to consistent 5 posts/week without adding headcount. The key is templates and systems, not more hours."

---

## Objection: "Our competitors are already huge on social. How do we compete?"
**Response:** "The brands winning on social aren't necessarily the biggest — they're the most systematic. A competitor with 100K followers but no retargeting, no paid amplification, and no content pillars is less effective than a brand with 30K followers and a full performance system. We help you compete by being smarter, not by outspending. In Elevate's case, their engagement rate went from 1.8% to 4.7% — higher than many competitors with larger followings. Quality of engagement and conversion beats quantity of followers."

---

## Objection: "We already use Shopify and think about social as awareness. Why change?"
**Response:** "Shopify's strength is e-commerce infrastructure, but it's not a social strategy. Social can drive awareness, but it can also drive direct revenue — and often at a lower CAC than search or display ads. For DTC brands especially, social platforms are where your customers live, discover products, and get recommendations from people they trust (influencers, friends, UGC). Ignoring that journey means you're ceding those customers to competitors who are present. The brands winning DTC right now are treating social as a performance channel, not an afterthought."

---

## Objection: "We don't have anyone who can create content consistently."
**Response:** "The batch creation workflow we implement doesn't require a content agency or in-house creative team. We use Canva templates that your existing staff can operate — a 4-hour session once a week produces all your content for the following week. For Elevate, this meant their marketing coordinator (one person) could run the entire social system without additional support. We also integrate UGC heavily, which means you're reposting customer content (no creation required) and using it in ads. The system is designed to be maintainable, not dependent on heroic daily effort."
