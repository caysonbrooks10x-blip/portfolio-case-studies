# Objection Handling — Paid Ads + Funnel Optimization

## Objection: "We tried paid ads before and it didn't work."
**Response:** "That's actually the norm, not the exception. Most paid ad failures stem from one of three root causes: broken tracking, no funnel architecture, or campaigns built without understanding where buyers actually convert. We start every engagement with an audit to identify which problem you have. In NovaTech's case, their Meta pixel was firing on the wrong events, which meant the algorithm was optimizing for the wrong people entirely. Once we fixed tracking, performance improved 44% in the first three weeks — before we even changed creative or targeting. The ads weren't the problem. The foundation was."

---

## Objection: "Our audience is too niche/targeted for paid advertising to work."
**Response:** "That's a common assumption, but the data usually contradicts it. NovaTech sells project management software to mid-market B2B companies. They said the same thing. After we rebuilt their targeting using intent signals — in-market audiences combined with keyword targeting, plus lookalikes based on their best customers — they went from 23 demos a month to 147. Niche audiences often perform better in paid because the competition is lower and the intent is higher. The key is finding where your buyers are actually searching or researching, not just assuming they're not on these platforms."

---

## Objection: "We don't have the budget for a full paid media overhaul."
**Response:** "We actually decreased NovaTech's ad spend by 8% while improving results by 300%+ on most metrics. The issue isn't total budget — it's where the budget goes and how campaigns are structured. We can often achieve better results within your existing spend by reallocating from underperforming campaigns to winners. Our Phase 1 audit tells us exactly where to cut and where to invest. Many clients find they can reallocate internally without increasing ad spend at all."

## Objection: "How long before we see results?"
**Response:** "You'd see early improvements within 2-3 weeks — particularly around tracking accuracy and landing page conversion rates. By day 45, we'd expect to see meaningful CAC improvements assuming your metrics are in the range we typically see. Full results across all channels typically materialize by day 90. That's the timeline that matched NovaTech's experience, and it's consistent with what we see across B2B SaaS clients. We're not promising overnight miracles, but we are promising a clear trajectory of improvement with weekly visibility into what's working."

**Response:** "That's one of the first things we fix. NovaTech came to us unable to connect ad spend to closed revenue. By the end of Week 2, we had GA4 and server-side tracking configured so every platform was properly credited. We built a unified Looker Studio dashboard that shows you, in real time, exactly which campaigns and ad groups are driving demo requests, qualified leads, and closed revenue. Attribution becomes a non-issue because the infrastructure makes it visible."

## Objection: "Our sales cycle is long (6+ months). How do you optimize for that?"
**Response:** "For long B2B cycles, we optimize for leading indicators — qualified demos booked, sales conversations started, opportunities created — rather than final closed revenue. These leading indicators are strongly correlated with eventual revenue, and they give us signal within 30-60 days rather than 6 months. We also build attribution models that give proper credit to upper-funnel touchpoints (brand awareness, consideration) that contribute to deals even if they weren't the final click. NovaTech's 94-day sales cycle shortened to 61 days partly because the leads we sent them were better pre-qualified and arrived faster."

---

## Objection: "What if Google/Meta/LinkedIn changes their algorithm or policies?"
**Response:** "Platform changes happen regularly — that's why we maintain diversified strategies across all three major platforms and don't concentrate all spend in one channel. If Meta changes its algorithm, we shift budget to Google or LinkedIn. If LinkedIn underperforms, we reallocate to Meta. We've managed through iOS 14 updates, Google Ads policy changes, and LinkedIn audience shifts. The framework we build — the tracking, the landing pages, the retargeting infrastructure — is platform-agnostic. The foundation holds even when specific tactics need to change."
