# Case Study: Paid Ads Management + Funnel Optimization
**Client:** NovaTech Solutions | **Project Duration:** 90 Days | **ROI: 312%**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

NovaTech Solutions, a B2B SaaS company offering project management software, was spending $42,000/month on paid advertising across Meta, Google Ads, and LinkedIn with little to show for it. Their customer acquisition cost (CAC) sat at $187, and the sales team was drowning in unqualified leads. After a comprehensive paid ads overhaul and funnel restructuring, we reduced their CAC to $61 — a 67% decrease — while increasing monthly qualified demos booked from 23 to 147. The 90-day engagement delivered a 312% return on ad spend and positioned NovaTech to scale profitably.

## 3. The Problem

NovaTech's leadership presented three primary pain points:

1. **CAC was unsustainable.** At $187 per acquisition, the math didn't work — their average contract value was $1,200/year, meaning payback period stretched beyond 18 months.

2. **Lead quality was inconsistent.** The sales team reported that 60%+ of booked demos were from prospects who didn't match their ICP, wasting significant sales capacity.

3. **No unified attribution.** Meta, Google, and LinkedIn were managed as siloed channels with no cross-channel visibility. Leadership couldn't tell which platform actually drove closed revenue.

Internal attempts to fix the issues had failed because there was no dedicated strategic owner. The marketing coordinator was managing day-to-day ad ops without bandwidth to think structurally about funnel optimization.

## 4. Approach & Strategy

### Audit Phase (Days 1-14)

We began with a full-funnel audit across all three platforms:
- Analyzed 12 months of ad account data, identifying underperforming campaign types and audience overlap
- Built a attribution model using Google Analytics 4 and Meta's native attribution tools to map the true customer journey
- Reviewed landing pages and identified 4 critical drop-off points in the conversion funnel
- Conducted a competitive analysis of 6 direct competitors' ad strategies

Key findings from the audit:
- Google Ads campaigns were targeting overly broad keywords, driving high-volume but low-intent traffic
- Meta campaigns were running without proper conversion events, optimizing for engagement rather than demo requests
- LinkedIn campaigns had stale audience targeting (last 5 years of data) and were using Message Ads without a proper nurture sequence
- No retargeting infrastructure existed across any platform

### Strategy Redesign (Days 15-30)

Based on audit findings, we rebuilt the entire paid strategy around a **hub-and-spoke model**:

- **Hub:** Google Ads as the primary intent-capture channel (prospects actively searching for project management solutions)
- **Spoke 1:** Meta as social proof and retargeting (building familiarity before conversion)
- **Spoke 2:** LinkedIn for enterprise decision-makers (C-suite, VP-level) who don't convert via bottom-of-funnel ads

**Funnel Architecture:**
```
Awareness → Retargeting → Consideration → Conversion → Nurture → Closed
```

New campaign structure:
- **Campaign 1:** Google Search — High-intent keywords (project management software, team collaboration tools, Gantt chart alternatives)
- **Campaign 2:** Google Display — Competitor Conquesting (targeting users researching Asana, Monday.com, Wrike)
- **Campaign 3:** Meta — Retargeting warm audiences (video viewers, page visitors, engagement custom audiences)
- **Campaign 4:** LinkedIn — Sponsored Content targeting VP/Director roles at companies 50+ employees
- **Campaign 5:** LinkedIn — Message Ads for enterprise outreach sequence

## 5. Implementation

### Week 1-2: Foundation Building
- Migrated Meta pixel to server-side tracking via Google Tag Manager for more reliable data
- Set up GA4 enhanced e-commerce tracking for full funnel visibility
- Built 6 new conversion actions in Google Ads aligned to actual business outcomes
- Created custom audience segments in Meta (warm, hot, cold) based on engagement patterns
- Refreshed LinkedIn audience parameters using job title, company size, and industry refinements

### Week 3-4: Creative and Landing Page Overhaul
- Developed 14 new ad creatives across platforms (carousels, single image, video)
- Wrote 8 new ad copy variants testing different value propositions
- Created dedicated landing pages for each campaign type (not just pointing to homepage)
- Implemented urgency elements on high-intent landing pages (limited demo slots, early-bird pricing)
- A/B tested 3 headline variants on primary conversion pages

### Week 5-8: Campaign Activation and Optimization
- Launched new campaign structure with initial $40K/month budget
- Set up automated bidding rules in Google Ads (target CPA of $75)
- Implemented dayparting in Meta to concentrate spend during peak conversion hours
- Launched LinkedIn Message Ads sequence for enterprise leads (5-touch sequence)
- Activated retargeting across all platforms with 30/60/90 day windows

### Week 9-12: Scaling What Works
- Reallocated 35% of budget from underperforming LinkedIn Sponsored Content to Google Search
- Introduced Smart Bidding with Conversion Value rules in Google Ads
- Launched lookalike audiences in Meta based on best-performing customer segments
- Added competitor conquesting campaigns in Google Display
- Implemented weekly optimization calls with NovaTech's sales team to align on lead quality

## 6. Key Features & Tactics

**Advanced Audience Targeting:**
- Used Meta's Advantage+ audience expansion strategically, capping reach to preserve relevance
- Built intent signals in Google Ads using in-market audiences combined with keyword targeting
- Created LinkedIn matched audiences from NovaTech's existing customer list (similar company profiles)

**Creative Testing Framework:**
- Ran 3 creative tests per platform simultaneously (creative fatigue was a known issue)
- Measured creative performance by cost per demo booked, not just CTR
- Killed low performers within 5 days, scaled winners within 7 days

**Funnel Optimization:**
- Added a pre-demo qualification form (2 questions) to filter out mismatched leads before sales intake
- Built a lead scoring model in HubSpot based on engagement data from paid channels
- Created a 7-email nurture sequence for leads who viewed pricing but didn't book a demo

**Attribution Modeling:**
- Implemented data-driven attribution in GA4 to properly credit upper-funnel touchpoints
- Created a unified dashboard in Looker Studio connecting all paid channels to closed revenue

## 9. Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1: Stale Meta Pixel Data**
The existing Meta pixel was firing incorrectly, causing the algorithm to optimize for the wrong events.
*Solution:* Migrated to server-side tracking via GTM. Cleaned up conversion events and rebuilt custom audience logic from scratch. Within 3 weeks, Meta CPA dropped 44%.

**Challenge 2: Google Ads Budget Bleed**
Broad match keywords were burning budget on irrelevant searches.
*Solution:* Implemented modified broad match with custom negative keyword lists (updated weekly). Added phrase match variants for high-performing terms. Separated brand campaigns at 20% of search budget to protect branded traffic.

**Challenge 3: LinkedIn Lead Quality Mismatch**
LinkedIn was generating C-suite conversations but no pipeline progression — executives were engaging but not converting to demos.
*Solution:* Rebuilt LinkedIn strategy around Sponsored Content vs. Message Ads. Created a content-led approach that warmed prospects before asking for a demo. Added a "book a strategy call" CTA instead of direct demo request.

**Challenge 4: Sales-Marketing Misalignment**
Sales was receiving leads 48-72 hours after capture, missing peak engagement windows.
*Solution:* Implemented real-time lead notifications via HubSpot and Slack integration. Created a same-day follow-up SLA for leads scoring above 70. Lead response time dropped from 52 hours to 4 hours.

## 10. Lessons Learned

1. **Attribution visibility is foundational.** Before we could optimize anything, we needed clean attribution data. GA4 and server-side tracking were prerequisites for every other improvement.

2. **Funnel architecture matters more than creative.** We spent two weeks on landing pages and form flows before touching ad creative. That structural work paid dividends in conversion rate improvement.

3. **Retargeting is where the money is made.** Cold audiences converted at 1.2%; retargeting audiences converted at 11.4%. Building a proper retargeting infrastructure was the single highest-leverage action we took.

4. **Sales alignment is a paid media lever.** We discovered that lead quality wasn't just a marketing problem — it was a sales follow-up problem. Tightening the handoff reduced our effective CAC by 18%.

5. **Budget reallocation beats budget increases.** We achieved better results by moving $14K/month from underperforming campaigns to winners than by increasing total budget.

## 16. Replication Formula

This methodology can be applied to any B2B company spending $20K+/month on paid advertising:

1. **Audit** — Full-funnel attribution audit across all platforms
2. **Architect** — Design hub-and-spoke model based on where your buyers convert
3. **Isolate** — Build dedicated landing pages for each campaign type
4. **Activate** — Launch with a testing framework (3 variants per element)
5. **Optimize** — Weekly performance reviews with budget reallocation decisions
6. **Scale** — Double down on <1.5x payback channels once validated
