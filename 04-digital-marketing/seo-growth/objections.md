# Objection Handling — SEO Growth Strategy

## Objection: "SEO takes too long. We need leads now."
**Response:** "You're right that SEO isn't instant — but it's not as slow as people think either. We typically see ranking improvements by Month 2-3, and meaningful traffic growth by Month 4. More importantly, unlike paid channels that stop producing the moment you stop spending, SEO rankings persist. The pages we build now will generate leads in 2 years. That's the real comparison: do you want to pay $165 per lead forever, or invest now to get $12 leads indefinitely?"

---

## Objection: "We tried content marketing before and it didn't work."
**Response:** "Most content marketing failures have a root cause: no keyword strategy, thin content (under 800 words), no internal linking, and no promotion. It's not that content doesn't work — it's that random content doesn't work. Our approach is systematic: we build topic clusters where every piece of content supports the others, we target keywords with documented search volume and commercial intent, and we build links to establish authority. Meridian had essentially no organic presence. After 6 months, they're generating 89 demo requests a month from organic alone. The difference is strategy and execution, not the channel itself."

---

## Objection: "Our industry is too competitive for SEO."
**Response:** "Competitive is relative. We don't try to outrank Microsoft or Tableau for generic head terms. We target long-tail commercial keywords where the competition is manageable and the intent is high. Instead of 'data visualization software' (dominated by brands with millions in SEO budgets), we targeted 'financial dashboard software for CFOs' — a niche term with strong commercial intent and much lower competition. Our strategy is to win the keywords that matter for conversion, not necessarily the highest-volume terms. Meridian now ranks for 47 keywords in the top 10, many of which are driving direct demo requests."

---

## Objection: "How do we know you'll actually produce the content?"
**Response:** "We provide a documented content calendar with topics, target keywords, and word count targets before we begin. You approve all briefs before writing. We track and report on every piece published. Over 6 months with Meridian, we produced 52 SEO-optimized pages — we don't miss deadlines because we have a production system, not just good intentions. We can share examples of our content production pipeline from previous engagements."

---

## Objection: "What if Google changes the algorithm and we lose rankings?"
**Response:** "Google algorithm updates happen regularly, and yes, they can impact rankings. But our defensive strategy is diversification: we rank for 47 different keywords across 5 topic clusters, so no single update can wipe out our progress. We also focus on white-hat, user-first content — the kind Google says it wants to rank. Sites that get hit by algorithm updates are usually doing something against guidelines. Our approach is to build genuine topical authority, which is the most durable ranking factor there is."

---

## Objection: "We don't have the budget for a 6-month engagement."
**Response:** "The budget is $40,500 over 6 months, which averages $6,750/month. Meridian's paid channel was generating about 30 qualified leads per month at $165 CAC — that's $4,950/month in acquisition cost. For less than their paid acquisition cost, they built a channel generating 89 leads/month at $12 CAC. If budget is a concern, we can phase the engagement — start with technical fixes and keyword research, prove the rankings move, then commit to full content production. But the math typically supports doing the full program."

---

## Objection: "Our sales team doesn't trust marketing leads anyway."
**Response:** "That's a common issue, and we address it directly. First, we implement lead scoring in HubSpot so only high-quality organic leads go to sales. Second, we gave Meridian data showing that organic leads actually had a 23% higher average contract value than paid leads — likely because organic searchers are further along in their buying journey. Once the sales team saw the quality difference, they started preferring organic leads. The key is making sure the wrong leads never reach sales in the first place."

---

## Objection: "Can't we just do this ourselves with a content writer?"
**Response:** "You could, but the difference between content and SEO content is the strategy underneath it. Anyone can write a blog post. The question is: does it target a keyword with search volume? Is it structured for both users and search engines? Does it link into a broader topical authority strategy? Does it have the backlinks it needs to rank? These are the elements that make content work as SEO, and they require specialized expertise. We also handle technical SEO, link building, and ongoing optimization that most content writers don't have the skill set for."

---

## Objection: "How do we track ROI from SEO?"
**Response:** "We set up GA4 with organic traffic attribution and HubSpot integration so every organic visitor is tracked through to demo request and closed revenue. At the end of 6 months, we can tell you exactly how many demo requests came from organic, what those converted to in revenue, and what your actual CAC was. We provide monthly ranking reports and traffic dashboards — you'll never be wondering whether SEO is working."

---

## Objection: "We're a small company. Do we really need SEO?"
**Response:** "Small companies often benefit most from SEO because they can't afford to outspend competitors on paid. An organic channel that generates $12 leads instead of $165 leads means your marketing budget goes 13x further. For Meridian, a 42-person company, SEO became their most efficient channel by far. You don't need a large marketing budget — you need a smart one. And organic compounding is especially powerful for smaller companies because every link you earn and every ranking you gain makes the next win easier."
