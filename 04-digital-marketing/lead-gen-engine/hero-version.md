## The Backstory

James Whitfield, VP of Sales at Pinnacle Logistics, had a $60K monthly marketing budget and a sales team starving for leads. The traffic was there — Google Ads, LinkedIn, organic search. But none of it converted.

His website was a static corporate brochure. No calls to action. No lead capture. No differentiation from the 47 other logistics companies in their region.

His sales team was following up on leads 48 hours after they came in — if they remembered to check the inbox. There was no CRM discipline, no scoring system, no prioritization.

The result: 85 qualified leads a month, a 12% lead-to-opportunity rate, and a sales team that blamed marketing for everything.

---

## What Nobody Tells You About Lead Gen

The most expensive thing in lead generation isn't the ad spend. It's the conversion moment — the gap between someone being interested and someone becoming a lead.

Most companies spend 90% of their budget driving traffic and 0% optimizing that conversion moment. That's backwards.

We audited Pinnacle's conversion infrastructure first. What we found:

- Zero dedicated landing pages — every ad sent traffic to the homepage
- A contact form that asked for 11 fields
- No thank-you pages, no confirmation, no secondary offer
- A CRM that nobody updated
- Sales follow-up happening 48-72 hours after lead submission
- No lead scoring — every lead treated identically

The traffic was bleeding out at the landing page. Fix that first.

---

## The System We Built

**Phase 1: Conversion Infrastructure (Weeks 1-3)**

We built 12 dedicated landing pages — one per service line and intent cluster. For each page:
- Single focused offer and CTA
- Trust signals above the fold (certifications, client logos, years in business)
- Lead magnet offer (free logistics assessment, industry report, pricing guide)
- Form reduced from 11 fields to 4 (name, email, company, phone)
- One-click Google and LinkedIn sign-in to reduce friction further

The homepage got rebuilt with a clear value proposition and 3 conversion paths.

**Phase 2: Lead Scoring and Routing (Weeks 3-5)**

We built a lead scoring model with James's sales team input. Every lead that came in was scored on:
- Company size (enterprise vs SMB)
- Service interest (high-value vs commodity)
- Engagement signals (downloaded guide, watched video, visited pricing page)
- Geographic priority

High-score leads triggered an immediate Slack notification to the assigned sales rep. Medium-score leads entered a nurture sequence. Low-score leads were auto-responded with content and re-engaged at 30 days.

**Phase 3: Nurture Automation (Weeks 4-6)**

Five automated nurture sequences built for different score tiers and service interests:
- Enterprise logistics decision-makers → 8-email sequence, executive focused
- SMB commodity shippers → 5-email sequence, price-competitiveness focused
- Re-engagement (cold leads) → 3-email sequence, new content only
- Partner/referral leads → 2-email, high-touch handoff
- Inbound inquiry → same-day personal response + 5-email nurture

**Phase 4: Paid Traffic Optimization (Weeks 5-8)**

Google Ads restructured around landing page themes. Separate campaigns for branded, non-branded, competitor-targeting, and product-category keywords. Each sending to dedicated landing pages.

LinkedIn campaigns rebuilt for decision-maker targeting (VP of Logistics, Director of Operations) with InMail sequences for high-score leads.

---

## The Lead Response Time Intervention

The single highest-leverage change we made was the fastest to implement.

We built a 15-minute SLA for all leads scoring above 70. When a high-score lead submitted a form, James got a Slack notification within 60 seconds. His team had a standing instruction: respond within 15 minutes during business hours.

For a logistics company, that matters more than most industries. Shipping decisions are often made in real-time. A lead inquiring about freight services at 10am has a decision window that closes by noon.

The sales team initially pushed back — "we can't drop everything for every lead." We built a simple Slack bot that only alerted them for leads scoring above 70. That filtered out the noise. Compliance went from 0% to 89% within the first week.

## What We'd Do Differently

**Build the CRM hygiene protocol from Day 1.** We spent 3 weeks mid-engagement cleaning up 2 years of CRM debt. Should have done a full CRM audit and hygiene sprint before launching the new lead flow.

**Invest in case study content earlier.** Logistics buyers are skeptical of marketing claims. The moment we added a "Results" page with specific client outcomes, landing page conversion went up another 1.2 percentage points.

**LinkedIn retargeting should have launched Week 2.** We waited until Week 7. The warm audience we built from site visitors would have accelerated everything.
