# Case Study: Conversion Rate Optimization Project
**Client:** Stratum Financial | **Project Duration:** 5 Months | **Conversion Rate: 3.1x**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Stratum Financial, a fintech company offering business banking and financial management tools for startups and SMBs, was spending heavily on paid acquisition but converting only 2.1% of website visitors to demo requests. Their $1.2M annual paid traffic was producing a CAC of $340 — unsustainable for a product with a $2,400 average contract value. After a comprehensive 5-month CRO program including UX research, A/B testing, landing page optimization, and funnel restructuring, we increased their conversion rate to 6.5% — a 3.1x improvement — while reducing their effective CAC to $112. The CRO program delivered $1.8M in incremental annual revenue without spending an additional dollar on traffic.

---

## 3. The Problem

Stratum's conversion challenges fell into four interconnected areas:

1. **Landing page misalignment.** Paid traffic from different campaigns (brand keywords, competitor keywords, product keywords) was all being sent to the same homepage or generic landing pages. Each traffic source had different intent, but the destination was the same.

2. **Unclear value proposition.** The homepage and key landing pages didn't communicate Stratum's differentiated value quickly enough. Visitors couldn't understand in 5 seconds why they should switch from their existing bank or financial tool.

3. **Trust deficit.** Financial services require trust, but the site lacked sufficient social proof elements — testimonials were minimal, no customer logos were displayed, security badges were buried, and no case studies were prominently featured.

4. **Friction in the conversion path.** The demo request form had 7 fields when 3 would have sufficed. The CTA button copy was generic ("Get Started"). No urgency or scarcity elements existed. The thank-you page was a dead end with no continued engagement.

Internal attempts at CRO had been ad-hoc — someone would have an idea, it would get implemented without testing, and results would be anecdotal. No structured experimentation program existed.

## 4. Approach & Strategy

### Phase 1: Research & Audit (Weeks 1-4)

We began with a comprehensive analysis of Stratum's current conversion performance:

**Quantitative Analysis:**
- Analyzed 12 months of GA4 data: traffic sources, user flows, conversion paths, drop-off points
- Reviewed paid campaign data: CPC, conversion rates by keyword type, audience segments
- Examined heatmaps and session recordings (Hotjar): scroll depth, click patterns, rage clicks, dead clicks
- Analyzed form abandonment data: which fields caused drop-off, average time on form

**Qualitative Research:**
- Conducted 8 user interviews (current customers and lost prospects) to understand decision journey
- Ran surveys with 240 respondents on purchase motivators and objections
- Analyzed support ticket themes to identify product misconceptions

**Competitive Teardown:**
- Analyzed landing pages of 6 direct competitors (Mercury, Relay, Wise, Novo, Bluevine)
- Documented conversion tactics used by high-performing fintech landing pages

**Key Findings:**
- Homepage was the #1 landing page but converted at only 1.8% — should have been a supporting page
- Product-specific landing pages converted at 3.2% but received only 12% of traffic
- Demo form had a 67% abandonment rate — 7 fields was causing friction
- 73% of visitors left within 30 seconds — value prop wasn't being communicated
- Trust signals were below fintech industry standards

### Phase 2: Experimentation Strategy (Weeks 5-8)

We developed a prioritized test plan based on potential impact and ease of implementation:

**High-Impact, Low-Effort Tests (Priority 1):**
- CTA button copy optimization ("Get Started" → "Book Your Free Demo")
- Form field reduction (7 fields → 3 fields)
- Above-fold value proposition rewrite
- CTA button color/size optimization

**High-Impact, High-Effort Tests (Priority 2):**
- Landing page restructuring (dedicated pages per traffic source)
- New homepage hero with clearer value prop
- Customer logo display and testimonial slider
- Trust signal placement (security badges, SOC2 certification)

**Moderate-Impact Tests (Priority 3):**
- Urgency/scarcity elements ("Limited demo slots available")
- Social proof notifications ("5 startups joined this week")
- Exit-intent overlay with lead capture
- Interactive ROI calculator

### Phase 3: Implementation & Testing (Weeks 9-20)

We implemented changes systematically, running A/B tests on high-impact elements:

**Test 1: CTA Button Copy**
- Control: "Get Started"
- Variant: "Book Your Free Demo"
- Result: +18% conversion rate improvement

**Test 2: Form Simplification**
- Control: 7-field form (Name, Company, Email, Phone, Company Size, Monthly Revenue, Use Case)
- Variant: 3-field form (Name, Email, Company Size)
- Result: +42% form completion rate

**Test 3: Homepage Hero Rewrite**
- Control: Generic hero ("Banking built for modern businesses")
- Variant: Specific outcome hero ("Move money 3x faster. 95% of startups approve in 24 hours.")
- Result: +31% demo request conversion

**Test 4: Trust Signal Placement**
- Control: Trust badges in footer
- Variant: Trust badges near CTA (security, SOC2, customer logos)
- Result: +22% conversion rate improvement

**Test 5: Dedicated Landing Pages**
- Control: All paid traffic to homepage
- Variant: Traffic segmented to specific landing pages by intent
- Result: +67% conversion rate improvement (combined effect)

### Phase 4: Optimization & Scale (Weeks 21-24)

After identifying winners, we scaled successful variants and continued testing:

- Homepage redesign based on winning hero variant
- Form simplification rolled out to all demo request forms
- Trust signals added to all high-traffic pages
- Dedicated landing pages built for 4 primary traffic segments
- Exit-intent overlay implemented site-wide
- ROI calculator added to homepage and landing pages

## 6. Key Features & Tactics

**Scientific A/B Testing:**
Every change was tested before full implementation. We ran tests to statistical significance (95% confidence level) and tracked not just primary conversion metrics but also secondary effects (time on page, bounce rate, form abandonment). This prevented us from optimizing one metric at the expense of another.

**Customer Journey Mapping:**
We mapped the full customer journey from first touch to demo request, identifying all the moments where friction occurred or trust was lacking. Each test addressed a specific friction point or trust gap.

**Landing Page Segmentation:**
Instead of sending all paid traffic to one destination, we built dedicated landing pages for each traffic segment. Someone searching for "Mercury vs Stratum" got a different landing page than someone searching for "best business bank account."

**Form Psychology:**
We studied which form fields caused anxiety (phone number, monthly revenue) and which were necessary for qualification. The goal was to minimize friction while preserving enough information for sales to prioritize leads.

**Trust Signal Architecture:**
In fintech, trust is the primary currency. We developed a systematic approach to placing trust signals at the exact moments when doubt typically arises — near CTAs, on forms, in headers.

**Social Proof Engineering:**
We went beyond testimonials, adding customer logos, live notification pop-ups ("5 startups joined this week"), case study excerpts, and security certifications to create layers of social proof.

## 9. Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1: No Baseline Data**
Stratum had been tracking pageviews but not user behavior flows or drop-off points.
*Solution:* We installed Hotjar and analyzed session recordings for 2 weeks before making any changes. This gave us qualitative data (where users were getting stuck) that complemented the quantitative GA4 data.

**Challenge 2: Sales Team Pushing for More Form Fields**
Sales wanted 7 fields because they wanted maximum information for prioritization.
*Solution:* We negotiated a middle ground: a 3-field form for initial capture with progressive profiling (additional questions) after the demo was booked. We showed sales data that 67% form abandonment was costing them more leads than the field reduction would cost them in qualification data.

**Challenge 3: Design Stakeholder Resistance**
The design team was protective of the existing site aesthetic and reluctant to make changes.
*Solution:* We framed everything around test data, not opinions. When the A/B test showed +18% improvement from CTA copy change, resistance melted. We got buy-in by showing wins, not asking for permission.

**Challenge 4: Limited Traffic for Statistical Significance**
With 12,000 monthly visitors and a 2.1% conversion rate, achieving statistical significance on small changes took time.
*Solution:* We focused on high-impact tests first — changes that modeling suggested would move the needle significantly. We also used multi-armed bandit algorithms in Optimizely to direct more traffic to winning variants mid-test.

**Challenge 5: Google Optimize Sunset**
Google announced Optimize was being sunset, which Stratum had been using informally.
*Solution:* We migrated to Optimizely for proper server-side experimentation, which also gave them more robust statistical capabilities and better integration with their data stack.

## 10. Lessons Learned

1. **The homepage is usually the wrong landing page.** For paid traffic, dedicated landing pages aligned to the search intent will always outperform sending everyone to the homepage. Stratum's homepage converted at 1.8%; dedicated landing pages hit 8.1%.

2. **Form fields are a conversion killer.** Every additional form field reduces completion rates exponentially. Going from 7 fields to 3 increased form completions by 42%. If you don't need the data for the initial conversion, ask for it later.

3. **Trust signals must be placed at friction points.** Burying trust badges in the footer is useless. We placed security certifications, customer logos, and testimonials exactly where doubt arises — near CTAs, on forms, above the fold.

4. **Copy is a CRO lever.** Changing "Get Started" to "Book Your Free Demo" improved conversion by 18%. The specific, benefit-driven headline improved it 31%. Copy choices that seem minor can have outsized impact.

5. **You can't optimize what you can't measure.** Before this engagement, Stratum had no A/B testing program. Every change was made based on intuition. Building a testing culture and infrastructure meant continuous improvement beyond the engagement.

## 16. Replication Formula

This methodology applies to any company with traffic but poor conversion:

1. **Research** — Analytics review, heatmaps, session recordings, user interviews
2. **Map** — Customer journey mapping, friction point identification
3. **Prioritize** — Impact/effort matrix for test ideas
4. **Test** — Scientific A/B tests to statistical significance
5. **Scale** — Roll out winners, retire losers
6. **Iterate** — Continuous testing cadence (2-3 tests/month)
7. **Embed** — Build internal testing culture and infrastructure

## 17. Risk Management

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Test results not statistically significant | Focus on high-impact tests, use multi-armed bandit |
| Sales resistance to form simplification | Progressive profiling, show abandonment data |
| Design stakeholder pushback | Data-driven wins, show test results |
| Measurement validity issues | Guardrail metrics, set up proper event tracking |
| Temporary seasonality skewing results | Use rolling averages, compare YoY where possible |
