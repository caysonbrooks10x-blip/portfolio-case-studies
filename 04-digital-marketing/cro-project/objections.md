# Objection Handling — Conversion Rate Optimization

## Objection: "We don't have enough traffic to run A/B tests."
**Response:** "Stratum had 12,000 monthly visitors and we ran 9 tests successfully over 5 months. Even at moderate traffic levels, you can run A/B tests — you just need to focus on high-impact changes and use the right statistical approach. For lower-traffic pages, we use multi-armed bandit algorithms that direct more traffic to winning variants faster. The real question isn't whether you have enough traffic to test — it's whether you can afford NOT to test when you're paying $340 per lead and converting only 2.1%."

---

## Objection: "Our developers are too busy to implement A/B tests."
**Response:** "We handle the testing infrastructure and manage the tests themselves. Your developers approve the winning variants — we handle the implementation of the test code and the analysis. We use server-side testing via Optimizely, which minimizes developer involvement. They spend maybe 2 hours a month reviewing results and approving rollouts. The only time we need significant developer resources is for major site architecture changes, and we scope those separately."

---

## Objection: "We've tried CRO before and it didn't work."
**Response:** "Most CRO failures fall into one of three categories: testing without research (random ideas, not addressing actual friction), running tests that never reach statistical significance (no conclusive results), or not implementing winners (tests pass but changes never ship). We start with 4 weeks of research — session recordings, user interviews, analytics analysis — so we know exactly what friction points to address. Every test we run has a clear success metric and a guardrail metric to catch negative side effects. And we don't leave winners on the testing platform — we implement them."

---

## Objection: "How long before we see results?"
**Response:** "You'll see quick win improvements within 4-6 weeks — CTA copy changes, form simplification, hero rewrites typically show results within 2-3 weeks of launching. The bigger wins (landing page restructure, dedicated pages) take longer because they require more traffic to reach significance, but you should see meaningful results by Month 3. Full results at Month 5. Stratum saw their conversion rate improve from 2.1% to 3.2% in the first 8 weeks just from quick wins."

---

## Objection: "Our product is too complex for simple CTAs."
**Response:** "Complexity is exactly why CRO matters more, not less. When products are complex, the job of the landing page is to reduce complexity anxiety — not to explain everything. Stratum is a financial services product with real complexity. The winning hero variant wasn't the one with more information; it was the one that made a specific, credible promise: 'Move money 3x faster. 95% of startups approve in 24 hours.' That's a simple hook that creates curiosity without overwhelming. Complexity requires clarity, not more content."

---

## Objection: "Our sales team wants more form fields."
**Response:** "This is a common tension, and we solve it with progressive profiling. The initial form capture should be minimal — just enough to route the lead and trigger a follow-up. We showed Stratum that their 7-field form had a 67% abandonment rate, which was costing them far more leads than the additional qualification data was worth. We negotiated a 3-field initial form with progressive profiling after demo booking. Sales gets the data they need, just at a later stage when the lead has already shown intent."

---

## Objection: "How do we know the tests are statistically valid?"
**Response:** "We run all tests to 95% statistical significance before calling a winner. We also track guardrail metrics — bounce rate, time on page — to catch false positives where conversion improves but for the wrong reasons (e.g., deceptive tactics that hurt long-term trust). For Stratum, 8 of 9 tests reached statistical significance. The one that didn't, we retired without implementation."

---

## Objection: "Our website is going through a redesign soon anyway."
**Response:** "That's actually an ideal time to invest in CRO. You'll have new pages to optimize, and the insights from our testing program will inform the redesign — we can tell you exactly what elements on the current site are working and what needs to change. We'd recommend running a few quick tests now to validate assumptions, then focusing the redesign on the high-impact pages we've already identified. The redesign becomes an implementation of what we've learned, not a starting-from-scratch exercise."

---

## Objection: "We want to focus on traffic growth, not conversion."
**Response:** "I understand the instinct to invest in traffic, but consider the math. If you double your traffic tomorrow but your conversion rate stays at 2.1%, you're just doubling your costs. If you improve conversion from 2.1% to 6.5% first, then double traffic, you're getting 3x the results for the same traffic investment. CRO makes your traffic more efficient — it compounds with every channel you run. For Stratum, the CRO program delivered $1.8M in incremental revenue. If they'd spent that $50K on traffic instead, they'd have gotten maybe $150K in additional leads at $340 CAC. CRO wins."

---

## Objection: "We don't have the budget for a 5-month engagement."
**Response:** "The $50K CRO investment returned $1.8M in incremental revenue — that's a 36x ROI, which is exceptional even by CRO standards. The typical ROI for a well-executed CRO program is 5-10x. If budget is a concern, we can phase the engagement — run the research and quick wins first ($15K), prove the results, then continue. But the full program is designed to pay for itself within the first 2 months based on CAC improvement alone."
