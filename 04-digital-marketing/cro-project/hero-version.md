# HERO CASE STUDY: Stratum Financial — Conversion Rate Optimization
**3.1x Conversion Rate. CAC Dropped From $340 to $112.**
*Category: Digital Marketing | Client: Stratum Financial | 5-Month Engagement*

## The Hook
Stratum Financial was spending $100K/month on paid ads and converting just 2.1% of visitors to demo requests. Their $340 CAC was mathematically broken for a $2,400 ACV product. No A/B testing infrastructure existed. No systematic CRO. No hypothesis-driven optimization. Just "more traffic" as the strategy. We turned that around: 3.1x conversion rate, $112 CAC, $1.8M incremental annual revenue at zero additional traffic cost.

---

## The Backstory

Rachel Torres, Head of Growth at Stratum Financial, had a math problem.

She was spending $100K/month on Google Ads and Meta. Getting 12,000 visitors. Converting 252 to demo requests. Paying $340 per demo. Closing at 18% to $2,400 ACV contracts.

$100K/month × 18% close × $2,400 = $43,200 revenue.
Return on ad spend: 0.43x.

She was burning money on ads that were working — technically. Visitors were coming. The problem was at the conversion layer. And she knew it, but her team didn't have CRO expertise in-house.

The ask: systematic conversion rate optimization. Turn the existing traffic into more demos without spending an additional dollar on ads.

---

## The CRO Audit: What We Found

Before running a single test, we spent 3 weeks auditing. The audit revealed the full picture:

**Traffic quality:** Fine. 62% of visitors matched ideal customer profile (mid-market B2B, financial services, teams of 10-200). The ads were working.

**Homepage:** No clear value proposition above the fold. "Empowering financial teams" — meaningless to a VP of Finance trying to solve a specific workflow problem.

**Landing pages:** Every traffic source — branded, competitor, product, SMB — landed on the same homepage. Zero segmentation.

**Demo request form:** 7 required fields. Name, email, phone, company, team size, annual revenue, "How did you hear about us?" — for a demo request.

**Trust signals:** Customer logos buried at the bottom. No security badges near the CTA. No specific outcomes mentioned.

**A/B testing:** Had Optimizely installed. Zero tests running. No hypothesis documentation. No statistical rigor.

The conversion rate wasn't a traffic problem. It was a trust and clarity problem.

---

## What We Built

**Week 1-2: Research Layer**

We ran Hotjar session recordings on 500 visitors. The patterns were clear:
- 73% of visitors bounced within 15 seconds without scrolling
- The ones who stayed: watched the product demo video (3.2 min average) but didn't convert
- Main objection visible in abandonment: "I need to justify this to my manager"

We ran 8 user interviews with recent demo abandoners. Three themes emerged:
1. "I didn't know what would happen after the demo" — form was a black box
2. "I wasn't sure this was for a company our size" — sizing uncertainty
3. "Too many fields felt like a sales trap" — form felt like a lead capture, not a service

**Week 3-4: Infrastructure**

- Dedicated landing pages for 4 traffic segments: brand (already aware), competitor (switching), product (actively evaluating), SMB (wrong fit — routed to different flow)
- Each page: specific headline for that intent, specific outcome statement, specific proof points
- ROI calculator embedded on product evaluation landing page — "Calculate your time savings"
- Exit-intent overlay with lead magnet offer (original research report)

**Week 5-10: Testing**

We ran 9 A/B tests. Most failed. That's how CRO works.

Tests that won:
1. **Homepage hero rewrite** — "Close your month-end books 3x faster" vs "Empowering financial teams." CVR: +34%.
2. **Demo form reduction** — 7 fields → 3 fields (name, email, company size). Form completion: 33% → 68%.
3. **Trust signal placement** — Moved customer logos, security badges, and testimonials directly adjacent to the CTA. CVR: +22%.
4. **Outcome-first CTAs** — Changed "Request Demo" to "See How It Works in 20 Minutes." CVR: +41%.
5. **Pre-form content** — Added a 2-minute explainer video directly above the form. Drop-off at form: -35%.

Tests that failed:
1. Chat widget above fold — increased bounce rate
2. Exit-intent discount offer — wrong signal for B2B enterprise product
3. Social proof carousel — static logos outperformed

## The Specific Moment It Changed

Week 8. Test #7. We'd just launched the outcome-first CTA copy change.

Monday morning, Rachel texted: "What did you change? We had 31 demos yesterday. We usually do 8."

The new CTA — "See How It Works in 20 Minutes" — reduced the perceived commitment of the demo request. Buyers who'd been hesitant to "request a demo" (sounds like a sales call) were comfortable "seeing how it works" (sounds like information).

The same traffic. Zero additional spend. 3.9x more demos.

## What We'd Do Differently

**Run the user interviews before the first landing page.** We built the first landing page before talking to customers. The copy was good — but not specific enough. The interviews surfaced language we didn't know to use.

**Start with form optimization, not page optimization.** The form reduction from 7 fields to 3 had the highest ROI of any single change we made. Should have been test #1, not test #7.

**Set up proper statistical significance tracking from Day 1.** Early tests had us making decisions on 200-visitor samples. We should have known better. Some decisions got reversed when significance cleared.
