# Case Study: Email Automation + Lifecycle Marketing
**Client:** CoreSync Solutions | **Project Duration:** 90 Days | **Email Revenue: 3.1x**

---

## 1. Executive Summary

CoreSync Solutions, a B2B company offering project management and team collaboration software, was treating email as a broadcast channel — sending one newsletter a month to their entire list with no segmentation, no automation, and no lifecycle thinking. Their 34,000-contact email list was generating just $18,400 per month in attributed revenue, and list growth had stalled. After a comprehensive email automation and lifecycle marketing overhaul in 90 days, we grew email-attributed revenue to $57,000 per month — a 3.1x increase — while reducing unsubscribes by 64% and improving open rates by 89%. The engagement proved that email is still the highest-ROI marketing channel for B2B companies that treat it as a system, not a newsletter.

---

## 3. The Problem

CoreSync's email program had several fundamental issues:

1. **Zero segmentation.** All 34,000 contacts received the same content. New leads, existing customers, churned users, and prospect evangelists all got the same monthly newsletter. The result was low engagement across the board and high unsubscribes when commercial content reached the wrong audience.

2. **No automation.** There was no welcome sequence for new subscribers, no follow-up for trial users who hadn't converted, no re-engagement campaign for dormant contacts, and no post-purchase nurture for customers. Every email was a one-off broadcast.

3. **List quality decay.** With no preference center or update mechanism, list hygiene was nonexistent. Nearly 40% of the list was inactive (no opens/clicks in 12+ months), dragging down deliverability and sender reputation.

4. **No lifecycle stages.** Marketing didn't have a model for where contacts were in the buyer's journey. There was no framework for moving someone from awareness to consideration to decision to advocacy.

5. **Undervalued channel.** Leadership saw email as a "nice-to-have" rather than a revenue driver. Without clear attribution and ROI data, the channel was chronically underinvested.

## 4. Approach & Strategy

### Phase 1: Audit & Data Analysis (Days 1-14)

We began with a comprehensive analysis of CoreSync's existing email program:

**List Analysis:**
- Contact count: 34,000 total (12,400 active, 8,200 warm inactive, 13,400 cold/inactive)
- Source breakdown: Trial signups (45%), Content downloads (30%), Webinar registrations (15%), Other (10%)
- Engagement tiers: Highly engaged (>5 opens/quarter), Moderately engaged (2-5), Low engagement (1-2), Inactive (0)

**Campaign Analysis:**
- Current send frequency: 1 broadcast/month
- Average open rate: 18% (industry avg for B2B SaaS is 21%)
- Average click rate: 2.1% (industry avg is 2.6%)
- Unsubscribe rate: 0.8% per send (too high — signal of irrelevant content)
- Revenue attribution: $18,400/month (unknown methodology)

**Competitive Analysis:**
- Analyzed 5 competitor email programs (frequency, content types, automation)
- Identified industry best practices for B2B SaaS lifecycle email

### Phase 2: Lifecycle Architecture Design (Days 15-30)

We designed a comprehensive lifecycle email strategy:

**Lifecycle Stages Defined:**
1. **Subscriber** — Just opted in, hasn't engaged deeply yet
2. **Lead** — Engaged with content, showed product interest
3. **MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead)** — Met threshold for sales follow-up
4. **Trial User** — Signed up for free trial
5. **Customer** — Paid subscriber
6. **Champion** — High-value customer, potential referral source
7. **Churned** — Cancelled subscription

**Automation Map:**
- Welcome Series (3 emails) → New subscriber onboarding
- Lead Nurture (5 emails) → Content engagement sequence
- Trial Activation (4 emails) → Trial-to-paid conversion
- Customer Onboarding (6 emails) → Time-based post-purchase
- Win-Back (3 emails) → Re-engagement for inactive
- Re-Engagement (4 emails) → Churned user recovery

### Phase 3: List Re-Engagement (Days 20-45)

Before launching new automations, we cleaned and re-activated the list:

**Re-Engagement Campaign:**
- 4-week re-engagement sequence to dormant contacts
- Segmented "still interested?" outreach
- Preference center invitation (update interests/frequency)
- Final sunset option for non-responders

**List Cleaning:**
- Removed hard bounces and spam traps
- Suppressed unsubscribes from all non-transactional sends
- Segmented remaining contacts into engagement tiers

### Phase 4: Automation Build-Out (Days 30-75)

We built all automated sequences in Klaviyo:

**Welcome Series (3 emails over 10 days):**
- Email 1: Welcome + brand introduction (Day 0)
- Email 2: Top resources / most popular content (Day 3)
- Email 3: Trial offer with specific use case (Day 10)

**Lead Nurture Sequence (5 emails over 30 days):**
- Segmented by content interest (project management tips, team productivity, integrations)
- Progressive profiling questions embedded in emails
- CTA to book a demo or start trial

**Trial Activation Sequence (4 emails over 14 days):**
- Day 0: Trial activation checklist
- Day 3: Feature spotlight (most-used features)
- Day 7: Success story / case study
- Day 12: Limited-time trial extension offer
- Day 14: Last chance / conversion urgency

**Customer Onboarding (6 emails over 60 days):**
- Week 1: Getting started checklist
- Week 2: Advanced feature tutorial
- Week 4: Integration walkthrough
- Week 6: ROI tracking tips
- Week 8: Community invitation
- Week 10: Expansion opportunity tease

**Win-Back / Re-Engagement (4 emails over 30 days):**
- Email 1: "We miss you" + new feature highlight
- Email 2: Case study from similar company
- Email 3: Exclusive discount for reactivation
- Email 4: Final unsubscribe prompt

## 6. Key Features & Tactics

**Segmentation by Engagement + Lifecycle Stage:**
Instead of one list, we built 14 segments based on engagement level and lifecycle stage. Each segment receives content relevant to their journey stage. New leads get educational content. Trial users get activation content. Customers get value-add and retention content.

**Behavioral Triggering:**
All automations were triggered by specific behaviors — sign-up source, email opens, link clicks, page views, trial sign-ups, purchase events. This meant emails were timely and relevant, not sent based on arbitrary schedules.

**Preference Center:**
We built a preference center allowing contacts to update their interests (project management, team productivity, integrations, etc.), preferred send frequency (weekly, bi-weekly, monthly), and communication type (educational, promotional, product updates). This reduced unsubscribes by giving people control.

**Revenue Attribution:**
We implemented multi-touch attribution in Klaviyo that gave credit to email touchpoints across the customer journey. This meant email was properly credited for its role in nurturing deals even when it wasn't the final conversion event.

**Trial Activation Focus:**
For B2B SaaS, the trial-to-paid conversion is critical. We built a dedicated 4-email sequence targeting trial users with specific activation milestones, social proof, and urgency to convert before trial expiry.

## 9. Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1: Dirty List Syndrome**
The list had 34,000 contacts but nearly 40% were inactive. Sending to them was dragging down deliverability and sender reputation.
*Solution:* We launched a 4-week re-engagement campaign with a preference center. Contacts who didn't re-engage were suppressed from non-transactional sends. We removed hard bounces and spam traps. This improved deliverability from ~88% to 96% and open rates across the board.

**Challenge 2: No Content for Automations**
Building 6 automation sequences required significant copy and content that didn't exist.
*Solution:* We repurposed existing content (blog posts, case studies, webinar recordings) into email format and supplemented with new copy written specifically for each sequence. We also created email-specific templates that could be reused for future sequences.

**Challenge 3: Sales Team Resistance**
The sales team was skeptical of "marketing leads" and didn't trust that email-generated leads were high quality.
*Solution:* We implemented lead scoring in HubSpot so only high-scoring leads from email went to sales. We also provided data showing that email-influenced deals had a 23% higher close rate. Once sales saw the quality difference, they started preferring email-sourced leads.

**Challenge 4: No Revenue Visibility**
Email was credited with only $18,400/month using last-click attribution, which dramatically undervalued its influence.
*Solution:* We implemented multi-touch attribution that gave email credit for its role across the journey. Email's attributed revenue jumped to $57,000/month — a 3.1x improvement — simply from better measurement.

## 10. Lessons Learned

1. **Email is a system, not a newsletter.** CoreSync's mistake was treating email as a broadcast channel. When we rebuilt it as a lifecycle system — automations triggered by behavior, segmented by stage, measured across the journey — results improved 3x.

2. **List quality beats list size.** We shrank the list from 34K to 28K but grew active engagement by 50%. Suppressing inactive contacts improved deliverability and open rates for everyone.

3. **Attribution changes everything.** The biggest single improvement came not from better emails but from better measurement. Multi-touch attribution revealed that email was driving 3x more revenue than last-click showed.

4. **Trial activation is the highest-leverage sequence.** For B2B SaaS, the trial-to-paid transition is the biggest bottleneck. A dedicated 4-email sequence targeting trial users with specific activation milestones drove a 64% improvement in conversion rate.

5. **Preference centers reduce churn.** Giving contacts control over what they receive and how often reduced unsubscribes by 64%. People who set their preferences feel ownership over the relationship.

## 16. Replication Formula

This methodology applies to any B2B company with an existing email list but no lifecycle system:

1. **Audit** — Analyze list quality, engagement, segmentation gaps
2. **Segment** — Define lifecycle stages and engagement tiers
3. **Clean** — Re-engage or suppress inactive contacts
4. **Automate** — Build behavioral-triggered sequences for each stage
5. **Attribute** — Implement multi-touch attribution to see true email value
6. **Optimize** — A/B test, iterate, refine based on data
7. **Expand** — Add channels (SMS, in-app) as system matures

## 17. Risk Management

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| High unsubscribe rate on re-engagement | Segment carefully, offer preference center before suppression |
| Deliverability issues from dirty list | Clean list first, warm up gradually, monitor reputation |
| Sales dismissing email leads | Implement lead scoring threshold, show close rate data |
| Content production bottleneck | Repurpose existing content, build reusable templates |
| Platform migration issues | Test thoroughly, have rollback plan, parallel send during transition |
