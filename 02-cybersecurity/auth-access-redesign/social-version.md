# LinkedIn Post — Authentication & Access Control Case Study

---

Just wrapped up a critical authentication and access control engagement with OmniBank — and the results demonstrate why modern identity management is non-negotiable for financial institutions.

**The Situation:**
OmniBank (regional bank, $18B assets, 1.2M customers) faced regulatory enforcement actions after an OCC examination found critical authentication deficiencies. No multi-factor authentication for 340,000 online banking users. Shared admin accounts for core banking. No privileged access management.

They had 90 days to demonstrate corrective action or face penalties that could reach $12M+.

**What We Found:**
Our IAM team conducted a comprehensive assessment — authentication systems, identity infrastructure, privileged access, access governance:

🔴 8 Critical deficiencies
- Zero MFA for online banking (340,000 users)
- Shared administrative accounts for core banking
- No privileged access management
- Inadequate access logging
- No formal access certification process
- Third-party vendor access unmonitored

🟠 14 High severity issues
🟡 12 Medium severity issues

**Total: 34 authentication and access control deficiencies.**

**The Outcome:**
✅ 100% MFA coverage for online banking and admin access
✅ Privileged access management: 100% of admin accounts
✅ Account takeover: 97% reduction (2,340→89 attempts/month)
✅ Fraud losses: 98% reduction ($180K→$4.2K/month)
✅ Regulatory examination passed with zero critical findings
✅ $12M in potential enforcement penalties avoided
✅ 79x ROI on the engagement

**The Real Value:**
Beyond passing the regulatory examination, OmniBank fundamentally transformed their security posture. They went from reactive, compliance-focused security to proactive, zero-trust identity management.

OmniBank's CISO put it well: "We went from facing potential enforcement actions to being cited as a model for authentication security in regional banking. The transformation was remarkable."

---

💡 **Key Takeaway:** In financial services, authentication and access control isn't optional — it's regulatory required. The cost of enforcement actions, fraud losses, and data breaches far exceeds the investment in modern identity management. Regulators aren't waiting. Neither should you.
