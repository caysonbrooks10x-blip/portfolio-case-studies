# Objection Handling — Compliance Readiness

## Objection: "We already have informal security practices. We don't need formal certification."

**Response:**
Informal security practices are valuable — they show you understand security basics. But enterprise customers and their auditors need documentation. They need to see that controls are consistently applied, not just that people are generally careful.

TechFlow had good security instincts. But when their enterprise prospects asked for audit reports, "we try to be careful" wasn't sufficient. Documentation transforms good practices into auditable controls.

---

## Objection: "We'll get SOC 2 first, then add ISO 27001 later."

**Response:**
You could — but it would cost more and take longer overall.

SOC 2 and ISO 27001 share significant overlap. Access controls, incident response, change management — many controls satisfy both frameworks simultaneously. By pursuing both together, you do one set of work that counts toward both certifications.

TechFlow achieved both for $95,000 total. Companies that do them sequentially often spend $120,000-$150,000 because they're essentially doing the work twice.

---

## Objection: "We're a startup. Enterprise certification can wait."

**Response:**
Enterprise certifications take time. If you're targeting enterprise customers, the time to start is now — not when a deal is on the table.

TechFlow learned this the hard way. They had $4.2M in enterprise deals ready to close when the certifications came up. If they had started their compliance program 6 months earlier, those deals wouldn't have been blocked.

Every quarter you delay is another quarter of lost enterprise revenue.

## Objection: "Our competitor got SOC 2 certified for $40,000. You're double that."

**Response:**
I've seen those quotes. Here's what often differentiates pricing:

Scope: Are they certifying all trust service criteria or just security? Type I or Type II? Full ISO 27001 or just Annex A?

Support: Do they help implement controls or just document what you have? Do they stay through the audit or disappear before certification?

Automation: Do they set up compliance automation or leave you with manual evidence collection forever?

TechFlow compared several firms. The $40K quotes were for gap assessments only — no policy development, no automation, no audit support. We'd have cost more upfront but delivered complete certification. And our 85% automation means TechFlow saves $40K+ annually on compliance maintenance.

**Response:**

## Objection: "We already use [compliance tool]. Can't we just do this ourselves?"

**Response:**
Compliance tools like Drata, Vanta, and Secureframe are excellent — and we use them too. But they automate evidence collection; they don't create policies, design controls, or prepare you for audits.

TechFlow had Drata before we started. They still needed:
- Someone to map their controls to SOC 2 and ISO 27001 requirements
- Someone to create 18 formal policies that satisfied the frameworks
- Someone to design the technical controls the policies required
- Someone to guide them through the audit process

The tool is necessary but not sufficient.

## Objection: "What if we fail the audit?"

**Response:**
With proper preparation, the risk of failure is minimal. We don't send clients to external audit until they're ready.

TechFlow had a full internal audit before their external audit. We remediated all findings. When A-Lign came in, they found zero material findings.

We'll tell you honestly where your gaps are. We'll help you fix them. And we'll make sure you're truly ready before the external auditor walks in.
