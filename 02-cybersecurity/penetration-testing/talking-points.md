# Sales Talking Points — Meridian Health Penetration Test

## Opening Hooks

- "Meridian Health thought their security was reasonable until our red team showed them what a real attacker could do—Domain Admin in 4 hours from a single phishing email."
- "A healthcare organization with 1.2 million patient records needed to prove to their board, insurers, and regulators that they were secure. We showed them exactly how secure—or not."
- "We don't run checklist audits. We act like adversaries. The difference in what we find is significant."

## Challenge Points

- **Healthcare is a target:** "Patient data is worth 10-50x more than financial data. Healthcare organizations are primary targets for ransomware and data theft."
- **Compliance isn't security:** "HIPAA compliance doesn't mean you're secure. It means you've documented your controls. We test whether those controls actually work."
- **Cyber insurance requirements:** "Insurers are raising the bar. A checkbox penetration test won't cut it anymore—you need documented, realistic testing."
- **Board accountability:** "Meridian's board mandated testing after a phishing incident. They got evidence of real risk and measurable improvement."

## Methodology Highlights

- "We simulate realistic adversaries—nation-state actors, organized crime, insider threats. Not just automated scanners."
- "Six-person red team with specialists in external, internal, web app, and social engineering."
- "Full attack chain testing—we don't just find vulnerabilities, we chain them together to demonstrate real business impact."
- "Healthcare-specific experience—we understand medical devices, clinical workflows, and HIPAA requirements."

## Results to Emphasize

- **"From a single phishing email to Domain Admin in 4 hours."**
- **"47 workstations compromised from one initial access."**
- **"1.2 million patient records accessible from the initial foothold."**
- **"37% click rate on phishing → 12% after training."**
- **"HIPAA audit passed, cyber insurance renewed, board satisfied."**

## Attack Scenarios to Highlight

- **Kerberoasting:** "A service account with Domain Admin had a weak password. We cracked it in 3 hours. That single finding gave us complete network control."
- **Patient enumeration:** "The patient portal accepted sequential MRNs with no rate limiting. Any attacker could have enumerated all patients. That's a breach waiting to happen."
- **Phishing chain:** "127 clicks, 89 credentials submitted, 34 malware executions from one email. One employee, one click, entire network potentially compromised."

## ROI Statements

- "The engagement cost $125,000. The breach we demonstrated could have cost $8M+ in direct costs, HIPAA penalties, and reputational damage."
- "The cyber insurance renewal alone justified the investment—coverage denial would have been catastrophic."
- "HIPAA penalties are $100 to $50,000 per violation, up to $1.5M per violation category. A breach affecting 1.2M records? The math is obvious."

## Healthcare-Specific Points

- "Healthcare is the #1 target for ransomware because patient data is so valuable."
- "Medical devices often can't be patched—but that doesn't mean they should be on the same network as patient data."
- "Clinical staff are often the most susceptible to phishing—stress, distraction, urgency to help patients."
- "The difference between healthcare security and other industries: when you fail, people's health records are exposed, not just their credit cards."

## Questions to Ask Prospects

- "When was your last real penetration test—not a vulnerability scan, but active exploitation testing?"
- "Have you tested whether your employees will click phishing links? What was the click rate?"
- "If an attacker got a single workstation via phishing, how far could they get? Have you tested that?"
- "How quickly could an attacker move from initial access to Domain Admin in your environment?"

## Close Options

- "I can have a proposal ready within 48 hours with scope customized to your specific environment."
- "Let's schedule a scoping call to understand your compliance requirements and testing boundaries."
- "We have capacity for 2 more healthcare engagements this quarter. I recommend scoping early."
